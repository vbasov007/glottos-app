# Test for Lesson 37: Congiuntivo passato and trapassato — compound subjunctives

**How do you say it in Italian?**
Translate the phrases into Italian. Use congiuntivo passato (*che io abbia / sia + part.*), congiuntivo trapassato (*che io avessi / fossi + part.*), and the type-3 se-clause.

---

1. I'm glad you came.
2. It's a shame you didn't call.
3. I don't think he's already arrived.
4. They've probably already left.
5. I hope you slept well.
6. I doubt she told the truth.
7. It's a good thing you finished on time.
8. If I had known, I would have told you. *(counterfactual)*
9. If you had listened to me, you wouldn't have been late.
10. If we had left earlier, we wouldn't have been late. *(counterfactual)*
11. If they had told me, I would have understood.
12. I should have studied more.
13. I could have helped you.
14. I shouldn't have said that.
15. I wish I'd known that earlier!
16. I didn't think he had already returned.
17. I doubted they had finished the project.
18. It was strange that you didn't tell me.
19. If I had studied more back then, I would have a good job now. *(mixed type)*
20. If he hadn't missed that train, he'd be in Rome today.
21. I regret not having called my grandmother.
22. If you had warned me, I would have come.
23. She may have already seen him.
24. I don't think they've had dinner yet.
25. I would have preferred you to come with me. *(condizionale passato + cong. trapassato)*
26. It's a good thing you did this.
27. I was glad you had arrived.
28. I didn't think they had already left.
29. If we had known, we wouldn't have gone.
30. If you had called me, I would have helped you.
