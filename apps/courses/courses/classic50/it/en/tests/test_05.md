# Test for Lesson 5: The verb *avere*, its idioms, *c'è / ci sono*

**How do you say this in Italian?**
Translate the phrases. Remember: "I'm hungry / I'm cold / I'm 20 / I have a headache" — all go through *avere*. *c'è / ci sono* = "there is / there are".

---

1. I have a dog.
2. Do you have a car?
3. He has a sister.
4. We have a house in Italy.
5. Do you (pl.) have time?
6. They have two children.
7. I'm hungry.
8. I'm thirsty.
9. I'm cold.
10. I'm hot.
11. I'm sleepy.
12. I'm afraid.
13. I'm in a hurry.
14. I'm right.
15. I have a headache.
16. I have a stomachache.
17. How old are you?
18. I'm twenty.
19. How old is he?
20. He's fifty.
21. There's a book on the table.
22. There are two chairs in the room.
23. Is there a bar here?
24. There's no bank here.
25. There are a lot of students here.
26. Maria is hungry, and Marco is thirsty. (use *e* / *ma*)
27. My sister has two dogs. (use *mia sorella*)
28. Dad has a toothache. (use *mio padre*)
29. We need water. (use *avere bisogno di acqua*)
30. They don't have time.
