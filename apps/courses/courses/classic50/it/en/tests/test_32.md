# Test for Lesson 32: Congiuntivo after will, necessity, and emotion

**How do you say it in Italian?**
Translate the phrases into Italian. Use the lesson's grammar and vocabulary. Remember the two-subject rule: one subject — infinitive (often with *di*), different subjects — *che* + congiuntivo.

---

1. I want you to come tomorrow.
2. I want to come tomorrow.
3. I hope you're doing well.
4. We prefer that you stay home.
5. The teacher asks us to open the book.
6. I advise you to study more. (natural Italian phrasing)
7. Let's hope it rains.
8. You need to see a doctor.
9. It's important that you (pl.) arrive on time.
10. Mom doesn't let me go out late.
11. The boss demands that we work.
12. I prefer to stay home.
13. I hope he's not late.
14. I'm asking you to tell the truth.
15. Smoking is forbidden here.
16. I wish you luck.
17. Better that you say nothing.
18. I want them to understand us.
19. I hope to arrive on time. (I'm the one arriving)
20. I hope you (pl.) arrive on time.
21. I propose that we rest.
22. Let him try.
23. You don't need to bring anything.
24. I want to eat.
25. I want you to eat.
26. It's urgent that he call me.
27. I'm glad you're here.
28. I'm glad to be here.
29. I'm sorry you can't come.
