# Test for Lesson 13: Direct object pronouns

**How do you say this in Italian?**
Translate the phrases into Italian. Use the grammar and vocabulary of the lesson. Pay special attention to pronoun placement before the verb and to gender/number agreement.

---

1. I see you.
2. Are you waiting for me?
3. We know them (the boys) well.
4. She's reading a book. → She's reading it.
5. I'm inviting Maria to dinner. → I'm inviting her to dinner.
6. They don't understand us.
7. I want to see him (Marco). *(use a pronoun, two forms)*
8. Buy these apples — they're tasty. *(at this stage: "are you buying them?")*
9. — Can you hear me? — Yes, I can hear you.
10. I can't find her (Anna) anywhere.
11. I thank you (pl.).
12. I write them (the letters) every day.
13. Do you see them (the girls)?
14. We eat it (the pizza) in the evening.
15. They're inviting us to the wedding.
16. I want to buy it (the book).
17. Don't wait for me, I'll be late.
18. I love you.
19. They drink it (wine) every day.
20. — Do you know this city? — Yes, I know it well.
21. We're waiting for you at the bar at five.
22. I hug and kiss them (my parents).
23. The teacher understands us.
24. I don't remember this song. → I don't remember it.
25. May I invite you (pl.)?
26. Maria is looking for her keys. → Maria is looking for them.
27. — Are you reading the paper? — No, I'm not reading it.
28. I want to see him (Marco). *(two forms with the modal)*
29. — How many times a week do you go to the gym? — I see him (the trainer) three times a week.
30. This book is interesting — I recommend it to all my friends.
