# Test for Lesson 35: The imperative

**How do you say it in Italian?**
Translate the phrases into Italian. Pay special attention to the *Lei* form (= congiuntivo lui!) and to pronoun attachment. And don't forget: the negative *tu* imperative is the **infinitive**!

---

1. Speak louder, please. (tu)
2. Speak louder, please. (Lei)
3. Tell me the truth! (tu)
4. Tell me the truth! (Lei)
5. Don't speak! (tu)
6. Don't speak! (Lei)
7. Give me it! (tu)
8. Give me it! (Lei)
9. Do it! (tu)
10. Do it! (Lei)
11. Don't do it! (tu)
12. Don't do it! (Lei)
13. Go straight. (tu)
14. Go straight. (Lei)
15. Sorry! (tu)
16. Excuse me! (Lei)
17. Give me the bill, please. (Lei)
18. Make me a coffee, please. (Lei)
19. Show me, please. (Lei)
20. Tell him it! (Lei)
21. Excuse me! (Lei — to get attention)
22. Come in, please. (Lei)
23. Wait a moment. (Lei)
24. Don't worry. (Lei)
25. Be careful. (tu)
26. Be careful. (Lei)
27. Tell me it! (double pronoun, tu)
28. Give it to him! (double pronoun, tu)
29. Show me! (tu)
30. Believe me! (Lei)
