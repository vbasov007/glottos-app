# Test for Lesson 45: Register — formal, neutral, colloquial Italian

**How do you say it in Italian?**
Translate the sentences into Italian in the **specified register** (*formale / neutro / colloquiale*). Where no register is given, use neutral.

---

1. I'm writing to inform you that the meeting has been postponed. (formale)
2. Listen, they moved the meeting. (colloquiale)
3. I would like to start the project next week. (formale)
4. Let's start the project next week. (colloquiale)
5. Unfortunately, I won't be able to attend the conference. (formale)
6. I can't make it. (colloquiale)
7. I apologize for the delay. (formale)
8. Sorry, I'm late! (colloquiale)
9. We would like to request additional information. (formale)
10. We need more info. (colloquiale)
11. Moreover, the budget is insufficient. (formale)
12. And besides, money's a bit short. (colloquiale)
13. The board of directors has rejected the proposal. (formale)
14. They turned me down. (colloquiale)
15. We are investigating this matter. (formale)
16. We'll deal with it. (colloquiale)
17. Please reply at your earliest convenience. (formale)
18. Reply when you can. (colloquiale)
19. I really need your help. (colloquiale)
20. I would be very grateful for your assistance. (formale)
21. Please confirm receipt. (formale)
22. Tell me you got it. (colloquiale)
23. It's expensive. (colloquiale — with an idiom)
24. The cost is excessive. (formale)
25. Sorry for the inconvenience. (formale)
26. Sorry to bother you. (colloquiale)
27. What's your name? (formale)
28. What's your name? (colloquiale)
29. Please wait. (formale, imperative on *Lei*)
30. Wait! (colloquiale)
