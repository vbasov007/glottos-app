# Test for lesson 22: Passato prossimo with essere. Agreement

**Come si dice in italiano?**
Translate the phrases. Distinguish *avere* and *essere* as auxiliaries. With *essere*, the participle agrees with the subject (-o/-a/-i/-e). With *avere* and a direct-object pronoun *lo/la/li/le*, the participle agrees with the pronoun.

---

1. Maria went to the shop.
2. We (mixed) arrived yesterday.
3. The girls left for Rome.
4. I (f.) was born in Moscow.
5. The kids have already gone to bed.
6. Anna left the house at eight.
7. Were you (m.) at the doctor's?
8. We stayed home all day.
9. Grandma died last year.
10. The train arrived on time.
11. I (f.) got up at seven.
12. They (m.) had a great time at the party.
13. We (all f.) got really tired.
14. Where is Marco? — He went out.
15. The book? I read it.
16. The letters? We wrote them.
17. Masha? I saw her yesterday.
18. The flowers? I bought them for you.
19. Mum liked the film. (piacere → essere)
20. We liked these books. (pl.)
21. What happened yesterday?
22. I (m.) slept well and got up at ten.
23. Friends came over for dinner.
24. The kids fell off the bike.
25. The lecture has already started.
26. I finished the book. (avere — there's a "what")
27. The book is over. (essere — no "what")
28. I (f.) felt unwell.
29. They fell in love with each other.
30. The train has just left.
