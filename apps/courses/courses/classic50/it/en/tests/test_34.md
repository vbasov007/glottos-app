# Test for Lesson 34: Congiuntivo after conjunctions

**How do you say it in Italian?**
Translate the phrases into Italian. Pay attention to the choice between **conjunction + congiuntivo** (two subjects) and **preposition + infinitive** (one subject). Remember the "expletive" *non* after *a meno che*.

---

1. I'm telling you so that you know.
2. I study to learn. (one subject)
3. I study so that he learns. (two subjects)
4. He's going out even though it's raining.
5. Although I'm tired, I keep working. (use *pur*)
6. Although he's tired, he keeps working. (use *sebbene*)
7. I call before leaving.
8. I call before you leave.
9. I'll come unless it rains.
10. I'll come provided you're there.
11. He left without saying a word.
12. He left without anyone noticing.
13. In case you have a problem, call me.
14. Despite the late hour, they're still working.
15. It's enough that you sign here.
16. I'll explain it to you so you understand well.
17. I'm not coming because I'm tired. *(perché — cause, indicativo!)*
18. I'm telling you so that you understand. *(perché — purpose, congiuntivo!)*
19. I agree provided you pay in advance.
20. However hard it is, we'll do it.
21. Speak slowly so that everyone understands.
22. Let me know before leaving.
23. Let me know before he leaves.
24. Do it before it's too late.
25. Although the weather is bad, we're going out. (use *nonostante*)
26. I'm calling you so that you don't worry.
27. It's enough that one person says "yes".
28. I'll come unless work holds me up.
29. Despite the rain, we went for a walk.
30. I agree, provided we all agree.
