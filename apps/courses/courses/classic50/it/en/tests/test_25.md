# Test for lesson 25: Futuro semplice

**Come si dice in italiano?**
Translate the phrases. Use futuro semplice (-ò/-ai/-à/-emo/-ete/-anno) for prediction, distant plans, promises, and conjecture about the present (*Saranno le otto*). Remember the nine irregular stems: *sar-, avr-, far-, andr-, verr-, potr-, dovr-, sapr-, vorr-*.

---

1. Tomorrow I'll work from home.
2. Next week we'll go to Rome.
3. In a year I'll finish university.
4. When I get to Moscow, I'll call you.
5. They'll arrive at eight o'clock.
6. What will you do on Saturday?
7. When I'm thirty, I'll buy a house.
8. Soon you'll know the truth.
9. In the future we'll live longer.
10. It must be eight o'clock by now. (conjecture)
11. Marco isn't answering. He must be busy. (conjecture)
12. How old is he? About forty, probably. (conjecture)
13. That must be the postman. (conjecture)
14. Tomorrow it'll rain.
15. I promise I'll help you.
16. I won't tell anyone.
17. In ten years I'll live in Italy.
18. As soon as I see Marco, I'll tell him everything.
19. We'll get married next year.
20. Mum will cook your favourite dish.
21. Don't worry: everything will be fine.
22. Prices will go up.
23. We'll have children.
24. I won't smoke anymore.
25. After university I'll look for a job abroad.
26. They'll have to wait.
27. He may be rich, but he isn't happy. (concession)
28. What will the weather be like tomorrow?
29. Will you tell me when you see him?
30. The train will be late.
