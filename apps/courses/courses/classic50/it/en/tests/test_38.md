# Test for Lesson 38: Sequence of tenses in the subjunctive

**How do you say it in Italian?**
Translate the phrases into Italian. Use the 2×2 matrix — present or past axis, action simultaneous with or earlier than the main verb.

---

1. I want you to come tomorrow. *(present axis, simult.)*
2. I wanted you to come yesterday. *(past axis, simult.)*
3. I'm glad you came. *(present axis, earlier)*
4. I was glad you had come. *(past axis, earlier)*
5. I doubt he's telling the truth.
6. I doubted he was telling the truth.
7. You need to study more.
8. It was necessary that you study more.
9. She may have already arrived.
10. It was possible that she had already arrived.
11. I asked him to help me.
12. I asked him to have already helped me. *(earlier)*
13. I don't think he'll come.
14. I didn't think he'd come.
15. It's a shame you didn't come.
16. It was a shame you hadn't come.
17. I work so that my children live better.
18. I worked so that my children would live better.
19. I'll do this before it's too late.
20. I did this before it was too late.
21. I would have preferred you to come with me. *(condizionale → past axis)*
22. He talks as if he knew everything. *(come se)*
23. She behaved as if nothing had happened.
24. If I had time, I'd go. *(type 2)*
25. If I'd had time yesterday, I'd have gone. *(type 3)*
26. Tell me when you arrive. *(after quando — indicative!)*
27. He told me to come whenever I could.
28. I'm surprised you didn't call.
29. I was surprised you hadn't called.
30. Nobody believed it was true.

<summary>Key</summary>

1. *Voglio che tu venga domani.*
2. *Volevo che tu venissi ieri.*
3. *Sono contento che tu sia venuto.*
4. *Ero contento che tu fossi venuto.* (or: *…che tu venissi*, if simultaneous)
5. *Dubito che dica la verità.*
6. *Dubitavo che dicesse la verità.*
7. *È necessario che tu studi di più.*
8. *Era necessario che tu studiassi di più.*
9. *È possibile che sia già arrivata.*
10. *Era possibile che fosse già arrivata.*
11. *Gli ho chiesto che mi aiutasse.* (or: *…di aiutarmi.*)
12. *Gli ho chiesto che mi avesse già aiutato.* (rare; usually rephrased)
13. *Non credo che venga.*
14. *Non credevo che venisse.*
15. *Mi dispiace che tu non sia venuto.* (or: *Peccato che…*)
16. *Mi dispiaceva che tu non fossi venuto.*
17. *Lavoro perché i miei figli vivano meglio.*
18. *Lavoravo perché i miei figli vivessero meglio.*
19. *Lo farò prima che sia tardi.*
20. *L'ho fatto prima che fosse tardi.*
21. *Avrei preferito che tu fossi venuto con me.*
22. *Parla come se sapesse tutto.*
23. *Si comportava come se non fosse successo niente.*
24. *Se avessi tempo, andrei.*
25. *Se avessi avuto tempo ieri, sarei andato.*
26. *Dimmi quando arrivi.* (or: *quando arriverai* — indicative!)
27. *Mi disse di venire quando potessi.* (or: *che venissi quando potessi*)
28. *Mi sorprende che non abbiate chiamato.*
29. *Mi sorprese che non aveste chiamato.*
30. *Nessuno credeva che fosse vero.*
