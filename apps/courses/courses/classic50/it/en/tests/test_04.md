# Test for Lesson 4: Subject pronouns and the verb *essere*

**How do you say this in Italian?**
Translate the phrases. In most cases the subject pronoun is **dropped** — the ending of *essere* already shows the person. Remember: profession takes **no article**; *è* is written with a grave accent.

---

1. I'm Italian.
2. I'm Italian (f.).
3. Are you a student?
4. Are you a student (f.)?
5. He's from Rome.
6. She's from London.
7. We're in Italy.
8. Are you tired? (addressing a group)
9. They're French.
10. They're French and Spanish.
11. Anna is a doctor.
12. Marco is a teacher.
13. We're engineers.
14. I'm not American.
15. I'm not American (f.).
16. Marco isn't here.
17. Maria is happy.
18. We're happy. (m. pl.)
19. The girls are happy. (use *le bambine*)
20. I'm ready.
21. They're busy.
22. Is that Anna?
23. Yes, it's me.
24. I'm also a student. (use *anch'io*)
25. Where are you from?
26. Where are you from? (polite, one person)
27. Who is it? (use *Chi è?*)
28. That's my teacher. (use *il mio professore*)
29. The house is big. (use *grande*)
30. The houses are big.
