# Test for Lesson 31: Congiuntivo presente — the forms

**How do you say it in Italian?**
Translate the phrases into Italian. Focus on the correct congiuntivo form after the frames *voglio che, spero che, speriamo che*.

---

1. I want you to speak Italian.
2. I want you (pl.) to eat well.
3. I hope he arrives on time.
4. I hope she sleeps well.
5. I want us to go together.
6. I hope I can help you.
7. I hope they tell the truth.
8. I want you to know the truth.
9. I hope it's true.
10. I want him to do his homework.
11. I hope you (pl.) are here tomorrow.
12. I hope the kids fall asleep quickly.
13. I want her to be happy.
14. I hope you get the job.
15. I hope you have luck.
16. I want you to ask for help.
17. I hope he knows the answer.
18. I hope they come soon.
19. I want us to think the same.
20. I hope she's at home.
21. I hope we can go to Rome.
22. I want you (pl.) to put this here.
23. I hope he says "yes".
24. I hope I have more time.
25. I want you to be careful.
26. I hope they work well.
27. I hope it doesn't rain.
28. I want us to leave early.
29. I hope you understand me.
30. I hope we have a good day.
