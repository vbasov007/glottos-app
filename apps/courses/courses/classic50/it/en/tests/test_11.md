# Test for Lesson 11: Modal verbs — potere, volere, dovere

**How do you say this in Italian?**
Translate the phrases into Italian. Use the grammar and vocabulary of the lesson.

---

1. I want to eat pizza.
2. Can you help me?
3. He has to go home.
4. We don't want to work today.
5. Can you (pl.) wait a moment?
6. They have to pay in cash.
7. I can't come to the party.
8. What do you want to do tonight?
9. The kids want to play in the park.
10. — May I come in? — Yes, please.
11. I have to study for the exam.
12. She wants to go to Rome.
13. Can we pay by card?
14. You must tell the truth.
15. They don't want to go out.
16. — What does the lady want? — She wants a glass of water.
17. I can't talk right now.
18. You need to take the bus.
19. They have to arrive at eight.
20. May I open the window?
21. We want to see the new film.
22. She has to finish the work today.
23. Do you want a coffee?
24. We can't wait any longer.
25. They want to buy a new car.
26. — May one smoke here? — No, one may not.
27. I don't want to eat anything.
28. First I have to call my mom, then I can go with you.
29. If you want to talk, you can come by my place after work.
30. — Why do you (pl.) have to leave so early? — We have to be at the airport by nine.
