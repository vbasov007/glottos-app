# Lesson 34: Congiuntivo after conjunctions
**Vocabulary: conjunctions of purpose, concession, time, condition; planning and the unexpected**

---

## How to work with this lesson

1. **Read** the list of conjunctions — about a dozen of them, and they **always** pull congiuntivo (5 minutes).
2. **Drill the pairs** "one subject → preposition + infinitive" / "two subjects → conjunction + congiuntivo". This is the core.
3. **Run the dialogs** — conjunctions only come alive in real planning and reasoning contexts.

> Lesson 32 gave you **will, necessity, emotion**. Lesson 33 — **doubt, opinion, search**. Today — the **fourth and broadest** trigger: **conjunctions** that by themselves require congiuntivo.

---

## Part 1: The big idea — some conjunctions ALWAYS pull congiuntivo

In the previous lessons, congiuntivo appeared because the main clause was **doing something with a fact** (expressing will, emotion, doubt). Today — a different logic.

> **Rule:** some conjunctions — *prima che, affinché, benché, sebbene, a meno che, purché, senza che, nonostante* — **by their nature** don't assert a fact. They introduce a hypothesis, a goal, a concession, a condition. The verb after them automatically goes into **congiuntivo**.

This mode **doesn't depend** on the main clause. Whether the main clause is "I know that…" or "I want that…" — after these conjunctions, you still use congiuntivo.

> **For English speakers:** in English most of these conjunctions just take a plain indicative ("although it's raining", "before he leaves", "so that you know"). Italian has a closed list of conjunctions that mechanically trigger the subjunctive — you must memorize them.

---

## Part 2: Conjunctions of purpose — "so that, in order that"

| Italian | English | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **affinché** | so that (formal) | <say>Te lo dico affinché tu lo sappia.</say> — I'm telling you so that you know. |
| **perché** (in the sense of "so that") | so that | <say>Te lo dico perché tu lo sappia.</say> — same. |
| **in modo che** | so that, in such a way that | <say>Parla piano in modo che tutti capiscano.</say> — Speak slowly so everyone understands. |

> **Heads up:** *perché* is a homonym. *Perché* meaning "because" → indicativo. *Perché* meaning "so that" → congiuntivo. Distinguish by sense:
> <say>Non vengo perché sono stanco.</say> — I'm not coming because I'm tired. (cause → ind.)
> <say>Te lo dico perché tu lo sappia.</say> — I'm telling you so that you know. (purpose → cong.)

> **One subject — infinitive:** If the same person does both actions, purpose is expressed with *per* + infinitive, without the conjunction *affinché/perché*:
> <say>Studio per imparare.</say> — I study (in order) to learn. (I → I)
> <say>Studio perché lui impari.</say> — I study so he learns. (I → he)

## Part 3: Concession conjunctions — "although, despite"

| Italian | English | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **benché** | although | <say>Esce benché piova.</say> — He's going out although it's raining. |
| **sebbene** | although | <say>Sebbene sia stanco, lavora.</say> — Although he's tired, he's working. |
| **nonostante** | despite the fact that | <say>Nonostante sia tardi, continua.</say> — Although it's late, he keeps going. |
| **malgrado** | despite | <say>Malgrado piova, usciamo.</say> — Despite the rain, we're going out. |
| **per quanto** | however much | <say>Per quanto sia difficile, lo faremo.</say> — However hard it is, we'll do it. |

> **Important:** in English "although" pairs with the plain indicative ("although it's raining"). In Italian after *benché, sebbene, nonostante, malgrado* — **only congiuntivo**. Memorize this as a fixed pattern.

> **One subject — *pur* + gerund:** If the concession applies to the same subject as the main verb, Italians prefer *pur* + gerund, no conjunction:
> <say>Pur essendo stanco, lavora.</say> — Although he's tired, he's working. (= Sebbene sia stanco, lavora.)
> <say>Pur sapendo la verità, non ha detto niente.</say> — Although he knew the truth, he said nothing.

---

## Part 4: Time conjunctions — "before"

| Italian | English | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **prima che** | before (someone else does) | <say>Telefoniamo prima che parta.</say> — Let's call before he leaves. |

> **"Before doing something YOURSELF" — *prima di* + infinitive:**
> <say>Telefono prima di partire.</say> — I'll call before leaving. (I → I)
> <say>Telefono prima che (lui) parta.</say> — I'll call before he leaves. (I → he)

> **Watch out for the other time conjunctions.** Not all of them require congiuntivo. *Quando, mentre, dopo che* in modern Italian usually take **indicativo** or future:
> <say>Quando arriva, lo chiamo.</say> — When he arrives, I'll call him. (ind.)
> <say>Dopo che è arrivato, abbiamo mangiato.</say> — After he arrived, we ate. (ind.)
> Only *prima che* among the time conjunctions always requires congiuntivo. *Finché (non)* can take either, more often ind.

---

## Part 5: Conditional conjunctions — "provided that, unless"

| Italian | English | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **purché** | provided that | <say>Vengo, purché tu sia presente.</say> — I'll come, provided you're there. |
| **a patto che** | on condition that | <say>Te lo presto a patto che me lo restituisca.</say> — I'll lend it on condition you give it back. |
| **a condizione che** | on condition that | <say>Accettiamo a condizione che paghino in anticipo.</say> — We agree on condition they pay in advance. |
| **a meno che (non)** | unless | <say>Vengo, a meno che non piova.</say> — I'll come, unless it rains. |
| **nel caso che** | in case | <say>Chiamami nel caso che tu abbia bisogno.</say> — Call me in case you need to. |
| **qualora** | in case (formal) | <say>Qualora ci fosse un problema, avvisami.</say> — Should there be a problem, let me know. *(imperfetto cong. — L36)* |

> **Trap with *a meno che*:** Italian inserts a "pleonastic" *non* in this construction, which does **not** negate the verb:
> <say>Vengo, a meno che non piova.</say> — I'll come, unless it rains. (NOT "unless it doesn't rain"!)
> This is so-called "expletive non" — part of the conjunction's shape, with no negative meaning. Memorize it as a unit.

---

## Part 6: Conjunctions of manner and exception

| Italian | English | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **senza che** | without (someone else's doing) | <say>È uscito senza che lo vedessi.</say> — He left without me seeing him. *(imperfetto cong. — L36)* |
| **a costo che** | even at the cost that | rare |
| **basta che** | it's enough that | <say>Basta che tu firmi qui.</say> — It's enough for you to sign here. |

> **One subject — *senza* + infinitive:**
> <say>È uscito senza salutare.</say> — He left without saying goodbye. (he → he)
> <say>È uscito senza che lo vedessi.</say> — He left without me seeing him. (he → I)

---

## Part 7: The one-subject / two-subject rule — summary table

Most conjunctions that require congiuntivo have a "paired" preposition + infinitive for the one-subject case.

| Conjunction (two subjects → cong.) | Preposition (one subject → infinitive) |
|------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| <say>affinché / perché</say> (so that) | <say>per</say> |
| <say>prima che</say> (before someone else) | <say>prima di</say> |
| <say>senza che</say> (without sb else) | <say>senza</say> |
| <say>a meno che (non)</say> (unless) | <say>a meno di</say> |
| <say>nel caso che</say> (in case) | <say>nel caso di</say> |
| <say>benché / sebbene / nonostante</say> (although) | <say>pur</say> + gerund |
| <say>purché / a patto che</say> (provided that) | — (usually no infinitive alternative) |

### Pairs to drill

| One subject (infinitive) | Two subjects (conjunction + cong.) |
|--------------------------|------------------------------------|
| <say>Studio per imparare.</say> | <say>Studio perché lui impari.</say> |
| <say>Chiamo prima di partire.</say> | <say>Chiamo prima che lui parta.</say> |
| <say>Esco senza salutare.</say> | <say>Esco senza che lui se ne accorga.</say> |
| <say>Vengo a meno di essere malato.</say> | <say>Vengo a meno che non sia malato lui.</say> |
| <say>Pur essendo stanco, lavoro.</say> | <say>Sebbene sia stanco, continuo.</say> |

## Part 8: Scales — train the mouth

### Scale 1: *prima che* + changing person

| Before… | Italian |
|---------|---------|
| …you leave | <say>prima che tu parta</say> |
| …he leaves | <say>prima che lui parta</say> |
| …we leave | <say>prima che noi partiamo</say> |
| …you (pl.) leave | <say>prima che voi partiate</say> |
| …they leave | <say>prima che loro partano</say> |

### Scale 2: *benché* + being tired, different persons

| Although… | Italian |
|-----------|---------|
| …I'm tired | <say>benché io sia stanco</say> |
| …you're tired | <say>benché tu sia stanco</say> |
| …he's tired | <say>benché lui sia stanco</say> |
| …we're tired | <say>benché noi siamo stanchi</say> |

### Scale 3: *purché* + various promises

| Provided that… | Italian |
|----------------|---------|
| …you do it | <say>purché tu lo faccia</say> |
| …you say it | <say>purché tu lo dica</say> |
| …you come | <say>purché tu venga</say> |
| …you have time | <say>purché tu abbia tempo</say> |

### Scale 4: the "one subject / two subjects" switch

| English | Italian |
|---------|---------|
| I'm going out to eat. (I → I) | <say>Esco per mangiare.</say> |
| I'm going out so you can eat. (I → you) | <say>Esco perché tu mangi.</say> |
| I'll say it before leaving. (I → I) | <say>Dico prima di andarmene.</say> |
| I'll say it before you leave. (I → you) | <say>Dico prima che tu te ne vada.</say> |
| I'll leave without saying goodbye. (I → I) | <say>Esco senza salutare.</say> |
| I'll leave without him noticing. (I → he) | <say>Esco senza che se ne accorga.</say> |
| Although I'm tired, I work. (I → I) | <say>Pur essendo stanco, lavoro.</say> |
| Although he's tired, he works. (he tired, he works — but conjunction requires cong.) | <say>Sebbene sia stanco, lavora.</say> |

> **How to drill it:** take a pair, read the first line (infinitive), then immediately the second (congiuntivo). One-two-one-two. Until the switch becomes a reflex.

### Exercise 5. Tell *perché* apart — cause or purpose?

Read each sentence and say: does *perché* mean "because" (→ ind.) or "so that" (→ cong.)?

1. <say>Non vengo perché sono stanco.</say>
2. <say>Te lo dico perché tu lo sappia.</say>
3. <say>Studio perché voglio imparare.</say>
4. <say>Parlo piano perché tu capisca.</say>
5. <say>Mi alleno perché voglio essere in forma.</say>
6. <say>Te lo ripeto perché tu non lo dimentichi.</say>

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. "because" → ind. (*sono*) — cause.
2. "so that" → cong. (*sappia*) — purpose.
3. "because" → ind. (*voglio*) — cause.
4. "so that" → cong. (*capisca*) — purpose.
5. "because" → ind. (*voglio*) — cause.
6. "so that" → cong. (*dimentichi*) — purpose.

> **Heuristic:** *perché* + ind. = cause ("because"). *perché* + cong. = purpose ("so that").

## Lesson cheat sheet

```
THE FOURTH CONGIUNTIVO TRIGGER — CONJUNCTIONS

These conjunctions inherently pull congiuntivo
(regardless of the main clause):

PURPOSE:
  affinché              perché (in the sense of "so that")
  in modo che
  → one subject: per + INFINITIVE

CONCESSION:
  benché                sebbene
  nonostante            malgrado
  per quanto
  → one subject: pur + GERUND

TIME (only one — prima che):
  prima che
  → one subject: prima di + INFINITIVE
  (quando, dopo che, mentre — usually INDICATIVO!)

CONDITION:
  purché                a patto che
  a condizione che      nel caso che
  qualora (formal)

EXCEPTION:
  a meno che (non)      — watch the "expletive non"!
  → one subject: a meno di + INFINITIVE

MANNER:
  senza che
  → one subject: senza + INFINITIVE

SUFFICIENCY:
  basta che

ONE-SUBJECT / TWO-SUBJECT RULE:
  One subject     → PREPOSITION + INFINITIVE (no che, no cong.)
  Two subjects    → CONJUNCTION + CONGIUNTIVO

  Studio per imparare.       Studio perché lui impari.
  Chiamo prima di partire.   Chiamo prima che tu parta.
  Esco senza salutare.       Esco senza che lui se ne accorga.
  Pur essendo stanco, lavoro. Sebbene lui sia stanco, lavora.

PERCHÉ — TWO-FACED:
  perché + INDICATIVO = "because" (cause)
  perché + CONGIUNTIVO = "so that" (purpose)

  Non vengo perché sono stanco.   (cause → ind.)
  Te lo dico perché tu lo sappia. (purpose → cong.)

REFERENCE EXAMPLES:
  Te lo dico affinché tu sappia la verità.
  Esce benché piova.
  Telefono prima che parta.
  Vengo, purché tu sia presente.
  Vengo, a meno che non piova.
  È uscito senza che me ne accorgessi.
  Sebbene sia stanco, continua a lavorare.
  Basta che tu firmi qui.

SUMMARY — FOUR CONGIUNTIVO TRIGGERS (L32–L34):
  L32: will / necessity / emotion (voglio che, è necessario che, sono contento che)
  L33: doubt / opinion / probability (dubito che, credo che, può darsi che) +
       relative clauses with indefinite antecedent (cerco un libro che sia interessante)
  L34: conjunctions (affinché, prima che, benché, purché, a meno che non, senza che…)
```
