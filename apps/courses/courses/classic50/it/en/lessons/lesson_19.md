# Lesson 19: Present continuous — *stare + gerundio*
**Vocabulary: action verbs for live reporting**

---

## How to work with this lesson

1. **Read** the rule — feel the difference between "usually" and "right this second" (5 minutes)
2. **Translate** the exercises in writing — check against the key
3. **Say out loud** through the persons: <say>sto parlando, stai parlando, sta parlando, stiamo parlando, state parlando, stanno parlando</say>
4. **Speed up** — drill the matrix until *sto facendo, sto leggendo* fly out on autopilot

> English **loves** its present continuous. "I'm working", "she's reading", "they're playing" — you reach for the *-ing* form constantly, even for things that aren't happening right this second ("I'm reading a book about Rome" — meaning over the past few days, generally). Italian doesn't do that. Italian has *stare + gerundio*, but it uses it **much more sparingly** than English does. The simple present (<say>parlo, mangio, lavoro</say>) covers a wider range — including a lot of what English would push into the continuous.

---

## Part 1: When you need the continuous in Italian

In English, "I'm working" can mean both "right at this moment" and "in this general period of my life". Italian splits these. The simple present (<say>lavoro</say>) covers the general/habitual; *stare + gerundio* is reserved for **literally happening right now, in the moment of speaking**.

> **Typical "continuous" cues:**
> - <say>ora, adesso</say> — "now"
> - <say>in questo momento</say> — "at this moment"
> - <say>proprio ora</say> — "right now"
> - the live-report picture: what you can **see happening** this very second

Compare:

| Simple | Continuous |
|--------|------------|
| <say>Lavoro in banca.</say> "I work at a bank." (profession) | <say>Sto lavorando.</say> "I'm (currently) working." (in progress) |
| <say>Parlo italiano.</say> "I speak Italian." (ability) | <say>Sto parlando con Marco.</say> "(Right now) I'm talking with Marco." |
| <say>Cosa fai?</say> "What do you do? / What are you up to?" (broad) | <say>Cosa stai facendo?</say> "What are you doing (right now)?" |

> **English-speaker trap:** "I'm reading every day" in English uses present continuous, but Italian wouldn't put that into *sto leggendo*. "Every day" = habit = simple present: <say>Leggo ogni giorno.</say> The continuous is for the **moment of speaking**, not for habits.

---

## Part 2: The main hack — two pieces, the rest assembles itself

The construction is built from **two** parts:

> **Formula:** *stare* (conjugated by person) + gerund
>
> | Person | *stare* | + gerund |
> |--------|---------|----------|
> | io | <say>sto</say> | <say>parlando</say> |
> | tu | <say>stai</say> | <say>parlando</say> |
> | lui/lei | <say>sta</say> | <say>parlando</say> |
> | noi | <say>stiamo</say> | <say>parlando</say> |
> | voi | <say>state</say> | <say>parlando</say> |
> | loro | <say>stanno</say> | <say>parlando</say> |

*stare* is the verb you already met in L8. *stare* conjugates; the gerund **never** changes for person, number or gender. It's **one invariable form**, attached to any form of *stare*.

> **English analogue:** the verb-be + -ing pattern. *I am reading* = *sto leggendo*. *She is reading* = *sta leggendo*. The auxiliary changes; the *-ing* form / *-ndo* form stays the same. Comfortingly parallel — just don't overuse it the way you do in English.

---

## Part 3: How to form the gerund

This is the simplest rule in Italian conjugation. **No exceptions by conjugation group** — just two endings, picked by the infinitive type.

> **Regular rules:**
>
> | Infinitive | Drop | Add | Example |
> |------------|------|-----|---------|
> | *-are* | *-are* | *-ando* | <say>parlare → parlando</say> |
> | *-ere* | *-ere* | *-endo* | <say>leggere → leggendo</say> |
> | *-ire* | *-ire* | *-endo* | <say>dormire → dormendo</say> |

Notice: **both *-ere* and *-ire* verbs** take the **same** ending *-endo*. Verbs in *-isc-* (*finire, capire, preferire*) — no *-isc-* insert; the gerund is just *finendo, capendo, preferendo*.

## Part 4: A few exceptions — memorize them

These are few and they cluster. All come from verbs whose historical Latin stem was "longer" than the modern infinitive shows.

> **Key exceptions:**
>
> | Infinitive | Gerund | Note |
> |------------|--------|------|
> | <say>fare</say> | <say>facendo</say> | from old stem *fac-* |
> | <say>dire</say> | <say>dicendo</say> | from old stem *dic-* |
> | <say>bere</say> | <say>bevendo</say> | from old stem *bev-* |
> | <say>porre</say> | <say>ponendo</say> | rare, formal |
> | <say>tradurre</say> | <say>traducendo</say> | and other verbs ending in *-urre* |

Notice the pattern: the stem is "rebuilt" to its longer Latin shape — the same trick you'll see later in <say>facevo, dicevo, bevevo</say> (the imperfect, L23).

Read aloud:

```
io  sto  facendo     i compiti
io  sto  dicendo     la verità
io  sto  bevendo     un caffè
io  sto  parlando    al telefono
io  sto  mangiando   la pasta
io  sto  leggendo    il giornale
io  sto  dormendo    ancora
io  sto  finendo     il lavoro
```

---

## Part 5: Contrast with the simple present — when to pick what

Italian does **not** force you to switch to the continuous every time an action is in progress. Often the simple present is fine:

```
Cosa fai?           Cosa stai facendo?       (both normal)
Mangio.             Sto mangiando.           (both normal)
Parlo con Marco.    Sto parlando con Marco.  (both normal)
```

So **when** is *stare + gerundio* really needed?

> **Use *stare + gerundio* when:**
> 1. You want to **stress**: "right this second, not in general"
> 2. The action is actively in progress **right now**, and the contrast with the usual matters: <say>Di solito lavoro in ufficio, ma adesso sto lavorando da casa.</say>
> 3. You're describing a snapshot, a live report, what you can **see happening**
>
> **Use the simple present when:**
> 1. Habitual / regular action: <say>Ogni mattina prendo il caffè.</say>
> 2. General state: <say>Lavoro in banca.</say>
> 3. Near future: <say>Domani vado a Roma.</say> (the continuous **doesn't work** for the future — unlike English "I'm going tomorrow")
> 4. Stative verbs: <say>sapere, conoscere, amare, odiare, volere, capire</say> — these stay simple: <say>Lo so.</say> (not *sto sapendo*).

> **Key English-speaker reminder:** English "I'm reading a book by Calvino" (over this week, in general) → Italian uses simple present: <say>Leggo un libro di Calvino.</say> The Italian continuous is **narrower** than the English one. Use it for the live snapshot, not for the open-ended period.

---

## Part 6: Where the pronouns go

Same two rules as for the infinitive (L16, L17):

> **Rule 1.** Before *stare* — pronoun goes **in front**:
> - <say>Lo sto leggendo.</say> — I'm (currently) reading it.
> - <say>Mi sto vestendo.</say> — I'm getting dressed.
> - <say>Glielo sto dicendo.</say> — I'm telling him this (right now).
>
> **Rule 2.** Attached to the **gerund** (more formal):
> - <say>Sto leggendolo.</say> — Same meaning.
> - <say>Sto vestendomi.</say>
> - <say>Sto dicendoglielo.</say>

Both are correct. In speech, "pronoun in front" is the usual choice. In writing, both occur.

## Part 8: Language scales

### Scale 1 — *stare + parlando* through all persons

```
io   sto      parlando
tu   stai     parlando
lui  sta      parlando
lei  sta      parlando
noi  stiamo   parlando
voi  state    parlando
loro stanno   parlando
```

> Read aloud. Feel: the gerund **doesn't move**. Only *stare* conjugates.

### Scale 2 — the three verb groups

```
sto  parlando      (-are)
sto  mangiando     (-are)
sto  lavorando     (-are)

sto  leggendo      (-ere)
sto  scrivendo     (-ere)
sto  prendendo     (-ere)

sto  dormendo      (-ire)
sto  finendo       (-ire)
sto  partendo      (-ire)
```

### Scale 3 — exceptions

```
sto  facendo       (fare)
sto  dicendo       (dire)
sto  bevendo       (bere)
sto  traducendo    (tradurre)
```

### Scale 4 — simple vs continuous

```
parlo italiano.            sto parlando con Marco.
lavoro in banca.           sto lavorando da casa oggi.
mangio la pasta.           sto mangiando proprio ora.
leggo molti libri.         sto leggendo un romanzo in questo momento.
guardo la TV la sera.      sto guardando una partita.
```

> Run it. Feel: the left column is "in general, usually". The right is "right at this very second".

### Scale 5 — with pronouns

```
lo  sto  leggendo            (I'm reading it)
la  sto  scrivendo           (I'm writing it)
li  sto  aspettando          (I'm waiting for them)
mi  sto  vestendo            (I'm getting dressed)
ti  sto  ascoltando          (I'm listening to you)
glielo  sto  dicendo         (I'm telling him this)
```

### Exercise 3. Simple or continuous?

Pick the right form:

1. Di solito (lavoro / sto lavorando) in ufficio, ma oggi (lavoro / sto lavorando) da casa.
2. Marco (parla / sta parlando) tre lingue.
3. Zitti! Il bambino (dorme / sta dormendo).
4. Cosa (fai / stai facendo) la sera di solito?
5. In questo momento Maria (cucina / sta cucinando) la cena.
6. (Studio / Sto studiando) italiano da due anni.
7. Aspetta un attimo, (mi vesto / mi sto vestendo).
8. Ogni domenica (vado / sto andando) al parco.

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *Di solito **lavoro** in ufficio, ma oggi **sto lavorando** da casa.*
2. *Marco **parla** tre lingue.* (ability, general fact)
3. *Zitti! Il bambino **sta dormendo**.* (right now)
4. *Cosa **fai** la sera di solito?* (regularly)
5. *In questo momento Maria **sta cucinando** la cena.* (right now)
6. ***Studio** italiano da due anni.* (duration — simple present)
7. *Aspetta un attimo, **mi sto vestendo**.* (right now)
8. *Ogni domenica **vado** al parco.* (regularly)

## Lesson cheat sheet

```
FORMULA:
  stare + gerund
  (conjugates) (doesn't change)

STARE:
  sto    stai   sta    stiamo   state   stanno

GERUND — ONE OF TWO ENDINGS:
  -are  →  -ando      parlare  → parlando
  -ere  →  -endo      leggere  → leggendo
  -ire  →  -endo      dormire  → dormendo

EXCEPTIONS (memorize as a chant):
  fare       →  facendo
  dire       →  dicendo
  bere       →  bevendo
  tradurre   →  traducendo

USE IT WHEN:
  Right NOW, in the moment:
    Sto parlando al telefono.
    Cosa stai facendo?
  Cues — ora, adesso, in questo momento.

DON'T USE IT (use simple present instead):
  Habits:           Ogni mattina prendo il caffè.
  General state:    Lavoro in banca.
  Near future:      Domani vado a Roma.   (NOT sto andando!)
  Stative verbs:    Lo so. Ti amo. Capisco.

ENGLISH-SPEAKER REMINDER:
  English uses "I'm -ing" MUCH more freely than Italian uses stare+gerundio.
  "I'm reading every day" → Leggo ogni giorno (habit, simple present).
  Continuous is for THE LIVE MOMENT only.

PRONOUNS — TWO OPTIONS:
  Lo sto leggendo.       (in front — conversational)
  Sto leggendolo.        (attached — formal)
  Both correct.
```
