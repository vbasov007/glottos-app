# German Course Curriculum (50 units)
**Glottos Matrix — structural language-learning course**

> Principle: 5% understanding the rule, 95% training your mouth. Every topic gets drilled through the matrix: you → you-plural → he/she/they.

## BLOCK 2: FOUNDATION OF CASES (Lessons 9–18) → Test "Ritter / Ritterin" (Knight)

> **Heads up for English speakers:** German has four grammatical cases (Nominative, Accusative, Dative, Genitive). English used to have them too — that's why we still say "I/me" or "who/whom". German never gave them up. The article in front of a noun changes based on its role in the sentence. Don't panic — half the time only the article changes, and 50% of the patterns are predictable.

## How to work with each unit

1. **Theory** — watch the video / read the rule (5 minutes)
2. **Translation** — translate phrases from English to German, check against the key
3. **Language scales** — run the new rule through all persons (ich → du → er → wir → ihr → sie)
4. **Matrix** — drill through Q&A in three scenarios: you (sg.) → you (pl.) → third persons
5. **Mirror principle** — the answer is already encoded in the question, don't build from scratch

## Memory hacks

- **Articles**: 50% are predictable from suffix (-ung → die, -chen → das). The rest — visualize with color (blue = der, green = das, red = die). English has no gender — assign one mentally from day one.
- **Plurals**: learn through the "Ein... → Viele..." contrast.
- **Irregular verbs**: group by vowel pattern (bleiben-blieb-geblieben = schreiben-schrieb-geschrieben). Always learn with the auxiliary in a sentence: *Ich bin geblieben*.
- **Cognates**: ride the free vocabulary — Haus/house, Wasser/water, gut/good, Maus/mouse, Buch/book, Hand/hand, Finger/finger. ~40% of basic German words have an English cousin.
- **Word order trap**: German is V2 (verb second) in main clauses, verb-LAST in subordinate clauses. English is rigid SVO. This is the single biggest mental shift — practice it harder than vocabulary.
