# Test for lesson 24: Wissen or kennen?

**How do you say this in German?**
Translate the phrases into German. Use the grammar and vocabulary from the lesson.

---

1. Do you know this man (are you familiar with him)?
2. I know (as a fact) that he is married.
3. We are familiar with this company.
4. He knows (as a fact) where she works.
5. I know his son (am familiar with him).
6. Do you know (as a fact) whether he is married?
7. She knows Berlin well (is familiar with it).
8. Do you know his phone number (as a fact)?
9. I don't know this lawyer (am not familiar with him).
10. We don't know (as a fact) when the course begins.
11. Do you (plural) know my (female) partner (are you familiar with her)?
12. She knows (as a fact) that I am self-employed.
13. Are you familiar with this university?
14. I know (as a fact) that she is a nurse.
15. He doesn't know the way (isn't familiar with it).
16. What is your profession? — I'm an engineer.
17. Are you married? — No, I'm divorced.
18. Do you have children? — Yes, I have a son and a daughter.
19. What is your education? — I studied at university.
20. Do you know my boss (are you familiar with him)? — Yes, I know him.
21. Do you know (as a fact) where he lives? — Yes, I know where he lives.
22. She knows (as a fact) that I am unemployed.
23. We know his daughter (are familiar with her). — We know (as a fact) that he has a daughter.
24. I know this lawyer (am familiar with him). — I know (as a fact) that he is a lawyer.
25. Do you know this city (are you familiar with it)? — Yes, I know it well.
26. Does your brother know my (female) partner (is he familiar with her)? — No, he doesn't know her.
27. Does your sister know (as a fact) that I am married? — Yes, she knows that.
28. Where did you get your degree? — At the Technical University in Munich.
29. Are you familiar with the University of Munich? — Yes, I know it well. I studied there.
30. What is your profession? — I'm an engineer. I work at a big company. — Are you married? — No, divorced. I have two children.
