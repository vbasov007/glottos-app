# Test for lesson 17: Personal pronouns mich/mir/dich/dir/ihn/ihm + shopping

**How do you say this in German?**
Translate the phrases into German. Use the grammar and vocabulary from the lesson.

---

1. Do you see me? (Akk)
2. I'm helping you. (Dat — helfen takes Dat)
3. He hears us. (Akk)
4. I see him every day. (Akk)
5. How much does this cost?
6. I pay by card.
7. She helps him in the shop. (Dat)
8. Can I try this on?
9. This doesn't fit me. (Dat — passen takes Dat)
10. The salesman shows us the discounts. (Dat)
11. Do you know them? (Akk)
12. I'm buying this for you. (Akk — für + Akk)
13. She gives him the receipt. (Dat)
14. Do you have this in size M?
15. I'm looking for a jacket.
16. We're buying her a present. (Dat)
17. I need him. (Akk)
18. Do you pay in cash or by card?
19. Do you hear me? (Akk) — Yes, I hear you. (Akk)
20. The fitting room is on the right.
21. Where is the cashier?
22. Is there a special offer today?
23. Can you help me? (Dat) — Of course. What are you looking for?
24. She shows them the jacket. (Dat)
25. This dress doesn't fit me. Show me another size. (both Dat)
26. Can I exchange this? — Yes, bring me the receipt. (Dat)
27. We see her in the shop every day. (Akk)
28. He gives me the receipt (Dat), and I give him the money. (Dat)
29. How much does this sweater cost? — Forty-five euros. There's a ten percent discount.
30. Are you buying this for me? (Akk) — Yes, I'm buying it for you. (Akk) I'm paying cash.
