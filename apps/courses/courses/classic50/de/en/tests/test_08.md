# Block-Test 1 (Lessons 1–8): Knappe / Knappin — survival pre-A1

**How do you say this in German?**
Big test covering the whole first block: alphabet and numbers, pronouns, haben/sein, simple verbs, stem-changing verbs, modal verbs, possessives, question words. Translate into German.

---

1. Hi! How are you?
2. My name is Anna. And you?
3. Where are you from? — I'm from Russia.
4. How old are you? — I'm twenty-five.
5. One, two, three, four, five.
6. I have a brother and a sister.
7. He doesn't have a car.
8. I'm tired. We are hungry.
9. She is a doctor. They are students.
10. Where do you live? — I live in Berlin.
11. What are you doing? — I'm learning German.
12. My father works at a bank.
13. We play football on weekends.
14. Where are you going? — I'm going to Hamburg.
15. He's reading a book. She speaks German.
16. What do you see? — I see a dog.
17. Do you speak English? — Yes, a little.
18. I can come tomorrow.
19. You have to study.
20. I want pizza.
21. You can't smoke here.
22. I would like a coffee, please. (polite)
23. My mother is a teacher.
24. This is our house. That's her brother.
25. Are your kids already at school?
26. Where is your father? — He's at work.
27. This is my shirt. It's blue.
28. His trousers are black, and her skirt is red.
29. Who is that? — That's my uncle.
30. What is that? — That's a table.
31. What's your name? — My name is Thomas.
32. When are you coming home? — At seven.
33. Why are you learning German? — I work in Germany.
34. Where is the bed? — In the bedroom.
35. What floor do you live on? — On the third floor.
36. How much does this chair cost? — Forty euros.
37. Do you have a bathroom and a kitchen?
38. Where is the mirror? — In the bathroom.
39. What do you have in the living room? — A sofa, a table and a wardrobe.
40. I eat bread and drink water.
41. We drink tea in the morning.
42. He likes vegetables and fruit.
43. I don't like coffee without sugar.
44. How old is your sister? — She's eighteen.
45. Do they have children? — Yes, they have two kids.
46. Do you (pl.) speak German? — No, only English.
47. Does your son go to school? — Yes, he is eight.
48. I want to eat, but I can't cook.
49. Thank you! — You're welcome!
50. Goodbye! Bye!
