# Test for lesson 20: Separable prefixes aufstehen/anfangen/einkaufen + work

**How do you say this in German?**
Translate the phrases into German. Use the grammar and vocabulary from the lesson.

---

1. I get up at seven o'clock.
2. When do you get up?
3. Work starts at nine.
4. We shop at the supermarket.
5. Please close the door.
6. Are you coming with us?
7. I call my mother every evening.
8. The train leaves at eight.
9. When are you coming back?
10. I have to get up at five o'clock.
11. He wants to start earlier.
12. We can shop at the supermarket.
13. She calls her brother.
14. The lunch break is at twelve.
15. I eat at the canteen.
16. He finishes work at six.
17. Do you want to come with us?
18. I come home at seven.
19. He has a meeting today.
20. She commutes to work by S-Bahn.
21. When does your meeting start? — It starts at ten.
22. I'm doing overtime today.
23. When do you finish work? — Usually at six.
24. He understands me. (inseparable!)
25. She tells a story. (inseparable!)
26. I receive a letter. (inseparable!)
27. When do you get up? — I get up at six. And you? — I have to get up at five, I commute by S-Bahn.
28. Do you eat at the canteen? — Yes, during the lunch break. When do you finish work? — At six, but today I'm doing overtime.
29. My colleague starts work at eight and finishes at five. He commutes forty minutes to work.
30. I get up every morning at half past six, start work at nine, and come home at half past six in the evening.
