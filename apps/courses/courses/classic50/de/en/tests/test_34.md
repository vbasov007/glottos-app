# Test for Lesson 34: Weil / denn. Time prepositions vor / in / seit. Argumentation

**How do you say this in German?**
Translate the phrases into German. Use the grammar and vocabulary from the lesson.

---

1. I am staying at home because I am sick. (weil)
2. He is buying a new car because his old one is broken. (denn)
3. Two years ago I moved to Berlin.
4. In three days we have an exam.
5. She has been living in Munich for half a year.
6. In my opinion, that is a good idea.
7. I am in favor because it is important. (weil)
8. He is tired because he worked all day. (denn)
9. I am learning German because I want to live in Germany. (weil)
10. She isn't going to the party because she has a lot of work. (weil)
11. We aren't going on vacation because we have no money. (denn)
12. I agree with you.
13. He is of a different opinion.
14. I think that we need more time.
15. In a week I have an exam.
16. He has been working here for three months.
17. She is happy because she passed the exam. (weil)
18. I am against it because the argument doesn't convince me. (denn)
19. I am calling you because I have an important question. (weil)
20. That's right. / That's not right.
21. For example, we can start tomorrow.
22. She is learning German because she wants to study in Vienna. (weil)
23. Nevertheless, I think that it is a good idea.
24. Are you for or against this project?
25. He is staying at home because he has to get up early tomorrow. (weil)
26. I will come later because I have an appointment. (denn)
27. How long have you lived here? — For three years. I moved here three years ago.
28. What happened two years ago? — Two years ago I started learning German.
29. Are you for or against? — I am in favor because the plan is simple and fast. (weil)
30. Why are you learning German? — Because my wife is German. That's why I moved to Germany a year ago and have been living here for a year.
