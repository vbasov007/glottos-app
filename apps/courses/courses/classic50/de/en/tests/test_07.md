# Test for lesson 7: Possessive pronouns + clothes and colors

**How do you say this in German?**
Translate the phrases into German.

---

1. My jacket is red.
2. Where is your hat? (informal)
3. His shirt is blue.
4. Her dress is white.
5. Our bag is brown.
6. Your socks are gray. (informal singular)
7. This is my brother.
8. Is this your bag? (informal)
9. His coat is black.
10. Her sweater is green.
11. Our gloves are white.
12. Your shoes are brown. (plural)
13. What color is your T-shirt? — My T-shirt is yellow.
14. Is that his belt? — No, that's my belt.
15. Her skirt is pink, and my skirt is black.
16. I can cook well. (modal)
17. She'd like to order coffee and cake. (polite)
18. You have to drink more water. (objective necessity)
19. His mother is a doctor. Her father is a teacher.
20. My child is playing in the park.
21. Where is your sweater? — My sweater is at home.
22. Her trousers are gray, and his trousers are black.
23. Our grandpa lives in Hamburg.
24. What color is her coat? — Her coat is brown.
25. Is this your hat? — No, that's her hat. My hat is gray.
26. We want to buy new shoes. (firm intention)
27. His jacket is blue, and her jacket is red.
28. We're not allowed to smoke here.
29. What color are your gloves? (plural) — Our gloves are black.
30. My sister would like to order soup and bread with butter, and I'd like fish with rice. (polite)
