# Test for lesson 42: Passiv (wird + Part. II) and Passiv Perfekt (ist + Part. II + worden) + science and medicine

**How do you say this in German?**
Translate the phrases into German.

---

1. The patient is being examined by the doctor.
2. The medication was developed last year.
3. The experiment is being carried out in the laboratory.
4. The results have been published.
5. The disease is treated with a new therapy.
6. The vaccine is being tested on volunteers.
7. A new gene was discovered by a German scientist.
8. The patient was operated on yesterday.
9. The research is being carried out by an international team.
10. The therapy was prescribed by the doctor.
11. Three experiments are being carried out simultaneously.
12. The effect of the medication has been proven.
13. The results are published every month.
14. The medication has been tested successfully.
15. The patients are examined every three months.
16. The vaccine was developed two years ago.
17. A new virus was discovered by the researcher.
18. How is this disease treated?
19. Where is the experiment being carried out? — It is being carried out in the university laboratory.
20. Has the patient been examined? — Yes, he was examined yesterday.
21. Has the research already been published? — No, it has not yet been published.
22. The gene was discovered by an international team.
23. When was the vaccine developed? — It was developed last year.
24. The sick person is treated in this clinic. (Passiv)
25. The experiments are carried out every day.
26. The result was proven by a well-known scientist.
27. The (female) patient is well treated. (Passiv)
28. A new medication was developed by the company.
29. By whom was this gene discovered? — It was discovered by a German researcher.
30. The research was carried out, and the results were published.
