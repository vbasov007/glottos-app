# Test for lesson 48: Conjunctions indem / so dass + public speaking

**How do you say this in German?**
Translate the phrases into German. Use the grammar and vocabulary from the lesson.

---

1. You learn German by reading every day.
2. He spoke loudly, so that everyone heard him.
3. She convinces the audience by bringing clear arguments.
4. The talk was well structured, so that the audience understood everything.
5. You become a good speaker by speaking regularly in front of people.
6. I prepare for the talk by writing an outline.
7. She supported her thesis so well that no one objected.
8. How do you summarize the results? — I summarize the results by repeating the main points. (use indem)
9. How do you convince the listeners? — I convince them by providing evidence.
10. The students practiced so much that the talk was perfect.
11. He strengthens his argument by citing statistics.
12. I improve my speech through constant practice. (use durch)
13. She improves her pronunciation by reading aloud every day.
14. The professor spoke loudly enough that even the back row heard everything.
15. She contradicted the speaker by bringing a counterargument. (use indem)
16. In my opinion, more evidence needs to be cited.
17. We can conclude that this argument is stronger.
18. He speaks slowly so that everyone understands. (use damit)
19. He spoke slowly, so that everyone understood. (use so dass)
20. She is preparing a handout so that it becomes more illustrative. (damit)
21. She prepared a handout, so that it became more illustrative. (so dass)
22. I stay calm during the presentation by breathing deeply.
23. How does he prepare for the talk? — He prepares by reading scientific articles. (use indem)
24. Can you justify your point of view? — Yes, studies show that practice is more effective.
25. The speaker summarized, so that the discussion could begin.
26. Let me summarize. — Please, go ahead and summarize.
27. He convinces others through the presentation of facts. (use durch)
28. I learn new vocabulary by using flashcards.
29. The (female) speaker spoke so clearly that everyone applauded.
30. I am against this view. Theory is important, but you learn better by practicing. You have to give concrete examples and take a clear stand on this issue.
