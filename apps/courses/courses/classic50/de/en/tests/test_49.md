# Test for lesson 49: Modal verbs — subjective meaning + particles halt/eben/doch

**How do you say this in German?**
Translate the phrases into German. Use the grammar and vocabulary from the lesson.

---

1. He must be sick — he looks terrible. (subjective: speaker's strong inference)
2. She is said to be very rich. (hearsay)
3. He claims to have seen everything. (he asserts about himself)
4. That is probably right. (cautious assumption: dürfte)
5. Maybe that was a mistake. (possibility: kann)
6. Take a look here! (with the softening particle mal)
7. You know that, after all! (with reminder particle doch)
8. That's nonsense!
9. He must have forgotten the keys. (inference about the past)
10. He is said to be very strict. (hearsay)
11. She claims to have known everything. (her own assertion, past)
12. Why is Thomas absent again? — He must have been sick again.
13. Is the rumor about the company true? — That's probably unfortunately true.
14. She is said to speak three languages fluently.
15. He is said to have received a better offer.
16. Well, that's just how it is. (use halt)
17. Exactly, that's what I said. (use eben and ja)
18. Oh, just come with us! (use doch)
19. Could you give me a hand? (use mal)
20. So what are you actually up to this weekend?
21. He's probably already home. (use wohl)
22. That's true, sure, but there's a problem. (use schon, conceding)
23. But you said you'd come! (use doch, reproachful)
24. That's complete nonsense! (use ja, surprise)
25. No idea, we'll see. (use keine Ahnung, mal)
26. Well, I'm just tired. (use na ja, halt)
27. That guy is really cool, isn't he?
28. Do you think he's really sick? — He must be sick, he's coughing the whole time.
29. Have you heard he's a millionaire? — He claims to be a millionaire, but I don't believe him.
30. What do people say about the new boss? — He is said to be very strict. That may be so, but there's no proof. That's just how it is — he's new, after all.
