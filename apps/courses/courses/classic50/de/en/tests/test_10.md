# Test for lesson 10: Dativ + getting around the city

**How do you say this in German?**
Translate the phrases into German.

---

1. I'm helping my neighbor.
2. He answers the teacher.
3. She thanks the doctor.
4. My uncle likes the city.
5. The bike belongs to the girl.
6. Do you believe me? — Yes, I believe you.
7. We're showing the guest the way.
8. He's giving his wife flowers.
9. I give the waiter a tip.
10. We are congratulating our grandpa.
11. Where is the train station? — Go straight ahead to the intersection, then right.
12. How do I get to the post office? — Take the second street on the left.
13. Is there a bus stop nearby? — Yes, the stop is on the corner.
14. Is the hospital far from here? — No, five minutes on foot.
15. The library is opposite the town hall.
16. Go across the bridge and then straight ahead.
17. I ask the doctor. — I answer the doctor.
18. She sees the neighbor. — She helps the neighbor.
19. I'm buying the cake. He's looking for the checkout.
20. We need a shopping cart. Do you see the discount?
21. What color is your jacket? — My jacket is blue.
22. Where do you live? — I live in an apartment in Berlin.
23. Where are you going? — To the train station.
24. I'd like to order a coffee and bread with cheese. (polite)
25. Can you show me the way to the market square? (formal)
26. He's showing his colleague the way to the library.
27. The car belongs to my aunt. She lives near the hospital.
28. Where is the nearest parking lot? — Go straight ahead to the traffic light, then left.
29. We're giving our children books. The children thank us.
30. Excuse me, how do I get to the department store? — Go straight ahead, then take the second street on the right. The department store is opposite the library, next to the town hall.
