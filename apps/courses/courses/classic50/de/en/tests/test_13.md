# Test for lesson 13: Time um/von...bis/seit + times of day

**How do you say this in German?**
Translate the phrases into German. Use the grammar and vocabulary from the lesson.

---

1. It's eight o'clock now.
2. Half past eight. (8:30)
3. Quarter past six. (6:15)
4. Quarter to twelve. (11:45)
5. I get up at half past six.
6. We work from nine to five.
7. She has been learning German since October.
8. In the evening I read a book.
9. The train leaves at fourteen thirty.
10. Do you always come on time?
11. He works early.
12. She comes late.
13. I have no time in the morning.
14. Half past twelve. (12:30)
15. The course starts at nine o'clock.
16. The meeting lasts two hours — from ten to twelve.
17. In winter it gets dark early.
18. We have been living in Germany for three years.
19. What time do you get up? — At half past six.
20. The alarm clock rings at six.
21. Do you have time on Wednesday afternoon?
22. The pharmacy is open from eight to eighteen o'clock.
23. Don't be late! Be punctual! (du)
24. My brother gets up at five in the morning. He works early.
25. How long have you been learning German? — For four months.
26. When does the German course start? — At nine o'clock. Don't be late!
27. Do you have time on Wednesday afternoon? — Yes, I'm free from two to four.
28. I don't have an alarm clock. I get up late.
29. He works from eight to six. In the evening he doesn't work.
30. How long have you been living in Berlin? — Since last year. Eight months already. I work from nine to five.
