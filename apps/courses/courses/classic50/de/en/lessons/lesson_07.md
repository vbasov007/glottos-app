# Lesson 7: Possessive pronouns
**Vocabulary: Clothing and colors**

---

## How to work with this lesson

1. **Read** the rule — get the logic (5 minutes)
2. **Say it out loud** — slowly, consciously, every form
3. **Speed up** — run the scales and matrix until the forms fly out

> You can say what you do, can, and want. Now learn to say "mine", "yours", "his". Without these — you're handcuffed.

---

## Part 1: What possessive pronouns are

Possessive pronouns answer the question "whose?". Here's the full list:

| Pronoun | Possessive | English | Cognate |
|---|---|---|---|
| <say>ich</say> | <say>**mein**</say> | my / mine | ≈ "mine" |
| <say>du</say> | <say>**dein**</say> | your / yours (sg.) | ≈ "thine" (Shakespeare!) |
| <say>er</say> | <say>**sein**</say> | his | |
| <say>sie</say> | <say>**ihr**</say> | her | |
| <say>es</say> | <say>**sein**</say> | its | |
| <say>wir</say> | <say>**unser**</say> | our / ours | ≈ "ours" |
| <say>ihr</say> | <say>**euer**</say> | your / yours (pl.) | |
| <say>sie</say> (they) | <say>**ihr**</say> | their | |
| <say>Sie</say> (formal) | <say>**Ihr**</say> | your (formal) | |

Nine words. Looks like a lot, but you'll see — the endings are all the same.

Cognate notes: <say>**mein**</say> = "mine", <say>**dein**</say> = "thine" (English's old word for "thy/yours, singular"). <say>**unser**</say> = "ours" (drop the e). Once again — English used to have all of this, then simplified.

---

## Part 2: The main hack

> Possessive pronouns take the SAME endings as <say>ein/eine/ein</say>. If you know <say>ein</say>-words, you already know possessives.

Compare:

| | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|---|---|---|---|
| <say>**ein**</say> | <say>ein Bruder</say> | <say>ein**e** Schwester</say> | <say>ein Kind</say> |
| <say>**mein**</say> | <say>mein Bruder</say> | <say>mein**e** Schwester</say> | <say>mein Kind</say> |
| <say>**dein**</say> | <say>dein Bruder</say> | <say>dein**e** Schwester</say> | <say>dein Kind</say> |
| <say>**sein**</say> | <say>sein Bruder</say> | <say>sein**e** Schwester</say> | <say>sein Kind</say> |

See? Masculine and neuter — no ending. Feminine — ending **-e**. Just like <say>ein/eine/ein</say>.

This works for ALL possessives. No exceptions.

**Word of warning for English speakers:** German nouns have **gender**: masculine (<say>der</say>), feminine (<say>die</say>), neuter (<say>das</say>). English has no gender ("the dog" is the same word whether the dog is male, female, or robot). The possessive ending changes based on the gender of the **possessed thing**, not the owner. So **his sister** = *<say>seine Schwester</say>* (feminine ending -e) because *<say>Schwester</say>* is feminine. **Her brother** = *<say>ihr Bruder</say>* (no ending) because *<say>Bruder</say>* is masculine.

In English: HER = her, period. In German: *<say>ihr</say> / <say>ihre</say> / <say>ihr</say> / <say>ihre</say>* depending on what's being owned. Brace yourself.

## Part 3: Full table (Nominativ)

| | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| <say>**mein**</say> | <say>mein Vater</say> | <say>mein**e** Mutter</say> | <say>mein Kind</say> | <say>mein**e** Kinder</say> |
| <say>**dein**</say> | <say>dein Vater</say> | <say>dein**e** Mutter</say> | <say>dein Kind</say> | <say>dein**e** Kinder</say> |
| <say>**sein**</say> | <say>sein Vater</say> | <say>sein**e** Mutter</say> | <say>sein Kind</say> | <say>sein**e** Kinder</say> |
| <say>**ihr**</say> | <say>ihr Vater</say> | <say>ihr**e** Mutter</say> | <say>ihr Kind</say> | <say>ihr**e** Kinder</say> |
| <say>**unser**</say> | <say>unser Vater</say> | <say>unser**e** Mutter</say> | <say>unser Kind</say> | <say>unser**e** Kinder</say> |
| <say>**euer**</say> | <say>euer Vater</say> | <say>eur**e** Mutter</say> | <say>euer Kind</say> | <say>eur**e** Kinder</say> |
| <say>**ihr**</say> | <say>ihr Vater</say> | <say>ihr**e** Mutter</say> | <say>ihr Kind</say> | <say>ihr**e** Kinder</say> |
| <say>**Ihr**</say> | <say>Ihr Vater</say> | <say>Ihr**e** Mutter</say> | <say>Ihr Kind</say> | <say>Ihr**e** Kinder</say> |

The rule is simple: masculine and neuter — bare form. Feminine and plural — add **-e**.

---

## Traps

> **Trap 1: sein vs ihr**
> <say>**sein**</say> = his (from <say>**er**</say>). <say>**ihr**</say> = her (from <say>**sie**</say>). Beginners mix these up constantly.
> Anchor: **s**ein — from <say>**er**</say> (h**e**), <say>**ihr**</say> — from **s**ie (sh**e**).
> *<say>Sein Bruder</say>* = his brother. *<say>Ihr Bruder</say>* = her brother. Get this wrong and you change the meaning. Drill it.

> **Trap 2: euer drops its -e-**
> When you add the ending -e, the second "e" in <say>euer</say> drops: <say>eu**e**r</say> → <say>eur**e**</say>. Correct: <say>eur**e** Mutter</say>. Wrong: ~~<say>euere Mutter</say>~~. The mouth doesn't like three vowels in a row.

## Part 4: Vocabulary — Clothing and colors

### Clothing

| German | English | Cognate hint |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| <say>die Hose</say> | trousers / pants | |
| <say>der Rock</say> | skirt | ≈ "frock" (English once meant outer garment) |
| <say>der Mantel</say> | coat | ≈ "mantle" |
| <say>die Schuhe</say> | shoes | ≈ "shoes" |
| <say>die Socken</say> | socks | ≈ "socks" |
| <say>das T-Shirt</say> | T-shirt | direct steal |
| <say>das Hemd</say> | shirt | (same root as "chemise") |
| <say>das Kleid</say> | dress | |
| <say>die Jacke</say> | jacket | ≈ "jacket" |
| <say>der Pullover</say> | sweater | direct steal from English |
| <say>die Mütze</say> | hat / beanie | |
| <say>die Handschuhe</say> | gloves | lit. "hand-shoes". Beautiful logic. |
| <say>der Gürtel</say> | belt | ≈ "girdle" |
| <say>die Tasche</say> | bag | |

### Colors

| German | English | Cognate hint |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| <say>rot</say> | red | ≈ "red" |
| <say>blau</say> | blue | ≈ "blue" |
| <say>grün</say> | green | ≈ "green" |
| <say>gelb</say> | yellow | (same root, vowel shifted) |
| <say>schwarz</say> | black | (no cognate — just learn) |
| <say>weiß</say> | white | ≈ "white" |
| <say>grau</say> | gray | ≈ "gray" |
| <say>braun</say> | brown | ≈ "brown" |
| <say>rosa</say> | pink | ≈ "rose / rosé" |
| <say>orange</say> | orange | direct |

> **Remember:** Colors go after <say>sein</say> or before the noun. *<say>Mein Mantel ist schwarz.</say>* / *<say>Ich habe eine rote Jacke.</say>* When the color sits BEFORE the noun, it takes an ending — but that's a topic for later. For now, use colors after <say>sein</say>: "<say>X ist [color]</say>" — safe.

## Part 5: Language scales

### Scale 1 — possessive + family

Run it out loud. Change only the possessive — everything else on autopilot:

```
Mein Vater ist Arzt
Dein Vater ist Arzt
Sein Vater ist Arzt
Ihr Vater ist Arzt
Unser Vater ist Arzt
Euer Vater ist Arzt

Meine Mutter ist Lehrerin
Deine Mutter ist Lehrerin
Seine Mutter ist Lehrerin
Ihre Mutter ist Lehrerin
Unsere Mutter ist Lehrerin
Eure Mutter ist Lehrerin
```

### Scale 2 — possessive + clothing + color

```
Meine Jacke ist blau
Deine Jacke ist blau
Seine Jacke ist blau
Ihre Jacke ist blau
Unsere Jacke ist blau
Eure Jacke ist blau

Mein Mantel ist schwarz
Dein Mantel ist schwarz
Sein Mantel ist schwarz
Ihr Mantel ist schwarz
Unser Mantel ist schwarz
Euer Mantel ist schwarz
```

### Scale 3 — haben + possessive

Here for masculine, the form changes after <say>haben</say>: <say>mein</say> → <say>mein**en**</say>. Don't dig into why yet — just learn the sound. The Akkusativ explanation lands in Lesson 9.

```
Ich habe meinen Mantel
Du hast deinen Mantel
Er hat seinen Mantel
Sie hat ihren Mantel
Wir haben unseren Mantel
Ihr habt euren Mantel

Ich habe meine Tasche
Du hast deine Tasche
Er hat seine Tasche
Sie hat ihre Tasche
Wir haben unsere Tasche
Ihr habt eure Tasche
```

Repeat each scale 3 times. Then close the text and do it from memory.

## Part 6: Language matrix (Q-A)

### Matrix 1 — "You and I"

```
Ist das dein Bruder? – Ja, das ist mein Bruder
Ist das deine Tasche? – Ja, das ist meine Tasche
Ist das dein Mantel? – Ja, das ist mein Mantel
Ist das deine Mütze? – Ja, das ist meine Mütze
Ist das dein Hemd? – Ja, das ist mein Hemd
Ist das dein Pullover? – Ja, das ist mein Pullover
```

### Matrix 2 — Third persons

```
Ist das sein Auto? – Ja, das ist sein Auto
Ist das ihre Mutter? – Ja, das ist ihre Mutter
Ist das sein Hund? – Ja, das ist sein Hund
Ist das ihre Jacke? – Ja, das ist ihre Jacke
Ist das sein Kind? – Ja, das ist sein Kind
Ist das ihre Tasche? – Ja, das ist ihre Tasche
```

### Matrix 3 — Situational (clothing and shopping)

```
Welche Farbe hat dein T-Shirt? – Mein T-Shirt ist grün
Welche Farbe hat deine Hose? – Meine Hose ist schwarz
Welche Farbe hat sein Mantel? – Sein Mantel ist braun
Welche Farbe haben ihre Schuhe? – Ihre Schuhe sind weiß
Welche Farbe hat eure Jacke? – Unsere Jacke ist rot
Welche Farbe haben deine Socken? – Meine Socken sind grau
```

Drill each matrix until you answer without a pause.

## Lesson cheat sheet

```
POSSESSIVE PRONOUNS
Endings = same as ein/eine/ein!

mein (my — ≈ mine), dein (your — ≈ thine), sein (his), ihr (her)
unser (our — ≈ ours), euer (your pl.), ihr (their), Ihr (your formal)

Masculine / Neuter — no ending:
  mein Mantel, dein Kind

Feminine / Plural — ending -e:
  meine Jacke, deine Schuhe

Traps:
  sein (his, from er) vs ihr (her, from sie) — don't mix up!
  euer + ending → eure (NOT euere)

Reminder: ending depends on the gender of the OWNED thing,
  not on who owns it. (Different from English!)

Clothing: Hose, Rock, Mantel, Schuhe, Socken,
  T-Shirt, Hemd, Kleid, Jacke, Pullover,
  Mütze, Handschuhe (hand-shoes!), Gürtel, Tasche

Colors: rot, blau, grün, gelb, schwarz,
  weiß, grau, braun, rosa, orange
  (most are direct cognates)
```
