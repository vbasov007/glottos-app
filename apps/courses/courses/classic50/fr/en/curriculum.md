# French Course Curriculum (50 units)
**Glottos Matrix — structural language-learning course for English speakers**

> Principle: 5% understanding the rule, 95% training your mouth. Every topic gets drilled through the matrix: je → tu → il/elle → nous → vous → ils/elles.

---

## BLOCK 1: A1 FOUNDATIONS (Lessons 1–10) → Test "Apprenti / Apprentie" (Apprentice)

*Goal: build the spine of the language — pronouns, present tense, gender, basic agreement, negation.*

| # | Grammar | Vocabulary |
|---|---------|-----------|
| 1 | Read French aloud and hear the liaison | Greetings and politeness: bonjour, merci, s'il vous plaît, au revoir, pardon |
| 2 | Tell masculine from feminine nouns; form plurals | Everyday objects: house, food, animals |
| 3 | Use articles: definite, indefinite, partitive (du / de la / des) | Food and drinks, places in town, days of the week, months |
| 4 | The verb être: "I am a teacher", "I'm from England" | Nationalities and countries, professions, family members |
| 5 | The verb avoir + il y a: "I have", "there is/are" | Personal items, body parts, physical state, age |
| 6 | Describe with adjectives — agreement and position | Description: colors, size, character, appearance |
| 7 | Conjugate regular -ER verbs in the present | Action verbs: parler, manger, habiter, travailler, étudier, regarder, écouter, aimer |
| 8 | Say "no" (ne … pas) and ask questions | Question words: qui, que, quand, où, comment, combien, pourquoi |
| 9 | -IR verbs and aller. "I'm going to..." — futur proche | Travel, movement, transport, countries (à, en, au, aux) |
| 10 | -RE verbs and venir de + inf. "I've just done..." | Everyday scenes: faire les courses, prendre le métro |

**→ TEST "APPRENTI / APPRENTIE" (Apprentice): you can introduce yourself, order at a café, and find your way around town, level pre-A1 → A1**

## How to work with each unit

1. **Theory** — watch the video / read the rule (5 minutes)
2. **Translation** — translate phrases from English to French, check against the key
3. **Language scales** — run the new rule through every person (je → tu → il → nous → vous → ils)
4. **Matrix** — drill through question-and-answer in three scenarios: you (sg) → you (pl) → third persons
5. **Mirror principle** — the answer is already embedded in the question; don't build from scratch

## Memory hacks for English speakers

- **Gender**: 70% is determined by ending (-tion → la, -ment → le, -age → le, -té → la). The rest — learn by color: blue = *le*, red = *la*. English has no gender on nouns, so this is the single biggest mental shift you'll make.
- **Plurals**: learn by contrast — "Un livre... → Des livres..." The final -s is silent, only the article tells you it's plural in speech.
- **Irregular verbs**: group by pattern (prendre / comprendre / apprendre conjugate the same way). Always learn together with their auxiliary verb: *Je suis allé*, not just *allé*.
- **Liaison**: memorize the "liaison zone" — after *les, des, mes, mon, un, deux, trois* etc., a silent final consonant wakes up before a vowel: *les_amis* → "lay-zah-mee". Same instinct as English *an apple* vs *a banana* — the *n* appears to bridge a vowel.
- **The French R**: nothing like English R. It's a guttural sound, like a soft gargle at the back of the throat. If you can't make it yet, just use English R — French speakers will understand. Russian/Spanish rolled R is worse than English R for French ears.
- **Cognates**: roughly 40% of English vocabulary comes from French (after 1066 — *table, nation, information, restaurant, café*). When in doubt, try the English word with French pronunciation — you'll often be right.
