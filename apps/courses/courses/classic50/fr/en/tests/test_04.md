# Test for Lesson 4: Personal pronouns and the verb être

**How do you say it in French?**
Translate each phrase into French. Use the grammar and vocabulary from this lesson.

---

1. I am a student.
2. You are a student. (feminine, informal)
3. He is French.
4. She is French.
5. We are English.
6. You are American. (formal singular)
7. They are Italian. (men)
8. They are Italian. (women)
9. I am not a doctor.
10. You are not a teacher. (informal)
11. Who is it? — It is my brother.
12. This is Marie. She is my sister.
13. My father is an engineer.
14. My mother is a journalist.
15. Where are you from? — I am from England.
16. I am from Paris.
17. Paul and Marie are in London.
18. Where are the children? — They are at school.
19. This is a book. The book is on the table.
20. Today is Monday.
21. How old are you? — I am thirty years old. *(careful: age uses avoir)*
22. I have a dog. The dog is black. *(careful: avoir, then être)*
23. Are they students? — Yes, they are students.
24. You are not Italian, you are Spanish. (formal, to a woman)
25. We are happy.
26. Write the number: 40
27. Write the number: 75
28. Hello! Who are you? — I am Paul, I am Canadian.
29. Is this your sister? — No, this is my friend. She is German.
30. My parents are doctors, and I am a journalist.
