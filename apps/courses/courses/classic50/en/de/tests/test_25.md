# Test zu Lektion 25: Past Perfect

**Wie sagt man es auf Englisch?**
Übersetze die Wendungen ins Englische. Nutze die Grammatik und den Wortschatz dieser Lektion.

---

1. Als ich kam, war sie schon gegangen.
2. Als wir ankamen, hatte der Film schon angefangen.
3. Er war müde, weil er die ganze Nacht gearbeitet hatte.
4. Das war das erste Mal, dass ich den Ozean sah.
5. Ich konnte nicht hineinkommen, weil ich die Schlüssel vergessen hatte.
6. Die Diebe waren geflohen, bevor die Polizei kam.
7. Nachdem sie den Brief gelesen hatte, fing sie an zu weinen.
8. Bis Mittag hatte er die ganze Arbeit erledigt.
9. Ich habe sie nicht erkannt — wir hatten uns zehn Jahre nicht gesehen.
10. Sie war vorher noch nie im Ausland gewesen.
11. Das war der beste Film, den er je gesehen hatte.
12. Als ich den Fehler bemerkte, war es schon zu spät.
13. Der Zug war fünf Minuten vor meiner Ankunft abgefahren.
14. Ich wusste die Antwort, weil ich das Buch gelesen hatte.
15. Die Kinder waren müde — sie hatten den ganzen Tag gespielt.
16. Zu dem Zeitpunkt hatte er schon eine Entscheidung getroffen.
17. Sie fragte, ob ich schon mal dort gewesen war.
18. Ich hatte zu Hause gegessen, bevor ich zur Party ging.
19. Sie haben 2015 geheiratet. 2020 waren sie schon geschieden.
20. Als der Bus kam, hatten wir schon eine Stunde gewartet.
21. Als ich die Tür öffnete, waren die Besucher schon gegangen.
22. Es war das schönste Land, das ich besucht hatte.
23. Bis zum Jahresende hatte ich zwanzig Bücher gelesen.
24. Wir begriffen, dass etwas passiert war.
25. Er erzählte mir, dass er sie am Tag zuvor gesehen hatte.
26. So leckeres Essen hatte ich noch nie probiert.
27. Bevor ich nach London zog, hatte ich in München gewohnt.
28. Sie hatte lange an diesem Projekt gearbeitet.
29. Als ich kam, hatte sie schon das Abendessen gekocht.
30. Vor dieser Reise war ich noch nie geflogen.

---

<details><summary>Lösung</summary>

1. *When I came, she had already left.* (oder: *When I arrived, she had already gone.*)
2. *By the time we arrived, the film had already started.* (oder: *By the time we got to the cinema, the movie had begun.*)
3. *He was tired because he had worked all night.* (oder: *He was tired because he had been working all night.*)
4. *It was the first time I had seen the ocean.*
5. *I couldn't enter because I had forgotten my keys.* (oder: *I couldn't get in because I'd forgotten my keys.*)
6. *The thieves had escaped before the police arrived.* (oder: *The robbers had run away before the police came.*)
7. *After she had read the letter, she started crying.* (oder: *After she read the letter, she began to cry.*)
8. *By noon he had finished all his work.* (oder: *By midday he had finished all the work.*)
9. *I didn't recognise her — we hadn't seen each other for ten years.* (oder: *I didn't recognise her — we hadn't met for 10 years.*)
10. *She had never been abroad before.* (oder: *She had never travelled abroad before.*)
11. *It was the best film he had ever watched.* (oder: *It was the best movie he had ever seen.*)
12. *When I realised the mistake, it was already too late.* (oder: *By the time I realised the mistake, it was too late.*)
13. *The train had left five minutes before I ran up.* (oder: *The train had gone five minutes before I arrived.*)
14. *I knew the answer because I had read the book.*
15. *The children were tired — they had played all day.* (oder: *The kids were tired — they had been playing all day.*)
16. *By then he had already made his decision.* (oder: *By that time he had already decided.*)
17. *She asked if I had been there before.* (oder: *She asked whether I had been there before.*)
18. *I had eaten at home before I went to the party.* (oder: *I had had dinner at home before I went to the party.*)
19. *They got married in 2015. By 2020 they had already divorced.* (oder: *They got married in 2015. In 2020 they had already got divorced.*)
20. *When the bus came, we had already been waiting for an hour.* (oder: *When the bus arrived, we had been waiting for an hour.*)
21. *When I opened the door, the visitors had already left.* (oder: *When I opened the door, the guests had already gone.*)
22. *It was the most beautiful country I had visited.* (oder: *It was the most beautiful country I had ever been to.*)
23. *By the end of the year I had read twenty books.*
24. *We realised that something had happened.* (oder: *We understood that something had happened.*)
25. *He told me he had seen her the day before.* (oder: *He told me that he had seen her the previous day.*)
26. *I had never tried such delicious food.* (oder: *I had never eaten such tasty food before.*)
27. *Before I moved to London, I had lived in Moscow.* (oder: *Before moving to London, I had lived in Moscow.*)
28. *She had been working on that project for a long time.* (oder: *She had worked on that project for a long time.*)
29. *When I came, she had already cooked dinner.* (oder: *When I arrived, she had already made dinner.*)
30. *Before that trip, I had never flown on a plane.* (oder: *Before that trip, I had never flown in a plane.*)
