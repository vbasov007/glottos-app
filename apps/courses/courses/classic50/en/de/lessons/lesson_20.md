# Lektion 20: Zusammenhängender Text in Gegenwart und Vergangenheit
**Wortschatz: Verbindungswörter, Wiederholung Block 2**

---

## Wie du mit dieser Lektion arbeitest

1. **Lies** — das ist eine **Festigungslektion**, keine neue Grammatik (5 Minuten)
2. **Lies** die kurzen Texte und versteh, **warum** an jeder Stelle genau diese Form steht
3. **Erzähl** die Texte laut nach — erst mit Blick auf den Text, dann aus dem Gedächtnis
4. **Schreib** deinen eigenen zusammenhängenden Bericht — ein Absatz Gegenwart, ein Absatz Vergangenheit

> Das ist die letzte Lektion in Block 2. Du hast einen riesigen Weg zurückgelegt: Present Simple (L5–L6), Present Continuous (L11), Past Simple (L12–L13), Future (L15), Modalverben (L16), Vergleiche (L17), Präpositionen (L18), Adverbien (L19), und alles verflochten mit Artikeln. Heute — keine neue Grammatik, nur **Zusammenbau**. Das ist der **wichtigste** Lektionstyp im Kurs: Regeln einzeln zu kennen ist eine Sache — sie zusammenhängend in einem echten Text einzusetzen, eine andere. Bevor Block 3 die Perfektformen öffnet, vergewissern wir uns, dass das Fundament steht.

## Teil 1: Wozu „zusammenhängender Text"

In echter Sprache baut niemand einen Satz in Present Simple, dann einen in Past Simple, dann einen in Future. Eine echte Erzählung **wechselt** zwischen Zeitformen, je nachdem, was gerade gesagt werden soll.

> **Typisches Erzählmuster:**
> - **Beschreibung** der jetzigen Situation → Present Simple / Continuous
> - **Was passiert ist** → Past Simple
> - **Was geplant ist / sein wird** → will / be going to
> - **Verbindungswörter** — als Brücken zwischen den Teilen

Im Deutschen machst du das, ohne nachzudenken. Im Englischen — dieselben Übergänge, aber mit **strikten Formen**.

---

## Teil 2: Der wichtigste Hack — vier Stützformen

Merk dir diese Mini-Liste als „Stützpunkte" deiner Erzählung. 95 % eines zusammenhängenden A2-Textes bewegen sich zwischen diesen Formen.

> **Vier Stützzeiten aus Block 2:**
> 1. **Present Simple** — was **üblich** ist: <say>I work in a bank.</say>
> 2. **Present Continuous** — was **gerade jetzt** ist: <say>I'm working at home today.</say>
> 3. **Past Simple** — was **war**: <say>Yesterday I worked in the office.</say>
> 4. **Future** (*will / going to*) — was **sein wird**: <say>Tomorrow I'm going to meet my client.</say>

Wenn du dich bei jedem Satz fragen kannst **„ist das üblich, gerade jetzt, war es oder wird es sein?"** — dann wird die Zeitwahl automatisch.

## Teil 4: Mustertext Nr. 1 — Gegenwart (Tagesablauf)

Lies und versteh, **warum** in jedem Satz genau diese Form gewählt ist.

```
My name is Anna. I'm twenty-five years old, and I live in Berlin.
I work in a small office in the city centre. Every morning I wake
up at seven and have a quick breakfast. Then I take the metro to
work. I usually arrive at the office at half past eight.

My job is interesting, but it isn't easy. I work with three
colleagues. We often have long meetings, and sometimes I'm very
tired at the end of the day. However, I really like my job
because I love what I do.

At the moment I'm sitting at my desk and writing this short text.
The weather is beautiful today, so I'm going to walk home after
work. I'm not going to take the metro. I want to enjoy the sun.
```

### Was hier passiert

- <say>My name is</say>, <say>I'm twenty-five</say>, <say>I live in Berlin</say> — Present Simple, **Fakten über mich**.
- <say>I wake up</say>, <say>I take the metro</say>, <say>I arrive</say> — Present Simple, **Tagesablauf** (mit *every morning, usually*).
- <say>My job is interesting</say>, <say>I work with three colleagues</say> — Present Simple, **allgemeine Fakten**.
- <say>At the moment I'm sitting</say>, <say>writing</say> — Present Continuous, **gerade jetzt**.
- <say>I'm going to walk</say>, <say>I'm not going to take</say> — *be going to*, **Plan für heute Abend**.

> **Beachte:** Die Marker *every morning, usually, at the moment, today* sind keine Verzierung — sie sind **Signale** für die Zeitwahl.

## Teil 5: Mustertext Nr. 2 — Vergangenheit (Bericht über das Wochenende)

```
Last weekend was great. On Saturday morning my friend Tom called
me and said, "Let's go to the lake." I agreed immediately. We
took the train at ten, and we arrived at the lake at noon.

The weather was perfect — sunny, but not too hot. First, we
swam for an hour. Then we had a picnic on the beach. Tom brought
sandwiches and I brought lemonade. After lunch we walked around
the lake. Suddenly, it started to rain, and we ran back to the
station. We were a little wet, but we laughed a lot.

I came home at seven in the evening. I was tired, but very happy.
It was one of the best weekends of the summer. Next weekend we
want to go to the mountains. I'm going to invite Maria too.
```

### Was hier passiert

- <say>was, called, said, agreed, took, arrived</say> — Past Simple, **Handlungsfolge** (regelmäßige und unregelmäßige Verben durcheinander!).
- <say>The weather was perfect</say>, <say>sunny but not too hot</say> — Past Simple von *to be*, **Beschreibung**.
- <say>First, then, after lunch, suddenly</say> — Zeitverbinder.
- <say>I came home</say>, <say>I was tired but very happy</say> — Past Simple, **Bilanz**.
- <say>Next weekend we want to go</say>, <say>I'm going to invite</say> — Gegenwart (Plan im Kopf) + *be going to* (Plan).

> **Beachte:** In der Vergangenheitserzählung gibt es nichts außer Past Simple. Das ist die **Grundkombination zum Erzählen** auf A2.

## Teil 6: Mustertext Nr. 3 — gemischt (mein Leben in diesem Jahr)

```
This year is a special year for me. In January I started a new
job at a big bank. Before this, I worked at a small company for
three years. The change was difficult, but I think it was the
right decision.

I usually arrive at the office at nine, and I leave at six.
The bank is in the city centre, so I walk to work every morning.
I'm learning a lot, and my colleagues are very helpful.

This week is busy because we have an important project. At the
moment, I'm working on a report for our new client. We must
finish it by Friday. Next month I'm going to travel to London
for the first time. I can't wait!
```

### Was hier passiert

- <say>This year is a special year</say> — Present Simple (allgemeines Fakt über das Jahr).
- <say>In January I started</say> — Past Simple (ein Ereignis in der Vergangenheit).
- <say>I worked at a small company for three years</say> — Past Simple (abgeschlossener Zeitraum).
- <say>I usually arrive, I leave, I walk</say> — Present Simple (Tagesablauf).
- <say>I'm learning</say> — Present Continuous („gerade in einem Prozess", erweiterte Bedeutung — diese Lebensphase).
- <say>At the moment I'm working</say> — Present Continuous (genau jetzt).
- <say>We must finish it</say> — Modalverb (Pflicht).
- <say>Next month I'm going to travel</say> — *be going to* (Plan).
- <say>I can't wait</say> — Modalverb.

> **Das ist ein „reifer" A2-Text:** vier Zeitformen, ein Modalverb, und Verbindungswörter dazwischen. Keine neue Grammatik — aber echte Zusammenhängigkeit.

## Teil 8: Sprachtonleitern

### Tonleiter 1 — Zeitwechsel in einem Satz

```
I usually walk to work, but today I'm taking the bus
I usually drink coffee, but today I'm drinking tea
I usually work in the office, but today I'm working at home
I usually wake up at seven, but today I woke up at six
I usually have dinner at home, but yesterday I had dinner at a restaurant
```

> Fühle den Kontrast: der erste Teil — Present Simple (üblich), der zweite — Present Continuous (jetzt) oder Past Simple (gestern).

### Tonleiter 2 — Verbindungswörter in der Erzählung

```
First I woke up at seven
Then I had breakfast
After that I went to work
At twelve I had lunch
After lunch I had three meetings
Finally I came home at seven
```

### Tonleiter 3 — Gegenwart → Vergangenheit → Zukunft

```
Today I am working in the office
Yesterday I worked at home
Tomorrow I am going to work in the cafe

Today she is happy
Yesterday she was tired
Tomorrow she will be busy

This week we are studying English
Last week we studied Italian
Next week we will study French
```

## Teil 9: Sprachmatrix

## Teil 10: Übungen

### Übung 1. Wähle die richtige Zeitform

1. I (work / am working) in a bank, but today I (work / am working) from home.
2. Yesterday she (goes / went) to the cinema with her friends.
3. Tomorrow we (will visit / visit) my grandmother.
4. Tom usually (drinks / is drinking) coffee, but now he (drinks / is drinking) tea.
5. Last week I (am / was) very busy.
6. Next month they (are going to move / move) to Berlin.
7. The children (are playing / play) in the garden at the moment.
8. Every morning I (have / am having) breakfast at seven.

<details>
<summary>Lösung</summary>

1. *I **work** in a bank, but today I **am working** from home.* (üblich vs. jetzt)
2. *Yesterday she **went** to the cinema with her friends.* (Past Simple)
3. *Tomorrow we **will visit** my grandmother.* (Future)
4. *Tom usually **drinks** coffee, but now he **is drinking** tea.* (üblich vs. jetzt)
5. *Last week I **was** very busy.* (Past Simple von *to be*)
6. *Next month they **are going to move** to Berlin.* (Plan — *be going to*)
7. *The children **are playing** in the garden at the moment.* (jetzt)
8. *Every morning I **have** breakfast at seven.* (üblich)

</details>

### Übung 2. Setze ein Verbindungswort ein

Aus der Liste: *first, then, after that, but, because, so, however, finally*.

1. ___ I woke up at seven. ___ I had breakfast.
2. I wanted to walk to work, ___ it was raining.
3. The film was long, ___ very interesting.
4. We were tired, ___ we went home early.
5. I love coffee, ___ I drink it every morning.
6. We had a long meeting. ___ we went out for lunch.

<details>
<summary>Lösung</summary>

1. ***First** I woke up at seven. **Then** I had breakfast.* (oder: *After that*)
2. *I wanted to walk to work, **but** it was raining.*
3. *The film was long, **but** very interesting.* (oder: *however*, aber in der Alltagssprache häufiger *but*)
4. *We were tired, **so** we went home early.*
5. *I love coffee, **so** I drink it every morning.* (oder in umgekehrter Form: *because*)
6. *We had a long meeting. **After that** we went out for lunch.* (oder: *Then*; *Finally*)

</details>

### Übung 3. Übersetze eine kurze Erzählung

> Ich heiße Ivan. Ich wohne in Hamburg. Ich arbeite in einer großen Firma. Normalerweise stehe ich um sieben auf, aber gestern bin ich um acht aufgestanden, weil Samstag war. Zum Frühstück habe ich Kaffee getrunken und Toast gegessen. Nach dem Frühstück bin ich in den Park gegangen. Das Wetter war wunderschön. Am Abend habe ich Freunde in einem Café getroffen. Heute bin ich müde, aber morgen habe ich frei. Ich werde den ganzen Tag ein Buch lesen.

<details>
<summary>Musterübersetzung</summary>

*My name is Ivan. I live in Hamburg. I work in a big company. I usually get up at seven, but yesterday I got up at eight because it was Saturday. For breakfast I drank coffee and ate toast. After breakfast I went to the park. The weather was beautiful. In the evening I met friends in a cafe. Today I am tired, but tomorrow is my day off. I am going to read a book all day.*

(Wortwahl und Reihenfolge dürfen variieren — wichtig sind die richtigen Zeitformen und Verbindungswörter.)

</details>

### Übung 4. Finde den Fehler und korrigiere

1. Yesterday I am going to the cinema.
2. He usually is drinking coffee in the morning.
3. Last week I am very busy.
4. Tomorrow we go to Berlin.
5. I am working in a bank since five years.
6. She speaks always slowly.

<details>
<summary>Lösung</summary>

1. ***Yesterday I went to the cinema.*** (Past Simple, nicht Present Continuous)
2. ***He usually drinks coffee in the morning.*** (Present Simple für Routinen, nicht Continuous)
3. ***Last week I was very busy.*** (Past Simple von *to be*)
4. ***Tomorrow we are going to Berlin.*** (oder: *we will go to Berlin* / *we're going to go to Berlin* — Zukunft, nicht Present Simple)
5. ***I have worked in a bank for five years.*** (das ist schon Present Perfect — Block 3; auf A2-Maximalvariante: *I work in a bank. I started five years ago.*)
6. ***She always speaks slowly.*** (Häufigkeitsadverb — vor dem Vollverb)

</details>

### Übung 5. Schreib deinen eigenen Text

Schreib einen kurzen Text (8–10 Sätze) zu einem der Themen:

- Mein normaler Arbeitstag und was ich gestern gemacht habe.
- Mein Leben jetzt und meine Pläne für den nächsten Monat.
- Eine Reise, die mir in Erinnerung geblieben ist.

Verwende: **mindestens** Present Simple, **mindestens** Past Simple, **mindestens** ein Verbindungswort (*first, then, but, because*), **mindestens** einen Plan (*will / going to*).

<details>
<summary>Beispiel</summary>

*I'm a teacher. I usually start my day at seven. First I drink a coffee, then I take the metro to school. I work from eight to four every day. Yesterday was a special day because we had a school party. The children sang songs and we ate cake. I was tired but happy. Next weekend I'm going to visit my parents in the countryside. I love going there because it's very quiet.*

</details>

## Spickzettel zur Lektion — Block 2 gesamt

```
A2-ZEITEN:
  Present Simple       I work, he works               üblich, Fakten
  Present Continuous   I am working, he is working    jetzt, im Prozess
  Past Simple          I worked, I went, was/were     Ereignis in der Vergangenheit
  Future will          I will work                    Entscheidungen, Prognosen
  Future going to      I am going to work             Pläne, Offensichtliches

ZEITMARKER (SIGNALE):
  every day, usually, often, always       → Present Simple
  now, at the moment, today, this week    → Present Continuous
  yesterday, last week, ago, in 1990      → Past Simple
  tomorrow, next week, in two days        → Future

MODALVERBEN (L16):
  can / must / should  + nackter Infinitiv
  ohne -s, ohne do

VERGLEICH (L17):
  kurz → -er / -est            tall → taller → tallest
  lang → more / most           interesting → more interesting → most
  good → better → best         bad → worse → worst
  as … as                       Gleichheit

PRÄPOSITIONEN (L18):
  IN  groß (Jahr, Monat, Land, Stadt)
  ON  mittel (Tag, Datum, Straße)
  AT  Punkt (Stunde, Adresse, Ereignis)

ADVERBIEN (L19):
  Häufigkeit — vor Vollverb, nach to be
  always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never

ARTIKEL (L7, L14) — Zusammenfassung:
  a/an   Ersterwähnung, eins von vielen
  the    konkret, beiden bekannt
  nichts allgemeine Plurale, Unzählbares

VERBINDUNGSWÖRTER:
  first, then, after that, finally       Reihenfolge
  but, however, although                  Kontrast
  because, so, that's why                 Grund
  and, also, too, besides                 Ergänzung
```
