# Text zu Lektion 36: The missing keys
**Thema: Deduktion in Gegenwart und Vergangenheit — Suche nach den verschwundenen Schlüsseln**

---

## Text zum Hören und Wiederholen

1. I can't find my keys anywhere.
2. They must be somewhere in the house.
3. I might have left them on the kitchen table.
4. No, they aren't on the table.
5. They could be in my jacket pocket.
6. They aren't there either.
7. Wait, I might have dropped them in the car.
8. The car is locked, so they can't be inside.
9. Someone must have moved them.
10. My sister might have taken them by mistake.
11. She can't have taken them — she's been at school all day.
12. Maybe I left them at the office yesterday.
13. That can't be right — I drove home, so I must have had them.
14. I must have put them somewhere strange.
15. People often do that when they are tired.
16. I should have hung them on the hook by the door.
17. I shouldn't have just thrown them down.
18. Wait, I hear something.
19. The dog is playing with something on the floor.
20. He must have found my keys under the sofa.
21. He must have been hiding them all morning.
22. I should have looked there first.
23. The dog seems proud of himself.
24. He may think he has done a good thing.
25. Next time, I'll put my keys in a fixed spot.
26. I should have done that years ago.
27. A good habit could have saved me an hour today.
28. Now I have to leave or I'll be late.
29. The meeting must have started already.
30. I'll just tell them the dog had my keys.
