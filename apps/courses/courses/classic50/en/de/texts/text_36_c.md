# Text zu Lektion 36: A detective at the scene
**Thema: Dialog zweier Detektive — Vermutungen über das Geschehene**

---

## Text zum Hören und Wiederholen

1. Anna, look at this room. What happened here?
2. Someone must have broken in through the back window.
3. The glass is on the inside, so the thief must have come from outside.
4. The drawers are open. He must have been looking for something specific.
5. He can't have been here long — the coffee on the table is still warm.
6. He might have left just before we arrived.
7. We should have come ten minutes earlier.
8. Look, the safe is still closed. He can't have known the code.
9. Or he might not have had time to open it.
10. The dog is sleeping. He must know the person who came in.
11. The dog can't have barked, or the neighbours would have heard him.
12. A neighbour might have seen something through the window.
13. We should have asked them already.
14. It can't have been a random burglar — they don't usually choose this kind of house.
15. He must have been looking for a specific document.
16. The owner may have hidden it somewhere clever.
17. Wait, what is this on the floor?
18. It looks like a button from a coat.
19. He must have lost it as he was leaving.
20. The button is old, so the coat must have been old too.
21. He may have been an older man.
22. Or he could have been wearing his father's coat.
23. Let's check the cameras outside.
24. They might have recorded him.
25. We should have looked at them first.
26. Yes, look — at three a.m., a tall man walks past.
27. He must be our suspect.
28. The owner can't have been at home, or he would have seen him.
29. We can't have all the answers yet, but this is a good start.
30. By tomorrow morning, we must have a clearer picture.
