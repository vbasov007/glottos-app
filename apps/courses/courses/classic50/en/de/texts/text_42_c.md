# Text zu Lektion 42: Two emails — formal vs informal
**Thema: Eine Nachricht, zwei Register — Kunde vs Kollege**

---

## Hörtext

1. Anna had to send two messages on the same day.
2. The first one was for a client, Mr Brown.
3. The second one was for her colleague, Tom.
4. The content was the same, but the words were different.
5. To Mr Brown, she began very politely.
6. She wrote that she was writing to inform him of a delay.
7. The team had decided to postpone the product launch.
8. The reason was a technical issue raised by quality control.
9. The engineers would investigate the matter this week.
10. They would conduct a thorough review.
11. She promised to submit the findings by Friday.
12. She said she regretted the inconvenience.
13. She ended with kind regards and her full name.
14. The email sounded formal, careful, and precise.
15. Then Anna wrote to Tom in a very different voice.
16. She started with a simple hi and his first name.
17. She told him they were going to put off the launch.
18. She said the engineers were looking into a small issue.
19. They would go over everything and sort it out by Friday.
20. She added that she was sorry for the mess.
21. She finished with a quick cheers and her name.
22. Both messages reached their readers within minutes.
23. Mr Brown wrote back the same afternoon.
24. He thanked Anna for the prompt notification.
25. He confirmed that the new schedule was acceptable.
26. Tom replied with a thumbs up and a joke about coffee.
27. Anna smiled at her screen and stretched.
28. Two emails, one meaning, two different worlds.
29. This was the daily art of working across registers.
30. Every advanced speaker has to master it.
