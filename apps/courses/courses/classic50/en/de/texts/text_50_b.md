# Text B zu Lektion 50: Idiome und kulturelle Einheiten in lebendiger Rede
**Thema: Finaler Durchlauf durch idiomatisches Englisch**

---

## Text zum Hören und Wiederholen

1. When I finally got the job offer, I was over the moon.
2. The interview had been tough, and I had passed by the skin of my teeth.
3. My competitor for the role had been the elephant in the room all week.
4. I had to bite the bullet and ask my boss directly about the salary.
5. Honestly, the new role is a blessing in disguise.
6. I would never have applied if my old company hadn't cut corners.
7. Their decision to lay off the senior team was a colossal failure.
8. To add insult to injury, they did it just before the holidays.
9. So I had to think outside the box and look for something completely new.
10. I started networking, even though it felt awkward at first.
11. Eventually I made up my mind to take the plunge.
12. I applied for ten positions in one weekend, just to keep my chin up.
13. The first few rejections felt like Waterloo, but I kept going.
14. Then this opportunity appeared, almost once in a blue moon.
15. The hiring manager and I were on the same page from the start.
16. We talked for two hours and he hit the nail on the head about my goals.
17. He said the team needed someone willing to take advantage of every opportunity.
18. That kind of language is rare, and it really made a difference for me.
19. The salary offer was great, but I needed to bear in mind the long-term benefits.
20. I had to think it through and not let my emotions cloud my judgment.
21. After a day of weighing options, I called him back to accept.
22. He was thrilled and said they would roll out the red carpet for me.
23. My first day at the new company was a piece of cake.
24. The team welcomed me warmly, and the work culture was a breath of fresh air.
25. My old colleagues say they are in the same boat now, all looking for new jobs.
26. I tell them to keep their chin up and to learn from my experience.
27. Sometimes when one door closes, another one opens — even if not right away.
28. Looking back, the layoff was actually the best thing that could have happened.
29. It pushed me out of my comfort zone and made me grow in unexpected ways.
30. As they say, every cloud has a silver lining — even the dark ones.
