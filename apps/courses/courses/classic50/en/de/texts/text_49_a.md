# Text A zu Lektion 49: Britisch und Amerikanisch in Aktion
**Thema: Eine Geschichte — zwei Versionen. BrE und AmE im lebendigen Kontext**

---

## Text zum Hören und Wiederholen

1. Last summer I went on holiday to London.
2. I stayed in a small flat near the centre of the city.
3. Every morning I took the underground to visit museums.
4. The pavements were crowded but everyone queued politely.
5. I bought biscuits and sweets from a small shop nearby.
6. The chemist's was very helpful when I felt unwell.
7. I posted some letters and a few postcards to friends.
8. The autumn weather arrived early that year.
9. I had to buy a thicker jumper for the cool evenings.
10. The lift in my building was old but reliable.
11. Then I flew to New York for a different kind of trip.
12. I rented an apartment downtown for two weeks.
13. The subway was fast but the sidewalks were even more crowded.
14. People stood in line at the ATM for ten or fifteen minutes.
15. I bought cookies and candy at a small store on the corner.
16. The drugstore had everything you could possibly need.
17. I mailed some postcards to friends back in Germany.
18. The fall colors in Central Park were absolutely stunning.
19. I had to buy a warm sweater because the wind was sharp.
20. The elevator in my building was modern and very fast.
21. Both trips were amazing, but the contrast was striking.
22. The British seemed quieter and more reserved at first.
23. Americans were louder, friendlier, and faster in conversation.
24. Even the food labels were spelled differently in each country.
25. In London I saw "colour" and "centre" on every sign.
26. In New York the same words were "color" and "center."
27. I learned that English is not one language but many.
28. Each variety has its own charm and its own logic.
29. The grammar is mostly the same, but the details differ.
30. Knowing both varieties is what real fluency looks like today.
