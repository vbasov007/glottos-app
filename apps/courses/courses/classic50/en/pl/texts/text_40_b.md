# Tekst do lekcji 40: An academic discussion about technology and society
**Temat: Mowa akademicka — długie zdania z konektorami dyskursywnymi**

---

## Tekst do słuchania i powtarzania

1. Technology has changed our society in ways that no one could have predicted fifty years ago.
2. Although the benefits are obvious, the disadvantages are often overlooked.
3. Moreover, the speed of change has made it difficult for ordinary people to adapt.
4. For instance, jobs that existed when our parents were young have completely disappeared.
5. As a result, many workers have had to learn new skills several times in their lives.
6. Some experts argue that, despite these difficulties, society is better off overall.
7. They point out that life expectancy has risen and that poverty has fallen worldwide.
8. However, others claim that these improvements have not been distributed fairly.
9. While the rich have benefited enormously, the poor have seen smaller gains.
10. In other words, the gap between the wealthy and the poor has widened.
11. Furthermore, problems that did not exist before have appeared.
12. Mental health issues, which were rarely discussed in the past, are now common.
13. Studies suggest that social media, which connects people in many ways, may also isolate them.
14. Although researchers have not reached a consensus, the evidence is concerning.
15. Therefore, governments are starting to consider how technology should be regulated.
16. Some countries have already passed laws that limit how data can be collected.
17. Other countries, by contrast, have taken a more relaxed approach.
18. It remains to be seen which approach will work best in the long term.
19. If history is any guide, regulation usually arrives too late to prevent harm.
20. Nevertheless, doing nothing is not an option.
21. The challenges that we face are global, and they require global solutions.
22. International cooperation, which has often been promised but rarely delivered, is essential.
23. For instance, climate change cannot be solved by one country acting alone.
24. Similarly, the regulation of artificial intelligence requires shared standards.
25. Critics argue that countries are too divided to cooperate meaningfully.
26. Supporters reply that we have no choice if we want to avoid disaster.
27. In conclusion, the question is not whether technology will continue to change society, but how we will choose to respond.
28. If we act wisely, the next fifty years could be the best in human history.
29. If we fail to act, we may face problems that no generation has ever faced before.
30. The decisions that we make today will shape the world that our children will inherit.
