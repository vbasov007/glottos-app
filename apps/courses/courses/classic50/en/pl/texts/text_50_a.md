# Tekst A do lekcji 50: Pięćdziesiąt lekcji w jednej opowieści
**Temat: Integracyjna narracja — droga od pierwszego „hello" do C1**

---

## Tekst do słuchania i powtarzania

1. Three years ago I decided to learn English seriously.
2. At first I didn't know anything beyond a few simple words.
3. I couldn't even introduce myself without making mistakes.
4. The first lesson taught me how to say hello and goodbye.
5. Then I learned the simple present tense and basic word order.
6. The fixed word order felt strange after the freedom of Russian.
7. Articles were the worst, and they are still the worst today.
8. There is no Russian equivalent for the choice between a, the, and nothing.
9. I had to train my brain to ask "which article" every single time.
10. Then came the continuous tense, which Russian simply doesn't have.
11. Past simple followed, and with it the irregular verbs that haunt every learner.
12. By the time I reached block three, I felt that I could express most things.
13. The present perfect, however, was a brand new concept for me.
14. It took weeks before the difference between past simple and present perfect clicked.
15. I learned to see the perfect as a way of looking, not as a form.
16. After that, conditionals and the passive opened up complex sentences.
17. Reported speech showed me how to backshift tenses systematically.
18. The fourth block closed the grammar completely.
19. By the end of lesson forty, the entire grammatical system was in my hands.
20. The fifth block changed direction completely.
21. Instead of more grammar, we worked on phrasal verbs and idioms.
22. I learned that the same idea can sound formal or casual depending on the words I choose.
23. Register became a conscious tool rather than a mystery.
24. Word formation taught me to grow my vocabulary on my own.
25. Discourse markers gave my speech the connective tissue it needed.
26. Pronunciation work helped me understand fast speech for the first time.
27. The differences between British and American English stopped feeling confusing.
28. Now, as the course ends, I can read, listen, write, and speak with confidence.
29. I have a long road ahead, but I have all the tools I need.
30. The course is over, but the language is just beginning to feel like home.
