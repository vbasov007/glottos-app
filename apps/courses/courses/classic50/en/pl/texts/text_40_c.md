# Tekst do lekcji 40: A long conversation about a difficult decision
**Temat: Dialog dwojga ludzi — dyskusja o życiowym wyborze z długimi replikami**

---

## Tekst do słuchania i powtarzania

1. So, what did your sister finally decide about the offer she had been thinking about?
2. She told me yesterday that she had decided to accept it, although she still has doubts.
3. I thought she would refuse, since she was worried about leaving the city where she grew up.
4. Yes, that was the main concern, but she said she had realised that opportunities like this don't come often.
5. If she had refused, she might have regretted it for the rest of her life.
6. Exactly. She kept saying that, although she loved her current job, she felt she had stopped growing.
7. What did your parents say when she told them?
8. They were surprised at first, but once she explained why she had made the decision, they understood.
9. My father even said that, if he had been her age, he would have taken a similar chance.
10. That's nice. So when does she start the new job, which is in London, I think?
11. Yes, London. She starts in six weeks, which gives her enough time to find a place to live.
12. Has she already started looking at apartments?
13. She's been browsing online, but she hasn't visited any in person yet because she doesn't want to fly to London just for that.
14. I think she might be worrying too much about the practical details.
15. The main thing is that she has made the decision, and now she has to commit to it fully.
16. You're right. If she keeps doubting herself, she'll make the transition harder than it needs to be.
17. Did she tell you what the company offered in terms of relocation support?
18. Yes — they're covering moving costs and three months of temporary housing.
19. That's generous. Most companies don't provide that kind of support anymore.
20. Especially for someone who isn't even at the senior level yet.
21. Although she said the salary was the most important factor, I think she's also excited about the city.
22. London is exciting, but it's also expensive, so she'll need to budget carefully.
23. She knows that, and she said she had been saving for a long time, just in case an opportunity came up.
24. Smart. If she hadn't been saving, the move would be much more stressful.
25. What about you? Have you ever thought about making a big change like that?
26. I have, although I've never had the courage to act on it.
27. Sometimes I wonder if I should leave my current job, but every time I'm about to decide, something holds me back.
28. Maybe seeing your sister take the risk will inspire you.
29. Maybe. If she succeeds, which I'm sure she will, perhaps I'll feel braver too.
30. We'll see. For now, let's just hope that everything goes well for her in London.
