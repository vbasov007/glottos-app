# Tekst do lekcji 29: A small antique shop
**Temat: połączenie złożonych grup rzeczownikowych i zdań względnych**

---

## Tekst do słuchania i powtarzania

1. There is a small antique shop on the narrow street where I live.
2. The shop, which has been there for over fifty years, is run by an old gentleman.
3. The owner, whose name is Mr Henry, knows the history of every object he sells.
4. The shop window is full of strange, dusty, fascinating things.
5. In the corner stands a tall old wooden clock with a golden face.
6. The clock, which was made in Vienna in 1880, still keeps perfect time.
7. On a small round table lies a delicate silver tea set.
8. The tea set, which belonged to a Russian noble family, has six tiny cups.
9. Above the door hangs a large dark oil painting of a stormy sea.
10. The painting was made by an unknown artist whose name is now lost.
11. In a glass cabinet there is a collection of old silver coins.
12. The coins, which come from many different countries, tell stories of trade and travel.
13. On the floor stands a heavy wooden chest with thick iron bands.
14. The chest, which once contained a sailor's possessions, smells of distant oceans.
15. Mr Henry has a small leather notebook in which he writes the story of each item.
16. The history of every object, he says, is what gives it real value.
17. Many people come into the shop just to look and listen.
18. The conversations that happen here are often the best part of the visit.
19. Last week a young woman bought a small Italian silver mirror.
20. The mirror, which was made in Venice three hundred years ago, was a wedding gift for her grandmother.
21. Her grandmother, who had lost the original mirror during the war, will be very surprised.
22. The young woman, whose eyes were full of tears, paid more than she had planned.
23. But the joy that the gift will bring is priceless.
24. Mr Henry says that every good object eventually finds the right owner.
25. The shop is like a quiet bridge between past and present.
26. Each item that leaves the shop carries history into a new home.
27. I often walk past the window just to look at the beautiful old things.
28. The whole street, which is full of small independent shops, has its own slow rhythm.
29. In a world that moves too fast, this little shop is a small island of memory.
30. The kindness, the patience, and the love of beauty that I find here are rare and precious.
