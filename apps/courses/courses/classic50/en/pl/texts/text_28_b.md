# Tekst do lekcji 28: The book club
**Temat: mieszanka defining i non-defining, opis osób i idei**

---

## Tekst do słuchania i powtarzania

1. Last year I joined a book club that meets once a month.
2. The club, which started ten years ago, has about twelve members.
3. Most of the people who attend are over forty.
4. The lady who founded the club is a retired professor of literature.
5. Her name is Margaret, and she chooses the books that we read.
6. The book that we are discussing this month is a novel by a Russian author.
7. The author, whose first novel won a major prize, is now very famous.
8. The story takes place in a small town where almost nothing ever happens.
9. The main character is a quiet man who works in a library.
10. The library, which is described in detail, becomes almost a character itself.
11. There is a young woman whom the main character secretly loves.
12. She, however, is engaged to a man who is rich and powerful.
13. The reader, who slowly understands the truth, feels deep sympathy for the hero.
14. The ending, which I won't reveal, is both sad and beautiful.
15. Our discussions, which usually last two hours, are full of strong opinions.
16. Some members love books that have happy endings.
17. Others prefer stories that leave questions unanswered.
18. The conversations that we have often go beyond the book.
19. We talk about ideas that connect to our own lives.
20. Margaret, whose knowledge is enormous, guides every discussion gently.
21. She never imposes the opinion that she holds herself.
22. Last month we read a book that nobody liked.
23. The author, who is very popular, has many readers around the world.
24. But the book, which is over four hundred pages long, felt empty to us.
25. The meeting where we discussed it was unusually short.
26. Next month we will read a memoir by a journalist whose career spans five continents.
27. He writes about places where he risked his life.
28. The questions that his book raises are very serious.
29. I am looking forward to the discussion that will follow.
30. Books that make you think are the books that truly matter.
