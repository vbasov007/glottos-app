# Test for Lesson 46: Verbal periphrases

**How do you say it in Spanish?**
Translate the phrases into Spanish. Use the grammar and vocabulary from the lesson. Each example corresponds to one specific periphrasis from the lesson.

---

1. I just arrived.
2. I'm going to call him tomorrow.
3. I'm still working at that bank.
4. I've been studying Spanish for three years.
5. I'm reading this book again.
6. He quit smoking a year ago.
7. It's about to rain.
8. I'm eating right now — don't bother me.
9. She suddenly burst into tears.
10. I'm gradually getting it.
11. You should rest.
12. I have to study.
13. He must be at home.
14. They just left.
15. The rain has stopped.
16. I've been waiting for twenty minutes.
17. Are you still living in Madrid?
18. I haven't smoked for two weeks.
19. He started crying.
20. I don't want to see him again.
21. I've read a hundred pages (so far).
22. I have dinner ready.
23. He went so far as to become president.
24. I was going to leave when the phone rang.
25. We've been working together for five years.
26. He goes around talking nonsense.
27. I've been saying this for months. (retrospective)
28. I must confess that I don't know. (formal)
29. Let's start.
30. I don't live here anymore.
