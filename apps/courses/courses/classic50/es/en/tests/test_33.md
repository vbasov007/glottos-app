# Test for Lesson 33: Subjunctive after emotion and value judgment

**How do you say it in Spanish?**
Translate the phrases into Spanish. Use the grammar and vocabulary from the lesson. We're drilling the triggers: emotions (*me alegro de que, siento que, me gusta que…*), value judgments (*es bueno que, es una pena que…*), and live reactions (*¡qué pena que…!, ¡qué bien que…!*). Don't forget the two-subjects rule: one subject → infinitive, two subjects → *que* + subjuntivo.

---

1. I'm glad that you're here.
2. I'm glad to be here.
3. I'm sorry that you can't come.
4. I'm sorry I can't come.
5. It's good that you study.
6. It's a pity that it's raining.
7. How great that you're calling!
8. What a pity that they're leaving!
9. I like that you sing.
10. I like to sing.
11. It annoys me that he's late every day.
12. It's strange that María hasn't come yet.
13. It surprises me that you know that.
14. It's normal for a baby to cry.
15. I'm really glad that we're all together.
16. I'm afraid that they won't make it in time.
17. I'm afraid of being late.
18. How awful that this happened!
19. It's better that you rest.
20. It's sad that our friends are leaving.
21. I know that Carmen is coming. (just reporting a fact!)
22. I'm glad that Carmen is coming. (reacting!)
23. It's true that he's sick. (asserting a fact!)
24. It's a pity that he's sick. (evaluating!)
25. How strange that he isn't answering!
26. It makes me sad that you're so tired.
27. It's surprising that they won.
28. It's logical that she's angry.
29. How great that we have time!
30. It's a pity that you can't come, but I'm glad we talked.

1. *Me alegro de que estés aquí.*

9. *Me gusta que cantes.*
10. *Me gusta cantar.*
11. *Me molesta que llegue tarde todos los días.*
12. *Es extraño que María todavía no haya llegado.* (or with presente: *Es extraño que María todavía no llegue.*)
13. *Me sorprende que lo sepas.*
14. *Es normal que el niño llore.*
15. *Me alegro mucho de que estemos todos juntos.*
16. *Tengo miedo de que no lleguen a tiempo.* (or: *Temo que no lleguen a tiempo.*)
17. *Tengo miedo de llegar tarde.* (or: *Temo llegar tarde.*)
18. *¡Qué horror que haya pasado esto!*
19. *Es mejor que descanses.*
20. *Es triste que nuestros amigos se vayan.*
21. *Sé que Carmen viene.* (indicativo — reporting a fact!)
22. *Me alegro de que Carmen venga.* (subjuntivo — reaction!)
23. *Es verdad que está enfermo.* (indicativo — assertion!)
24. *Es una pena que esté enfermo.* (subjuntivo — evaluation!)
25. *¡Qué raro que no conteste!*
26. *Me da pena que estés tan cansado.* (or: *Es triste que estés tan cansado.*)
27. *Es sorprendente que hayan ganado.* (or: *Me sorprende que hayan ganado.*)
28. *Es lógico que esté enfadada.*
29. *¡Qué bien que tengamos tiempo!* (or: *¡Qué bueno que tengamos tiempo!*)
30. *Es una pena que no puedas venir, pero me alegro de que hayamos hablado.*
