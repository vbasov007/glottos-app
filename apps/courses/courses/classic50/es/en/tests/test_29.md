# Test for Lesson 29: Affirmative imperative

**How do you say it in Spanish?**
Translate the phrases into Spanish. Use the grammar and vocabulary from the lesson. If a form is given in parentheses, use exactly that form (tú / usted / vosotros / ustedes / nosotros).

---

1. Speak! (tú)
2. Eat! (tú)
3. Write! (tú)
4. Listen! (tú)
5. Look! (tú)
6. Come here! (tú, from *venir*)
7. Tell me the truth. (tú, from *decir*)
8. Get out of the room! (tú, from *salir*)
9. Do the homework! (tú, from *hacer*)
10. Be careful! (tú, from *tener cuidado*)
11. Go home! (tú, from *ir*)
12. Put this on the table. (tú, from *poner*)
13. Be good! (tú, from *ser*)
14. Call me! (tú, with pronoun)
15. Tell it to me! (tú, with two pronouns)
16. Buy it for me! (tú, with two pronouns)
17. Get up! (tú, from *levantarse*)
18. Wait, please. (usted)
19. Open the book. (usted)
20. Close the door, please. (usted)
21. Tell me your name. (usted)
22. Come in, please. (usted, from *pasar* / *entrar*)
23. Turn right at the corner. (tú)
24. Go straight ahead to the traffic light. (tú)
25. Cross the street. (tú)
26. Chop the onion and fry it. (tú)
27. Add salt and taste the soup. (tú)
28. Let's begin! (nosotros)
29. Let's go! / Let's get out of here! (nosotros, from *irse*)
30. Kids, come here and sit down! (vosotros, from *venir* and *sentarse*)
