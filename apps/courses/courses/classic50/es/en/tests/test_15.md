# Test for Lesson 15: Direct object pronouns (me, te, lo, la, nos, os, los, las)

**How do you say it in Spanish?**
Translate the phrases into Spanish. Use the grammar and vocabulary from the lesson: CD pronouns, their position before the conjugated verb or attached to the infinitive/gerund, *lo neutro*, personal *a*.

---

1. I see you.
2. I don't see him.
3. We know her.
4. They're waiting for us.
5. Do you hear me?
6. I'm inviting them (men) to dinner.
7. I'm inviting them (women) to dinner.
8. I'm sorry.
9. I don't know (that).
10. I understand (it).
11. Buy it! (the book)
12. Don't buy it!
13. Call me tomorrow.
14. Don't call me today.
15. I want to see her. (two options)
16. I'm going to call him. (two options)
17. We can't help you. (you = vosotros)
18. I'm listening to it. (la música)
19. I'm looking at you.
20. We're looking for them. (las llaves)
21. Do you know María? — Yes, I know her.
22. Are you buying the newspaper? — No, I'm not buying it.
23. Are you waiting for us? — Yes, I'm waiting for you.
24. I need my glasses. — I can't see them.
25. I'm bringing a gift. — Give it to me! (= ¡Dámelo!)
26. Who is calling me?
27. I love you.
28. What matters is family. (lo + adj.)
29. I always understand her.
30. This movie? — I watch it every week.
