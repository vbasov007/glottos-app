# Test for Lesson 27: Two futures — *ir a* + infinitive and Futuro Simple

**How do you say it in Spanish?**
Translate the phrases into Spanish. Use the grammar and vocabulary from the lesson: *ir a* + infinitive for immediate plans, Futuro Simple for predictions, promises, and the more distant future. Remember the 12 irregular stems.

---

1. I'm going to eat now.
2. Are you going to leave now?
3. We're going to learn Spanish.
4. Look at the sky — it's about to rain.
5. I'll call you tonight.
6. Tomorrow I'll talk to the teacher.
7. Next week we'll go to Madrid.
8. What are you going to do on Saturday?
9. Next year they will live in Barcelona.
10. I promise, I'll tell you the truth.
11. I'll do it tomorrow.
12. In two days he'll come.
13. We'll have a lot of work.
14. Will you be able to come?
15. I'll leave the house at eight.
16. Someday we'll know the truth.
17. The day after tomorrow she'll write a letter.
18. Next month I'll be in Seville.
19. A year from now I'll speak Spanish well.
20. They'll want to see the photos.
21. I wonder where Juan is. — He's probably at home.
22. What time is it? — It must be around five.
23. He must be about thirty.
24. Soon we'll know the answer.
25. I'll put the books on the table.
26. You (pl.) will do the exercise tomorrow.
27. My brother will arrive next Saturday.
28. How much will it cost?
29. What are you going to do tonight? — I'm going to rest.
30. Next weekend we'll go to the sea and we'll swim.
