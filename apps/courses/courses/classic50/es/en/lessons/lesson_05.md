# Lesson 5: The verb *estar*. Location and temporary states
**Vocabulary: prepositions of place, feelings and physical states, rooms of a house**

---

## How to work with this lesson

1. **Read** — get the rule (5 minutes)
2. **Say it out loud** — all six forms of *<say>estar</say>* in a row, like a mantra
3. **Run the matrix** — yo → tú → él → nosotros → vosotros → ellos, until it flies out on its own

> *<say>Ser</say>* = who you are. *<say>Estar</say>* = where you are and how you feel right now. Two different verbs, two different jobs. Don't mix them up.

---

## Part 1: Why Spanish needs a second "to be"

English uses one verb — *to be* — for everything: "I am American," "I am tired," "I am in the kitchen." Spanish splits that job in two. In Lesson 4 you met **ser** (the identity verb: who? from where? what profession?). Now meet **estar** — the **location and state** verb.

| Job | Verb | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| Who you are by nature | *<say>ser</say>* | *<say>Soy médico.</say>* — I'm a doctor. |
| Where you're from | *<say>ser</say>* | *<say>Soy de Estados Unidos.</say>* — I'm from the US. |
| **Where you are right now** | ***<say>estar</say>*** | ***<say>Estoy en casa.</say>*** — I'm at home. |
| **How you feel right now** | ***<say>estar</say>*** | ***<say>Estoy cansado.</say>*** — I'm tired. |

> **The principle:** if the English "am/is/are" can be paraphrased as "is located" or "is feeling" → *<say>estar</say>*. If it's "is by nature / by identity" → *<say>ser</say>*. Full *<say>ser</say> vs <say>estar</say>* showdown comes in Lesson 9.

## Part 2: Conjugating *estar* in the present

| Person | Form | Pronunciation |
|--------|------|---------------|
| <say>yo</say> | **<say>estoy</say>** | es-TOY |
| <say>tú</say> | **<say>estás</say>** | es-TAHS |
| <say>él</say> / <say>ella</say> / <say>usted</say> | **<say>está</say>** | es-TAH |
| <say>nosotros</say> / <say>nosotras</say> | **<say>estamos</say>** | es-TAH-mos |
| <say>vosotros</say> / <say>vosotras</say> | **<say>estáis</say>** | es-TAH-eess |
| <say>ellos</say> / <say>ellas</say> / <say>ustedes</say> | **<say>están</say>** | es-TAHN |

> **Five tildes out of six forms.** The only form without a tilde is *<say>estoy</say>* (it ends in *-y*, so the *aguda* stress is already obvious). The other five — *<say>estás</say>, <say>está</say>, <say>estamos</say>, <say>estáis</say>, <say>están</say>* — all need a tilde because the stress is on the last syllable AND the word ends in a vowel / -s / -n.
> Connect this back to Lesson 1: *aguda* + vowel/n/s ending = tilde required. *<say>Estar</say>*'s conjugation is a tilde drill in disguise.

**Trap:** don't confuse *<say>está</say>* (he/she is located) with *esta* without a tilde (this — demonstrative, Lesson 14). One tiny tilde, totally different word.

---

## Part 3: *Estar* for location — its main job

**Where something or someone is located — always *<say>estar</say>*.** No exceptions.

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| *<say>Estoy en Madrid.</say>* | I'm in Madrid. |
| *<say>El libro está sobre la mesa.</say>* | The book is on the table. |
| *<say>Mis padres están en casa.</say>* | My parents are at home. |
| *<say>¿Dónde estás?</say>* | Where are you? |
| *<say>Estamos en el parque.</say>* | We're at the park. |

> **Burn this in:** even permanent locations take *<say>estar</say>*. The Eiffel Tower has been in Paris for 130+ years, yet: *<say>La Torre Eiffel está en París.</say>* — never *<say>es</say>*. This is the #1 mistake English speakers make, because in English we just say "Paris IS in France" with no distinction.

**Contrast with *<say>ser de</say>* (origin):** *<say>Soy de México</say>* (I'm from Mexico — origin → *<say>ser</say>*) vs *<say>Estoy en México</say>* (I'm in Mexico — current location → *<say>estar</say>*).

## Part 4: *Estar* for temporary states

If the state **could change by tomorrow**, it's *<say>estar</say>*.

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| *<say>Estoy cansado.</say>* | I'm tired. |
| *<say>María está enferma.</say>* | María is sick. |
| *<say>Estamos contentos.</say>* | We're happy. |
| *<say>Los niños están tranquilos.</say>* | The kids are calm (right now). |
| *<say>¿Cómo estás?</say>* | How are you? |
| *<say>Estoy bien, gracias.</say>* | I'm fine, thanks. |

**Agreement:** the adjective after *<say>estar</say>* matches the subject's gender and number (same as with *<say>ser</say>*, Lesson 4). *<say>Cansado</say> / <say>cansada</say> / <say>cansados</say> / <say>cansadas</say>.* A man says *<say>Estoy cansado</say>*; a woman says *<say>Estoy cansada</say>*.

### Ser vs estar with adjectives: meaning shift (preview)

A few adjectives flip meaning depending on which "to be" you use:

| Adjective | *<say>ser</say>* (permanent trait) | *<say>estar</say>* (current state) |
|-----------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **<say>bueno</say>** | a good person | tasty / healthy right now |
| **<say>listo</say>** | smart, clever | ready |
| **<say>aburrido</say>** | boring (a bore) | bored right now |
| **<say>rico</say>** | rich (wealthy) | delicious (of food) |

> *<say>Juan es aburrido</say>* — Juan is boring (personality). *<say>Juan está aburrido</say>* — Juan is bored (right now). Same word, different verb, different meaning. Full treatment in Lesson 9.

## Part 5: Prepositions of place

With *<say>estar</say>* you almost always need a **preposition** or **place adverb**. The core set:

| Preposition / adverb | Translation | Example |
|----------------------|-------------|---------|
| **<say>en</say>** | in / on / at | *<say>Estoy en la cocina.</say>* |
| **<say>sobre</say>** / **<say>encima de</say>** | on (top of) | *<say>El gato está sobre la mesa.</say>* |
| **<say>debajo de</say>** | under | *<say>El perro está debajo de la cama.</say>* |
| **<say>al lado de</say>** | next to | *<say>Estoy al lado de mi hermano.</say>* |
| **<say>delante de</say>** | in front of | *<say>El coche está delante de la casa.</say>* |
| **<say>detrás de</say>** | behind | *<say>El jardín está detrás de la casa.</say>* |
| **<say>entre</say>** | between | *<say>Estoy entre Ana y Pedro.</say>* |
| **<say>cerca de</say>** | near | *<say>El hotel está cerca del centro.</say>* |
| **<say>lejos de</say>** | far from | *<say>Vivo lejos de la oficina.</say>* |
| **<say>dentro de</say>** | inside | *<say>El dinero está dentro del sobre.</say>* |

Place adverbs (no preposition needed): **<say>aquí</say>** (here), **<say>ahí</say>** (there), **<say>allí</say>** (over there).

> **Article contraction:** *<say>de</say> + <say>el</say> = <say>del</say>*, *<say>a</say> + <say>el</say> = <say>al</say>* (Lesson 3). *<say>Al lado del baño</say>*, *<say>cerca del parque</say>*. Mandatory, not optional. *De el* and *a el* don't exist — they always crunch into one word.

## Vocabulary

### Feelings and states (always with *estar*)

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| <say>cansado</say> / <say>cansada</say> | tired |
| <say>contento</say> / <say>contenta</say> | happy / pleased |
| <say>triste</say> | sad |
| <say>enfermo</say> / <say>enferma</say> | sick / ill |
| <say>ocupado</say> / <say>ocupada</say> | busy |
| <say>aburrido</say> / <say>aburrida</say> | bored |
| <say>nervioso</say> / <say>nerviosa</say> | nervous / anxious |
| <say>tranquilo</say> / <say>tranquila</say> | calm / relaxed |
| <say>enfadado</say> / <say>enfadada</say> | angry / annoyed |
| <say>listo</say> / <say>lista</say> | ready (with *<say>estar</say>*!) |

> Note that *<say>triste</say>* ends in *-e* — it has only two forms (singular *<say>triste</say>*, plural *<say>tristes</say>*), it doesn't shift by gender. Same for *-ista* nouns. Adjectives ending in *-o/-a* are the four-form ones.

### Rooms and parts of a house

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| <say>la casa</say> | house / home |
| <say>la cocina</say> | kitchen |
| <say>el salón</say> | living room |
| <say>el dormitorio</say> | bedroom |
| <say>el baño</say> | bathroom |
| <say>el comedor</say> | dining room |
| <say>el pasillo</say> | hallway / corridor |
| <say>la habitación</say> | room |
| <say>el garaje</say> | garage |
| <say>el jardín</say> | garden / yard |

> Cognate gold: *<say>garaje</say>* ≈ garage, *<say>jardín</say>* ≈ garden, *<say>salón</say>* ≈ saloon/salon, *<say>comedor</say>* — the place where you *come* and eat (well, almost: *<say>comer</say>* = to eat, *-dor* = doer-place suffix).

## Language scales

Run *<say>estar</say>* through all six persons, swapping only the location or state. Speed is the point.

**Scale 1 — location:**

> *(Yo) <say>**estoy** en casa</say>.* / *(Tú) <say>**estás** en la cocina</say>.* / *<say>Él **está** en el baño</say>.*
> *<say>Nosotros **estamos** en el salón</say>.* / *<say>Vosotros **estáis** en el jardín</say>.* / *<say>Ellos **están** en el dormitorio</say>.*

**Scale 2 — state (match the adjective!):**

> *(Yo, m.) <say>**estoy cansado**</say>.* / *(Tú, f.) <say>**estás cansada**</say>.* / *<say>Ella **está contenta**</say>.*
> *<say>Nosotros **estamos ocupados**</say>.* / *<say>Vosotras **estáis tranquilas**</say>.* / *<say>Ellos **están enfermos**</say>.*

> **Target:** under 15 seconds per scale. If you stumble, repeat until it's automatic.

---

## Language matrix

Mirror principle: the answer is already encoded in the question. Switch persons without rebuilding the verb.

**You (sg.):** <say>¿Dónde **estás**?</say> — <say>**Estoy** en la cocina.</say> / <say>¿Cómo **estás** hoy?</say> — <say>**Estoy** bien, gracias. ¿Y tú?</say>

**You (pl., vosotros):** <say>¿Dónde **estáis**?</say> — <say>**Estamos** en el salón.</say> / <say>¿**Estáis** cansados?</say> — <say>Sí, **estamos** muy cansados.</say>

**Third persons:** <say>¿Dónde **están** tus padres?</say> — <say>**Están** en el jardín.</say> / <say>¿Cómo **está** tu hermana?</say> — <say>**Está** un poco enferma, pero tranquila.</say>

## Exercises

### Exercise 1. Conjugate *estar*

Fill in the right form:

1. Yo ___ en el parque.
2. Tú ___ cansada.
3. Mi madre ___ en la cocina.
4. Nosotros ___ contentos.
5. Vosotros ___ en el baño.
6. Los niños ___ tranquilos.
7. Usted ___ ocupado, ¿verdad?
8. Ustedes ___ en Madrid.

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *estoy*
2. *estás*
3. *está*
4. *estamos*
5. *estáis*
6. *están*
7. *está* (usted = 3rd person singular)
8. *están* (ustedes = 3rd person plural)

</details>

### Exercise 2. *Ser* or *estar*? — the central contrast (back-ref → Lesson 4)

Pick the right verb and put it in the right form:

1. Yo (ser / estar) ___ americano, pero ahora ___ en España.
2. Mi padre (ser / estar) ___ médico. Hoy ___ muy ocupado.
3. ¿De dónde (ser / estar) ___ vosotros? — ___ de México.
4. Los libros (ser / estar) ___ sobre la mesa.
5. María (ser / estar) ___ profesora y siempre ___ cansada los viernes.
6. La Torre Eiffel ___ en París.
7. (Nosotros) ___ estudiantes y ___ muy contentos hoy.
8. ¿Cómo ___ tú? — ___ bien, gracias.

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *soy* … *estoy* (nationality = ser; current location = estar)
2. *es* … *está* (profession = ser; temporary state = estar)
3. *sois* … *somos* (origin = ser)
4. *están* (location = estar — even for inanimate things!)
5. *es* … *está* (profession = ser; temporary state = estar)
6. *está* (location, even permanent — always estar)
7. *somos* … *estamos*
8. *estás* … *estoy* (how you feel = estar)

</details>

### Exercise 3. Place prepositions — say where things are

Translate to Spanish:

1. The book is on the table.
2. The cat is under the bed.
3. I'm next to my sister.
4. The garage is behind the house.
5. We're between the kitchen and the living room.
6. The bathroom is far from the bedroom.
7. The children are in the garden.
8. The keys (*las llaves*) are inside the bag (*la bolsa*).

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *El libro está sobre la mesa.* (or: *encima de la mesa*)
2. *El gato está debajo de la cama.*
3. *Estoy al lado de mi hermana.*
4. *El garaje está detrás de la casa.*
5. *Estamos entre la cocina y el salón.*
6. *El baño está lejos del dormitorio.* (de + el = **del**)
7. *Los niños están en el jardín.*
8. *Las llaves están dentro de la bolsa.*

</details>

### Exercise 4. Adjective agreement with *estar*

Put the adjective in the right form:

1. María está (cansado) ___.
2. Los niños están (contento) ___.
3. Mis hermanas están (enfermo) ___.
4. Nosotros (m.) estamos (ocupado) ___.
5. Vosotras estáis (tranquilo) ___.
6. Pedro y Ana están (nervioso) ___.
7. La comida está (listo) ___.
8. Tú (f.) estás (triste) ___.

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *cansada*
2. *contentos*
3. *enfermas*
4. *ocupados*
5. *tranquilas*
6. *nerviosos* (mixed group m. + f. → masculine plural)
7. *lista* (la comida is feminine)
8. *triste* (ends in -e — doesn't change for gender, only number)

</details>

### Exercise 5. Free translation (ser + estar combo)

1. I'm Marco, I'm Italian, and right now I'm in Barcelona.
2. My mom is a doctor and today she's very busy.
3. Where are your brothers? — They're in the garden.
4. This soup (*la sopa*) is delicious, but I'm not hungry (= I don't have hunger, *no tengo hambre*).
5. The bathroom is between the kitchen and the bedroom.

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *Soy Marco, soy italiano y ahora estoy en Barcelona.*
2. *Mi madre es médica y hoy está muy ocupada.*
3. *¿Dónde están tus hermanos? — Están en el jardín.*
4. *Esta sopa está muy rica, pero no tengo hambre.* (*rica* with *estar* = "delicious"!)
5. *El baño está entre la cocina y el dormitorio.*

</details>

### Mini-dialog — read both voices out loud

> — ¡Hola, Ana! ¿Dónde estás?
> — Hola. Estoy en casa, en la cocina. ¿Y tú?
> — Estoy en el trabajo. Estoy muy cansado hoy.
> — ¿Por qué? ¿Estás enfermo?
> — No, no estoy enfermo, solo ocupado. ¿Y tu familia? ¿Cómo están?
> — Mis padres están bien, gracias. Mi hermana está un poco triste, pero tranquila.
> — Vale. Hasta luego.
> — ¡Hasta pronto!

Repeat 3 times. Then close the text and retell from memory.

## Lesson cheat sheet

```
ESTAR — THE LOCATION + STATE VERB

CONJUGATION:
  yo                estoy      (no tilde!)
  tú                estás
  él/ella/usted     está
  nosotros/as       estamos
  vosotros/as       estáis
  ellos/ellas/Uds.  están

TWO JOBS:
  1. WHERE? → estar + place preposition
       Estoy en casa.
       El libro está sobre la mesa.
       (even the Eiffel Tower → está en París!)

  2. HOW? (temporary state) → estar + adjective
       Estoy cansado / contenta / enfermo.
       (the adjective agrees in gender and number!)

SER vs ESTAR — THE CORE CONTRAST:
  ser   = who/what by nature  (nationality, profession, character, origin)
  estar = where / how right now  (location, temporary state, mood)

  Soy de México.     vs  Estoy en México.
  Es médico.         vs  Está ocupado.
  María es buena.    vs  La sopa está buena.

PLACE PREPOSITIONS:
  en              in / on / at
  sobre / encima de  on (top of)
  debajo de       under
  al lado de      next to
  delante de      in front of
  detrás de       behind
  entre           between
  cerca de        near
  lejos de        far from
  dentro de       inside

  Contraction: de + el = del;  a + el = al
  (al lado del baño, cerca del parque)

STATES (always with estar):
  cansado, contento, triste, enfermo, ocupado,
  aburrido, nervioso, tranquilo, enfadado, listo

ROOMS OF A HOUSE:
  la casa, la cocina, el salón, el dormitorio,
  el baño, el comedor, el pasillo, la habitación,
  el garaje, el jardín

QUESTION PATTERNS:
  ¿Dónde estás?       — Estoy en…
  ¿Cómo estás?        — Estoy bien / cansado / contento.
  ¿Dónde está…?       — Está en / sobre / al lado de…
```
