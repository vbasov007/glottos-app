# Lesson 3: Articles — el/la/los/las, un/una/unos/unas, al/del
**Vocabulary: Places around town, days of the week, months**

---

## How to work with this lesson

1. **Read** — understand the rule (5 minutes, no more!)
2. **Run through the scale** — el/la/los/las, un/una/unos/unas on every new noun
3. **Drill until automatic** — the article should fly out **together with** the noun, not be picked separately

> Learning a Spanish noun without its article = learning half the word. Memorize **"<say>la mesa</say>"** (not "<say>mesa</say>"), **"<say>el problema</say>"** (not "<say>problema</say>"). The article is part of the entry.

---

## Part 1: Why Spanish needs an article (and English barely uses one)

English has two articles total: **the** (definite) and **a / an** (indefinite). No gender, no plural form. Spanish has **eight**: four definite + four indefinite, agreeing in **gender** *and* **number**. Spanish also uses an article in places where English drops it — *Life is beautiful* but *<say>La vida es bella</say>* (literally "**The** life is beautiful"). Don't translate article-for-article from English; learn the Spanish pattern.

| Function | Example | What it tells you |
|----------|---------|-------------------|
| Gender | *<say>el problema</say>* / *<say>la mano</say>* | masculine or feminine |
| Number | *<say>el libro</say>* / *<say>los libros</say>* | one or many |
| Definiteness | *<say>el libro</say>* vs *<say>un libro</say>* | specific or any |

---

## Part 2: The definite article — el, la, los, las

Use the definite article when the noun is **specific, known, unique**, or refers to **a whole category**.

|         | Masculine | Feminine |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Singular | <say>**el** libro</say> | <say>**la** mesa</say> |
| Plural | <say>**los** libros</say> | <say>**las** mesas</say> |

| Case | Spanish | English |
|------|---------|---------|
| Specific, already-mentioned thing | *<say>El libro está en la mesa.</say>* | The book is on the table. |
| Unique objects | *<say>el sol</say>, <say>la luna</say>, <say>el mundo</say>* | the sun, the moon, the world |
| Generalizations | *<say>Me gusta el café.</say>* | I like coffee. *(in general)* |
| Days of week ("on …") | *<say>El lunes voy al banco.</say>* | On Monday I'm going to the bank. |
| Telling time | *<say>Son las tres.</say>* | It's three o'clock. |
| Title + last name (about) | *<say>El señor García trabaja aquí.</say>* | Mr. García works here. |

> **Trap:** addressing someone directly, the article disappears: *<say>¡Buenos días, señor García!</say>* (no *<say>el</say>*).

---

## Part 3: The indefinite article — un, una, unos, unas

Use the indefinite article for **a new, non-specific, "any old one"** noun. English **a / an** maps to *<say>un</say> / <say>una</say>* — but Spanish also has a plural indefinite (*<say>unos</say> / <say>unas</say>* = "some / a few"), which English doesn't.

|         | Masculine | Feminine |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Singular | <say>**un** libro</say> | <say>**una** mesa</say> |
| Plural | <say>**unos** libros</say> | <say>**unas** mesas</say> |

- **<say>un</say> / <say>una</say>** = "a", "one (unspecified)" — *<say>Veo un parque.</say>* (I see a park.)
- **<say>unos</say> / <say>unas</say>** = "some", "a few" — *<say>Compro unos libros.</say>* (I'm buying a few books.)

### Indefinite vs definite — side by side

| Indefinite | Definite |
|------------|----------|
| *<say>Hay un banco aquí.</say>* (some bank around) | *<say>El banco está cerrado.</say>* (that bank is closed) |
| *<say>Necesito una farmacia.</say>* (any pharmacy) | *<say>La farmacia está en la calle Mayor.</say>* (the one on Calle Mayor) |

> **First-mention rule:** First time → *<say>un</say> / <say>una</say>*. Second time, now it's *that* one → *<say>el</say> / <say>la</say>*.
> *<say>Veo un perro. El perro es grande.</say>* — English does the same thing; Spanish just has four forms of each.

## Part 4: Contractions — al and del

Spanish has exactly **two** mandatory contractions, both with the masculine singular article **<say>el</say>**:

| Preposition + article | Contraction | Example |
|-----------------------|-------------|---------|
| **<say>a</say> + <say>el</say>** | **<say>al</say>** | *<say>Voy al banco.</say>* — I'm going to the bank. |
| **<say>de</say> + <say>el</say>** | **<say>del</say>** | *<say>Vengo del mercado.</say>* — I'm coming from the market. |

Always. Writing "a el" or "de el" is a mistake.

### Crucial trap — these contractions happen **ONLY with el**

With *<say>la</say>, <say>los</say>, <say>las</say>* — no contraction. Just leave the two words side by side.

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| ~~Voy a el parque~~ | *<say>Voy al parque.</say>* |
| ~~Voy a la escuela~~ | *<say>Voy a la escuela.</say>* (no contraction!) |
| ~~Vengo de el museo~~ | *<say>Vengo del museo.</say>* |
| ~~Vengo de los museos~~ | *<say>Vengo de los museos.</say>* (no contraction!) |
| ~~Hablo de las clases~~ | *<say>Hablo de las clases.</say>* (no contraction!) |

> **Flag this trap and repeat out loud:** *<say>Voy AL parque</say>* (a + el → al), but *<say>Voy A LA escuela</say>* (a + la → stays separate).

### Proper-noun exception

If **<say>El</say>** is part of a proper name (country, city, book title), no contraction:
*<say>Vengo de El Salvador</say>* (not ~~del Salvador~~); *<say>Hablo de El Quijote</say>* (not ~~del Quijote~~).

---

## Part 5: Where Spanish uses an article and English doesn't

The #1 source of mistakes for English speakers.

| Case | Spanish | English |
|------|---------|---------|
| Generalizations | *<say>Me gusta el café.</say>* | I like coffee. |
| Abstract nouns | *<say>La vida es bella.</say>* | Life is beautiful. |
| Days of week ("on …") | *<say>El lunes trabajo.</say>* | On Monday I work. |
| Days of week (every …) | *<say>Los sábados no trabajo.</say>* | On Saturdays I don't work. |
| Telling time | *<say>Son las tres.</say> / <say>A las ocho.</say>* | It's three. / At eight. |
| Unique objects | *<say>El sol, la luna, el mundo.</say>* | The sun, the moon, the world. |

For days, the article does the work of English "on". For time, it's always *<say>las</say>* (or *<say>la</say>* for "one o'clock") — *<say>hora</say>(s)* is hiding in there.

### Where Spanish drops the article (English adds one)

| Case | Spanish | English |
|------|---------|---------|
| *<say>ser</say>* + profession (no descriptor) | *<say>Soy profesor.</say>* | I'm **a** teacher. |
| Months in dates | *<say>Estamos en abril.</say>* | It's April. |
| Person's first name | *<say>María viene.</say>* | María is coming. |
| After *<say>hay</say>* | *<say>Hay una tienda. Hay tiendas.</say>* | There's a shop. There are shops. |

> ***<say>hay</say>*** means "there is / there are" — by definition a first mention. Goes with *<say>un</say>/<say>una</say>/<say>unos</say>/<say>unas</say>* or no article. Never ~~hay el / hay la~~.
>
> *<say>Soy profesor</say>* (no article) is the opposite of English "I'm **a** teacher". Add *<say>un</say>* only if you describe the profession: *<say>Soy un profesor excelente.</say>*

---

## Part 6: Special case — *el* before feminine nouns starting with stressed *a-*

If a **feminine** noun begins with a **stressed *a-*** (or stressed *ha-*), the singular uses **<say>el</say>** to avoid two *a* sounds crashing. The noun is **still feminine** — the adjective and the plural reveal the real gender.

| Singular | Plural |
|----------|--------|
| <say>**el** agua fría</say> (cold water) | <say>**las** aguas frías</say> |
| <say>**el** águila negra</say> (black eagle) | <say>**las** águilas negras</say> |
| <say>**el** hambre</say> (hunger) | — |
| <say>**el** alma</say> (soul) | <say>**las** almas</say> |

> **Not a gender change!** Notice the feminine adjective *<say>fría</say>* (not ~~frío~~) and the plural *<say>las aguas</say>*. Only applies when the *a-* is **stressed**: *<say>la amiga</say>* (friend, fem.) keeps *<say>la</say>* — stress falls on the *i*.

## Part 7: Vocabulary

### Places around town

| Masculine | English | Feminine | English |
|-----------|---------|----------|---------|
| <say>el banco</say> | the bank | <say>la calle</say> | the street |
| <say>el mercado</say> | the market | <say>la tienda</say> | the shop |
| <say>el parque</say> | the park | <say>la plaza</say> | the square |
| <say>el café</say> | the café | <say>la farmacia</say> | the pharmacy |
| <say>el restaurante</say> | the restaurant | <say>la escuela</say> | the school |
| <say>el hotel</say> | the hotel | <say>la iglesia</say> | the church |
| <say>el supermercado</say> | the supermarket | <say>la estación</say> | the station (-ción → fem) |
| <say>el museo</say> | the museum | | |
| <say>el cine</say> | the cinema | | |
| <say>el aeropuerto</say> | the airport | | |

### Days of the week — all masculine, lowercase!

<say>el lunes</say>, <say>el martes</say>, <say>el miércoles</say>, <say>el jueves</say>, <say>el viernes</say>, <say>el sábado</say>, <say>el domingo</say>

> Two things English speakers always get wrong:
> 1. **Lowercase.** *<say>lunes</say>*, not *Lunes*.
> 2. **Plural identical to singular** for *<say>lunes</say> / <say>martes</say> / <say>miércoles</say> / <say>jueves</say> / <say>viernes</say>*: *<say>los lunes</say>* (Mondays). Only *<say>sábado</say>* and *<say>domingo</say>* take *-s*: *<say>los sábados</say>, <say>los domingos</say>*.

### Months — all masculine, lowercase!

<say>enero</say>, <say>febrero</say>, <say>marzo</say>, <say>abril</say>, <say>mayo</say>, <say>junio</say>, <say>julio</say>, <say>agosto</say>, <say>septiembre</say>, <say>octubre</say>, <say>noviembre</say>, <say>diciembre</say>

> Months usually go **without an article**: *<say>en enero</say>* (in January), *<say>Estamos en marzo</say>* (it's March). Compare with days, which usually take *<say>el</say> / <say>los</say>*.

## Part 8: Language scales

Run the article system through a typical noun, out loud — slowly, then faster.

### Scale 1 — *<say>libro</say>* (masc.) and *<say>mesa</say>* (fem.)

| Form | Masculine | Feminine |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Definite sg. | <say>el libro</say> | <say>la mesa</say> |
| Definite pl. | <say>los libros</say> | <say>las mesas</say> |
| Indefinite sg. | <say>un libro</say> | <say>una mesa</say> |
| Indefinite pl. | <say>unos libros</say> | <say>unas mesas</say> |
| a + definite sg. | <say>**al** libro</say> | <say>**a la** mesa</say> |
| de + definite sg. | <say>**del** libro</say> | <say>**de la** mesa</say> |

> Last two rows: contraction with *<say>el</say>* only. Feminine forms stay as two words.

### Scale 2 — places vocab

Run all four forms (masc: <say>el</say> / <say>un</say> / <say>los</say> / <say>unos</say>; fem: <say>la</say> / <say>una</say> / <say>las</say> / <say>unas</say>) through:
- masc: <say>banco</say>, <say>parque</say>, <say>hotel</say>, <say>mercado</say>
- fem: <say>tienda</say>, <say>farmacia</say>, <say>escuela</say>, <say>estación</say>

## Part 9: Language matrix (Q–A drills)

**Mirror principle:** the answer is already encoded in the question. Carry the article across.

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| <say>¿Hay un banco aquí?</say> (answer: yes, on the square) | <say>Sí, el banco está en la plaza.</say> |
| <say>¿Hay una farmacia cerca?</say> (answer: yes, on the main street) | <say>Sí, la farmacia está en la calle Mayor.</say> |
| <say>¿Vas a la tienda?</say> | <say>Sí, voy a la tienda.</say> |
| <say>¿Vas al mercado?</say> | <say>Sí, voy al mercado.</say> |
| <say>¿Vienes del parque?</say> | <say>Sí, vengo del parque.</say> |
| <say>¿Vienes de la escuela?</say> | <say>Sí, vengo de la escuela.</say> |
| <say>¿Trabajas el lunes?</say> | <say>Sí, el lunes trabajo.</say> |
| <say>¿Trabajas los sábados?</say> | <say>No, los sábados no trabajo.</say> |

Notice the rhythm: *<say>al</say> / <say>del</say>* pair with masculine singular places; *<say>a la</say> / <say>de la</say>* with feminine.

## Part 10: Exercises

### Exercise 1. Fill in the definite article (el / la / los / las)

1. ___ libros están en ___ mesa.
2. ___ farmacia está en ___ calle Mayor.
3. ___ problema es serio. *(careful: -ma!)*
4. ___ mano derecha. *(careful: -o ≠ masc!)*
5. ___ días pasan rápido.
6. ___ agua está fría. *(special case!)*
7. ___ museos abren a ___ diez.
8. ___ lunes voy al banco.

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. ***Los** libros están en **la** mesa.*
2. ***La** farmacia está en **la** calle Mayor.*
3. ***El** problema es serio.* (Greek *-ma* nouns are masculine)
4. ***La** mano derecha.* (*la mano* is an exception to the "-o = masc" rule)
5. ***Los** días pasan rápido.* (*el día* is an exception — masc despite -a)
6. ***El** agua está fría.* (feminine, but stressed *a-* takes *el* in the singular)
7. ***Los** museos abren a **las** diez.* (telling time → always *las*)
8. ***El** lunes voy al banco.* (al = a + el)

</details>

### Exercise 2. Definite or indefinite? Pick the right form

1. Hay (un / el) restaurante en la plaza.
2. Voy a (un / el) mercado los sábados — siempre el mismo.
3. Necesito (una / la) farmacia. ¿Hay (una / la) cerca?
4. (Un / El) sol es (una / la) estrella.
5. Me gusta (un / el) café por la mañana.
6. Compro (unos / los) libros para mi clase.

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *Hay **un** restaurante en la plaza.* (first mention, after *hay*)
2. *Voy **al** mercado los sábados.* (the same specific one → definite *el* → contracts to *al*)
3. *Necesito **una** farmacia. ¿Hay **una** cerca?* (any pharmacy, not a specific one)
4. ***El** sol es **una** estrella.* (the sun is unique → definite; one of many stars → indefinite)
5. *Me gusta **el** café.* (generalization — coffee in general)
6. *Compro **unos** libros.* (a few non-specific ones)

</details>

### Exercise 3. Contractions al / del

Combine the preposition and article where possible. If no contraction applies, write them separately.

1. Voy **a + el** banco.
2. Vengo **de + el** parque.
3. Voy **a + la** tienda.
4. Hablo **de + los** museos.
5. La puerta **de + el** hotel.
6. Vengo **de + El** Salvador. *(proper name!)*
7. Voy **a + las** plazas.
8. Voy **a + la** escuela.

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *Voy **al** banco.*
2. *Vengo **del** parque.*
3. *Voy **a la** tienda.* (no contraction with *la*)
4. *Hablo **de los** museos.* (no contraction with *los*)
5. *La puerta **del** hotel.*
6. *Vengo **de El** Salvador.* (proper name — no contraction!)
7. *Voy **a las** plazas.* (no contraction with *las*)
8. *Voy **a la** escuela.* (NOT ~~ala~~ — only *a + el* contracts)

</details>

### Exercise 4. Translate from English to Spanish

1. I'm going to the shop on Monday.
2. On Saturdays I don't work.
3. The café is on the corner.
4. I'm coming back from the park.
5. It's a market. The market is big.
6. I like coffee.
7. It's three o'clock.
8. It's cold in January.
9. I'm going to the airport.
10. I'm coming from the school.

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *Voy a la tienda el lunes.* (note: *a la* stays separate; *el lunes* = "on Monday")
2. *Los sábados no trabajo.* ("on Saturdays" = *los sábados*)
3. *El café está en la esquina.*
4. *Vengo del parque.* (de + el → del)
5. *Es un mercado. El mercado es grande.* (first mention → *un*, second mention → *el*)
6. *Me gusta el café.* (generalization → definite article!)
7. *Son las tres.* (always *las* for time)
8. *En enero hace frío.* (months — no article)
9. *Voy al aeropuerto.* (a + el → al)
10. *Vengo de la escuela.* (de + la → no contraction)

</details>

### Exercise 5. Find and fix the mistake

Each sentence has one article-related error.

1. Voy a el banco.
2. Hay el supermercado aquí.
3. La problema es difícil.
4. En el enero hace frío.
5. La agua está caliente.
6. Soy un profesor.
7. Lunes voy a la escuela.
8. Son la tres.

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *Voy **al** banco.* (a + el = al — mandatory contraction)
2. *Hay **un** supermercado aquí.* (after *hay* → indefinite)
3. ***El** problema es difícil.* (*problema* is masc — Greek -ma!)
4. *En enero hace frío.* (months take no article)
5. ***El** agua está caliente.* (feminine, but *el* before stressed *a-*)
6. *Soy profesor.* (ser + profession with no descriptor → no article)
7. ***El** lunes voy a la escuela.* ("on Monday" needs *el*)
8. *Son **las** tres.* (time: always feminine plural *las*)

</details>

## Lesson cheat sheet

```
DEFINITE (specific/known)         INDEFINITE (new / "a / some")
       masc.   fem.                       masc.   fem.
sg.    el      la                  sg.    un      una
pl.    los     las                 pl.    unos    unas

CONTRACTIONS — only with el!
    a  + el = al     →  Voy AL banco.
    de + el = del    →  Vengo DEL parque.

    With la / los / las — NO contraction: a la tienda, de los museos.
    With proper name El — NO contraction: de El Salvador.
    TRAP: Voy AL parque   vs.   Voy A LA escuela.

SPANISH USES ARTICLE, ENGLISH DOESN'T:
    Generalizations:  Me gusta EL café.       (I like coffee)
    Abstract nouns:   LA vida es bella.
    Days of week:     EL lunes trabajo. / LOS sábados descanso.
    Time:             Son LAS tres. / A LAS ocho.
    Unique things:    EL sol, LA luna, EL mundo.

SPANISH OMITS ARTICLE, ENGLISH ADDS ONE:
    ser + profession: Soy profesor.           (I'm A teacher)
    Months:           En enero.
    Person's name:    María viene.
    After hay:        Hay un banco.           (NOT hay el banco)

SPECIAL CASE — stressed a-/ha- feminine nouns take EL in sg.:
    el agua fría, el águila negra, el hambre, el alma.
    Plural reverts to las: las aguas frías.

FIRST-MENTION RULE:  1st → un/una;  2nd → el/la.

DAYS & MONTHS — lowercase, all masculine.
    lunes…viernes: plural = singular (los lunes = "Mondays")
    sábado/domingo: take -s. enero…diciembre: usually no article.

MEMORIZE NOUNS WITH THEIR ARTICLE — article = half of the word.
```
