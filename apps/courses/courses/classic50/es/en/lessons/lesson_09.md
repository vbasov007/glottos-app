# Lesson 9: Ser vs Estar — the full contrast
**Vocabulary: Two-meaning adjectives, weather, time expressions**

---

## How to work with this lesson

1. **Read** — understand the rule (5 minutes, no more!)
2. **Say it out loud** — slowly, through every person
3. **Speed up** — repeat until the choice of *ser* or *estar* becomes reflex

> **The mission:** stop translating English "to be" word-for-word; choose by type of statement. *<say>Ser</say>* = part of who/what something IS. *<say>Estar</say>* = a state or a place.

---

## Part 1: One English verb, two Spanish verbs

English overloads "to be": *She **is** a doctor. She **is** at home. She **is** tired. She **is** tall. It **is** five o'clock. The soup **is** cold.* Six different ideas, one verb. Spanish splits "to be" in two and forces you to pick every time.

| English | Spanish | Why |
|---------|---------|-----|
| She is a doctor. | <say>Es médica.</say> | profession = identity → **<say>ser</say>** |
| She is at home. | <say>Está en casa.</say> | location → **<say>estar</say>** |
| She is tired. | <say>Está cansada.</say> | temporary state → **<say>estar</say>** |
| She is tall. | <say>Es alta.</say> | defining trait → **<say>ser</say>** |
| It is five o'clock. | <say>Son las cinco.</say> | time → **<say>ser</say>** |
| The soup is cold. | <say>La sopa está fría.</say> | result → **<say>estar</say>** |

> **The diagnostic question:** is this *what* something IS, or *where/how* it is right now?
> Permanent / defining → **<say>ser</say>**. Temporary / location / result → **<say>estar</say>**.

This is THE classic Spanish trap. Once you crack the logic, Spanish feels more precise than English. But you have to crack it 500 times — that's what this lesson drills.

## Part 2: Forms (quick refresher from Lessons 4–5)

| Person | <say>ser</say> | <say>estar</say> |
|--------|-----|-------|
| <say>yo</say> | <say>soy</say> | <say>estoy</say> |
| <say>tú</say> | <say>eres</say> | <say>estás</say> |
| <say>él</say> / <say>ella</say> / <say>usted</say> | <say>es</say> | <say>está</say> |
| <say>nosotros/as</say> | <say>somos</say> | <say>estamos</say> |
| <say>vosotros/as</say> | <say>sois</say> | <say>estáis</say> |
| <say>ellos</say> / <say>ellas</say> / <say>ustedes</say> | <say>son</say> | <say>están</say> |

> **Notice the tildes on *<say>estar</say>*:** *<say>estás</say>, <say>está</say>, <say>estáis</say>, <say>están</say>* — stress on the last syllable, ending in vowel/n/s → tilde required (Lesson 1).

## Part 3: SER — the big domains

> **Mnemonic — DOCTOR:** **D**escription, **O**ccupation, **C**haracteristic, **T**ime, **O**rigin, **R**elationship.

| Domain | Examples |
|--------|----------|
| **Identity** — who/what something is | *<say>Soy María. Es un libro. Somos estudiantes.</say>* |
| **Origin / nationality** | *<say>Soy de Inglaterra. Son de Madrid.</say>* |
| **Profession** | *<say>Es médico. Somos profesores.</say>* |
| **Defining trait** — looks, character, color, size | *<say>Es alto. Eres simpática. El cielo es azul.</say>* |
| **Time and date** | *<say>Son las tres. Hoy es lunes. Es enero.</say>* |
| **Possession / relationship** | *<say>Es mi hermano. El libro es de Ana.</say>* |
| **Material** | *<say>La camisa es de algodón.</say>* |
| **Where an EVENT takes place** | *<say>La fiesta es en mi casa. El concierto es a las ocho.</say>* |

> **Shortcut:** if English "to be" states *what/who* something is, or the *time/date*, or *whose* it is → almost always **<say>ser</say>**.

---

## Part 4: ESTAR — the big domains

> **Mnemonic — PLACE:** **P**osition, **L**ocation, **A**ction (in progress), **C**ondition, **E**motion.

| Domain | Examples |
|--------|----------|
| **Location of a thing / person** | *<say>Estoy en casa. Madrid está en España. El libro está en la mesa.</say>* |
| **Physical state** | *<say>Estoy cansado. Estás enfermo. Estamos bien.</say>* |
| **Emotional state** | *<say>Está triste. Estoy contento. Están nerviosos.</say>* |
| **Result / changed quality** | *<say>La sopa está fría. La puerta está abierta.</say>* |
| **Progressive (Lesson 20)** | *<say>Estoy comiendo. Está lloviendo.</say>* |
| **Marital status (regional)** | *<say>Está casada con Pedro.</say>* |

> **Trap #1 (event vs thing):** *<say>La fiesta es en mi casa</say>* (event — **<say>ser</say>**) vs *<say>Mi casa está en Madrid</say>* (building — **<say>estar</say>**).
> **Trap #2 (location is always estar):** even for things that never move — *<say>El Everest está en Asia.</say>* English speakers feel this is permanent and reach for *<say>ser</say>*. Don't.
> **Trap #3:** *<say>casado</say>* is often **<say>ser</say>** in Spain, **<say>estar</say>** in Latin America. Both accepted.

## Part 5: The heart of the lesson — adjectives that change meaning

The same adjective with *ser* and with *estar* means **two different things**. Mix these up and you say something you didn't mean — sometimes hilariously.

| Adjective | With **<say>ser</say>** = trait | With **<say>estar</say>** = state |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| **<say>aburrido</say>** | boring (a dull person) | bored |
| **<say>listo</say>** | clever, smart | ready |
| **<say>rico</say>** | rich (wealthy) | tasty / delicious |
| **<say>bueno</say>** | good (person, quality) | tasty / attractive / recovered |
| **<say>malo</say>** | bad (person, quality) | ill / spoiled |
| **<say>verde</say>** | green (color / variety) | unripe |
| **<say>vivo</say>** | sharp, quick-witted | alive |
| **<say>abierto</say>** | open-minded | open (door, shop) |
| **<say>cerrado</say>** | closed-off, reserved | closed |

### The classic mini-pairs — burn these in

| With <say>ser</say> | With <say>estar</say> |
|----------|------------|
| *<say>Pedro es aburrido.</say>* — Pedro is boring. | *<say>Pedro está aburrido.</say>* — Pedro is bored. |
| *<say>La niña es lista.</say>* — The girl is clever. | *<say>La niña está lista.</say>* — The girl is ready. |
| *<say>La manzana es verde.</say>* — Green variety. | *<say>La manzana está verde.</say>* — Unripe. |
| *<say>Ana es buena.</say>* — Ana is a good person. | *<say>Ana está buena.</say>* — Ana looks attractive / has recovered. |
| *<say>Mi tío es rico.</say>* — My uncle is wealthy. | *<say>La paella está rica.</say>* — The paella is delicious. |

> **Danger zone:** *<say>está buena/o</say>* applied to a person is colloquial for "attractive / hot". *<say>Es buena/o</say>* means "is a good person". Different choice — flirt or moralize.

## Part 6: Weather — the verb depends on the noun

English says "it's hot / it's cloudy / it's raining" — one verb for everything. Spanish picks the construction by WHAT FOLLOWS:

| Construction | When | Examples |
|--------------|------|----------|
| **<say>hacer</say> + noun** ("it makes") | weather, temperature | *<say>Hace frío / calor / sol / viento / fresco / buen tiempo.</say>* |
| **<say>estar</say> + adjective** | sky / atmosphere now | *<say>Está nublado / despejado / soleado.</say>* |
| **<say>hay</say> + noun** ("there is") | a phenomenon | *<say>Hay niebla / tormenta / nubes.</say>* |
| **own verb** | precipitation | *<say>Llueve. Nieva. Está lloviendo / nevando.</say>* |

Mechanical decision: noun like *<say>frío/calor/sol/viento</say>* → **<say>hace</say>**; noun like *<say>niebla/tormenta</say>* → **<say>hay</say>**; adjective like *<say>nublado/despejado</say>* → **<say>está</say>**; rain/snow → own verb.

> **Trap:** *<say>Hace frío</say>* (the weather is cold) vs *<say>Tengo frío</say>* (I'm cold — lit. "I have cold"). NEVER *<say>Estoy frío</say>* — that means "I'm cold to the touch", i.e. a corpse. Same for *<say>calor</say>*.
> **Question:** *<say>¿Qué tiempo hace?</say>* — What's the weather like?

---

## Part 7: Time and times of day — always SER

- **Clock time:** *<say>Es la una. Son las tres. Son las diez y media.</say>*
- **Day / month / date:** *<say>Hoy es martes. Es marzo. Es 14 de mayo.</say>*
- **Part of day:** *<say>Es de día. Es de noche. Es tarde. Es temprano.</say>*

> **Quirk:** *<say>Es la una</say>* (singular — one o'clock), *<say>Son las dos / tres / …</say>* (plural).

**Times of day:** *<say>la mañana</say>* (morning), *<say>el mediodía</say>* (noon), *<say>la tarde</say>* (afternoon/evening), *<say>la noche</say>* (night), *<say>la medianoche</say>* (midnight); *<say>por la mañana / tarde / noche</say>* (in the morning / afternoon / at night); *<say>temprano</say> / <say>tarde</say>* (early / late); *<say>ahora</say>, <say>hoy</say>, <say>ayer</say>, <say>mañana</say>* (now, today, yesterday, tomorrow).

---

## Part 8: Vocabulary banks

**Always with SER (defining traits):** *<say>alto/a</say>* (tall), *<say>bajo/a</say>* (short), *<say>joven</say>* (young), *<say>viejo/a</say>* (old), *<say>inteligente</say>* (smart), *<say>simpático/a</say>* (nice), *<say>antipático/a</say>* (unpleasant), *<say>tímido/a</say>* (shy), *<say>honesto/a</say>* (honest), *<say>trabajador/a</say>* (hard-working), *<say>amable</say>* (kind), *<say>guapo/a</say>* (good-looking).

**Always with ESTAR (states / results):** *<say>cansado/a</say>* (tired), *<say>enfermo/a</say>* (sick), *<say>contento/a</say>* (happy), *<say>triste</say>* (sad), *<say>nervioso/a</say>* (nervous), *<say>ocupado/a</say>* (busy), *<say>enfadado/a</say>* (angry), *<say>preocupado/a</say>* (worried), *<say>roto/a</say>* (broken), *<say>abierto/a</say>* (open), *<say>cerrado/a</say>* (closed), *<say>sucio/a</say>* (dirty), *<say>limpio/a</say>* (clean), *<say>lleno/a</say>* (full), *<say>vacío/a</say>* (empty).

**Weather/time nouns:** *<say>el frío</say>, <say>el calor</say>, <say>el sol</say>, <say>el viento</say>, <say>la lluvia</say>, <say>la nieve</say>, <say>la nube</say>, <say>la niebla</say>, <say>la tormenta</say>, <say>el tiempo</say>* (weather/time), *<say>la hora</say>* (hour).

## Part 9: Language matrix — question and answer

> **Mirror principle:** the answer is already encoded in the question.

### Matrix 1: "Who/What" vs "Where/How"

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| <say>¿Quién eres?</say> | <say>Soy estudiante.</say> |
| <say>¿De dónde eres?</say> | <say>Soy de Inglaterra.</say> |
| <say>¿Cómo eres?</say> | <say>Soy tranquilo y honesto.</say> |
| <say>¿Dónde estás?</say> | <say>Estoy en casa.</say> |
| <say>¿Cómo estás?</say> | <say>Estoy bien, gracias.</say> |
| <say>¿Qué hora es?</say> | <say>Son las cinco.</say> |
| <say>¿Qué día es hoy?</say> | <say>Hoy es jueves.</say> |
| <say>¿Qué tiempo hace?</say> | <say>Hace sol y calor.</say> |
| <say>¿Está abierto el banco?</say> (answer: no) | <say>No, está cerrado.</say> |
| <say>¿Dónde es la fiesta?</say> | <say>Es en mi casa.</say> |
| <say>¿Dónde está tu casa?</say> | <say>Está en el centro.</say> |

### Matrix 2: Two-meaning adjectives — same word, two answers

| Question | SER (trait) | ESTAR (state) |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| <say>¿Cómo es/está Pedro?</say> (ser: boring; estar: bored) | <say>Es aburrido.</say> (dull) | <say>Está aburrido.</say> (bored) |
| <say>¿La sopa?</say> | <say>Es buena para la salud.</say> | <say>Está buena, ¡qué rica!</say> |
| <say>¿El niño?</say> | <say>Es muy listo.</say> | <say>Ya está listo, vámonos.</say> |

### Matrix 3: Run the contrast through every person

| Person | SER (what I am) | ESTAR (how I am now) |
|--------|------------------|----------------------|
| <say>yo</say> / <say>tú</say> | <say>Soy alegre.</say> / <say>Eres tranquilo.</say> | <say>Estoy alegre hoy.</say> / <say>Estás tranquilo ahora.</say> |
| <say>él</say> / <say>nosotros</say> | <say>Es nervioso.</say> / <say>Somos optimistas.</say> | <say>Está nervioso por el examen.</say> / <say>Estamos optimistas.</say> |
| <say>vosotros</say> / <say>ellos</say> | <say>Sois pacientes.</say> / <say>Son serios.</say> | <say>Estáis pacientes hoy.</say> / <say>Están serios después.</say> |

> Run all six persons out loud three times. On the third pass, cover the first column and rebuild from memory.

## Lesson cheat sheet

```
SER vs ESTAR — ONE DECISION IN ONE SECOND

DOCTOR  →  SER          |  PLACE  →  ESTAR
────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────
Description (trait)     |  Position / Location of thing
Occupation              |  Action in progress (estoy -ndo)
Characteristic          |  Condition / physical state
Time / date             |  Emotion / mood right now
Origin                  |  Result / changed quality
Relationship / poss.    |  Marital status (regional)
Material                |
LOCATION OF EVENT       |  LOCATION OF A THING/BUILDING
(La fiesta ES en…)      |  (Mi casa ESTÁ en…)

FORMS:
  ser:   soy  eres  es   somos  sois   son
  estar: estoy estás está estamos estáis están

TWO-MEANING ADJECTIVES (the gold):
  aburrido:  ser = boring    / estar = bored
  listo:     ser = clever    / estar = ready
  rico:      ser = wealthy   / estar = tasty
  bueno:     ser = good      / estar = tasty / attractive / well
  malo:      ser = bad       / estar = ill / spoiled
  verde:     ser = green     / estar = unripe
  vivo:      ser = sharp     / estar = alive
  despierto: ser = bright    / estar = awake

WEATHER:
  HACE + noun:    frío, calor, sol, viento, fresco, buen/mal tiempo
  ESTÁ + adj.:    nublado, despejado, soleado
  HAY + noun:     niebla, tormenta, nubes
  own verb:       llueve, nieva  (or está lloviendo / está nevando)
  ⚠ tengo frío (I'm cold), NEVER estoy frío

TIME / DATE:
  ¿Qué hora ES?  — Es la una.  Son las tres y media.
  Hoy ES lunes.  Es mediodía.  Es de noche.  Es tarde.

THE KEY QUESTION:
  Is this WHAT something IS (by nature)?     → SER
  Is this WHERE it is or HOW it is now?      → ESTAR
  Is it an EVENT?                            → SER (es en…)
  Is it a THING / BUILDING?                  → ESTAR (está en…)
```

The third verb of existence: "there is / there are" in the impersonal sense. *Hay un libro* (some book exists) vs *El libro está en la mesa* (the specific book is located there). Plus quantifiers (mucho, poco, bastante, demasiado), furniture vocabulary, and ordinal numbers 1–10.
