# Lesson 32: Subjunctive after verbs of will and influence
**Vocabulary: Verbs of wanting, requesting, advising, commanding; obligation**

---

## How to work with this lesson

1. **Read** — figure out where subjuntivo kicks in and where it doesn't (5 minutes).
2. **Run the scales** — *quiero que / espero que / es necesario que* + different persons + different verbs. This is the core.
3. **Drill the matrix** — short Q&A "what do you want me to do?" pairs. Until question-and-answer pairs flow on their own.

> Lesson 31 gave you the **forms** of subjuntivo. This lesson gives you its first and clearest **trigger**: one person's will aimed at another.
> Knowing the rule = 5%. Training your mouth = 95%.

---

## Part 1: The big idea — one person wills, another acts

> **I want YOU to do something.** Two different subjects → the second verb goes into **subjuntivo**.

English has a trick that hides this: instead of saying "I want **that** you eat", we collapse it into "I want you **to eat**". Spanish doesn't allow that shortcut. Whenever the subject of the second action is **different** from the subject of the main verb (and the main verb is one of will/influence), you MUST use *que* + subjunctive.

| English | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I want to come. (same subject — me wanting, me coming) | *Quiero venir.* (infinitive) |
| I want you to come. (different subjects — me wanting, you coming) | *Quiero que vengas.* (que + subjunctive) |
| She asks us to be quiet. | *Ella pide que (nos) callemos.* |
| The teacher tells us to open the book. | *El profesor manda que abramos el libro.* |

> **The two-subject rule:** If both actions belong to the same person → **infinitive**, no *que*. If the actions belong to different people → **que + subjuntivo**.

| One subject (infinitive) | Two subjects (que + subjuntivo) |
|--------------------------|---------------------------------|
| *Quiero estudiar.* — I want to study. | *Quiero que estudies.* — I want you to study. |
| *Espero llegar a tiempo.* — I hope to arrive on time. | *Espero que llegues a tiempo.* — I hope you arrive on time. |
| *Prefiero quedarme en casa.* — I prefer to stay home. | *Prefiero que te quedes en casa.* — I prefer that you stay home. |

> **Trap #1:** Never say *Quiero que yo vaya* — same subject means *Quiero ir*.
> **Trap #2:** English drops "that" — "I want you to come" has no visible "that". Spanish *que* is **never optional**: *Quiero que vengas*, never *Quiero vengas*.
> **Trap #3:** Don't slip back into indicative. *Espero que **vienes*** is wrong — *esperar que* always takes subjuntivo: *Espero que **vengas***.

## Part 2: Triggers — the verbs that flip the switch

All of these verbs express **will, desire, request, advice, command, permission, or prohibition**. After them, when the subject changes and you cross a *que* — **only subjuntivo**.

### Group 1: Wanting and hoping

| Spanish | English | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **querer que** | to want (somebody) to | *Quiero que vengas.* — I want you to come. |
| **desear que** | to wish that | *Deseo que tengas suerte.* — I wish you luck. |
| **preferir que** | to prefer that | *Prefiero que lo hagas tú.* — I'd rather you do it. |
| **esperar que** | to hope that | *Espero que estés bien.* — I hope you're well. |

> *Esperar* is a double agent. "To hope" → subjuntivo. "To wait for" → plain object: *Espero a María* (I'm waiting for María).

### Group 2: Requesting, advising, commanding

| Spanish | English | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **pedir que** | to ask (somebody) to | *Te pido que me ayudes.* — I'm asking you to help me. |
| **rogar que** | to beg that | *Te ruego que me escuches.* — I beg you to listen to me. |
| **suplicar que** | to plead that | *Le suplico que me perdone.* — I plead with you to forgive me. |
| **mandar que** | to order that | *El jefe manda que trabajemos.* — The boss orders us to work. |
| **ordenar que** | to order (formal) | *El juez ordena que se calle.* — The judge orders him to be quiet. |
| **exigir que** | to demand that | *Exijo que me digan la verdad.* — I demand that you tell me the truth. |
| **sugerir que** | to suggest that | *Sugiero que descansemos.* — I suggest we rest. |
| **aconsejar que** | to advise (somebody) to | *Te aconsejo que estudies más.* — I advise you to study more. |
| **recomendar que** | to recommend that | *Recomiendo que pruebes la paella.* — I recommend you try the paella. |

### Group 3: Permitting and prohibiting

| Spanish | English | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **prohibir que** | to forbid that | *Prohíben que fumemos aquí.* — They forbid us to smoke here. |
| **permitir que** | to allow that | *No permito que me hables así.* — I won't allow you to talk to me like that. |
| **dejar que** | to let (somebody) | *Deja que lo intente.* — Let him try. |
| **hacer que** | to make (somebody) do | *Haz que se sientan bien.* — Make them feel good. |

> Note: *prohíbo, prohíbes, prohíbe, prohibimos, prohibís, prohíben* — the tilde splits the *i* from the *o*.

### Group 4: Impersonal expressions of necessity

After these, **always** subjuntivo if there's a subject. Without a subject — plain infinitive, expressing a general rule.

| Spanish | English | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **es necesario que** | it's necessary that | *Es necesario que vayas al médico.* — You need to see a doctor. |
| **es preciso que** | it's needed that | *Es preciso que actuemos ya.* — We need to act now. |
| **hace falta que** | it's needed that | *Hace falta que estudies más.* — You need to study more. |
| **es importante que** | it's important that | *Es importante que llegues a tiempo.* — It's important you arrive on time. |
| **es preferible que** | it's preferable that | *Es preferible que descanses.* — It's better you rest. |
| **es mejor que** | it's better that | *Es mejor que no digas nada.* — Better that you say nothing. |
| **es urgente que** | it's urgent that | *Es urgente que vengas.* — It's urgent that you come. |

| Without subject (infinitive) | With subject (que + subjuntivo) |
|------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| *Es necesario estudiar.* — One has to study. | *Es necesario que estudies.* — You need to study. |
| *Es importante llegar a tiempo.* — It's important to be on time. | *Es importante que llegues a tiempo.* — It's important that you arrive on time. |

### Group 5: Pure wishing — ojalá

**Ojalá (que)** comes from Arabic *wa-šā' allāh* — "and God willing". It means "I hope / I wish / if only". It **always** triggers subjuntivo, and the *que* is optional. *Ojalá* is the one trigger that needs no main clause and no different subject — it stands alone.

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| *Ojalá llueva.* | I hope it rains. / If only it would rain. |
| *Ojalá (que) vengas mañana.* | I hope you come tomorrow. |
| *Ojalá tengas razón.* | I hope you're right. |
| *Ojalá no llegue tarde.* | I hope he doesn't arrive late. |

## Part 3: Scales — train the mouth

The goal: make the transition *querer/esperar/es necesario + que + subjuntivo* automatic. Run each scale out loud 3 times.

### Scale 1: *querer que* + changing person + *venir*

| English | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I want you to come | *Quiero que vengas.* |
| I want him to come | *Quiero que venga.* |
| I want us to come | *Quiero que vengamos.* |
| I want you (pl., Spain) to come | *Quiero que vengáis.* |
| I want them to come | *Quiero que vengan.* |

### Scale 2: *espero que* + tú + changing verb

| English | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I hope you come | *Espero que vengas.* |
| I hope you call | *Espero que llames.* |
| I hope you rest | *Espero que descanses.* |
| I hope you understand | *Espero que entiendas.* |
| I hope you tell the truth | *Espero que digas la verdad.* |
| I hope you can come | *Espero que puedas venir.* |

### Scale 3: *es necesario que* + changing person + *estudiar*

| English | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| It's necessary that I study | *Es necesario que (yo) estudie.* |
| It's necessary that you study | *Es necesario que estudies.* |
| It's necessary that he studies | *Es necesario que estudie.* |
| It's necessary that we study | *Es necesario que estudiemos.* |
| It's necessary that they study | *Es necesario que estudien.* |

### Scale 4: One subject vs. two — the switch

The single most important drill of the lesson. Same verb *querer*, only the second-action subject changes.

| English | Spanish | Form |
|---------|---------|------|
| I want to sleep. | *Quiero dormir.* | infinitive |
| I want you to sleep. | *Quiero que duermas.* | subjuntivo |
| I want to eat. | *Quiero comer.* | infinitive |
| I want him to eat. | *Quiero que coma.* | subjuntivo |
| We prefer to leave. | *Preferimos irnos.* | infinitive |
| We prefer that you leave. | *Preferimos que te vayas.* | subjuntivo |
| I hope to arrive on time. | *Espero llegar a tiempo.* | infinitive |
| I hope they arrive on time. | *Espero que lleguen a tiempo.* | subjuntivo |

> **How to drill it:** take a pair, read the first line (infinitive) and then immediately the second (subjuntivo). One-two-one-two. Keep going until the switch becomes a reflex.

## Part 4: Matrix — Q&A pairs

Short Q&A built on the lesson's triggers. Say each pair out loud 3 times.

> — *¿Qué quieres que haga?* — *Quiero que me ayudes con la cena.*
> — *¿Qué esperas de mí?* — *Espero que seas honesto.*
> — *¿Qué me aconsejas?* — *Te aconsejo que hables con tu jefe.*
> — *¿Qué me recomiendas?* — *Te recomiendo que pruebes el pescado.*
> — *¿Es necesario que vaya yo?* — *Sí, es necesario que vayas tú.*
> — *¿Me dejas salir esta noche?* — *Sí, dejo que salgas, pero vuelve temprano.*
> — *¿Puedo fumar aquí?* — *No, no permiten que se fume dentro.*
> — *¿Quieres que te espere?* — *No, prefiero que no me esperes.*
> — *¿Crees que ganaremos?* — *Ojalá ganemos.*
> — *¿Hará buen tiempo mañana?* — *Ojalá no llueva.*

## Part 5: Vocabulary — verbs of will, influence, and necessity

| Spanish | English | Note |
|---------|---------|------|
| querer que | to want sb to | base trigger |
| desear que | to wish that | slightly more formal |
| preferir que | to prefer that | e→ie in present |
| esperar que | to hope that | not "to wait for sb" |
| pedir que | to ask sb to | e→i |
| rogar que | to beg that | stronger than pedir |
| mandar que | to order that | everyday "tell sb to" |
| ordenar que | to order that | official register |
| exigir que | to demand that | sharp, forceful |
| sugerir que | to suggest that | e→ie |
| aconsejar que | to advise sb to | friendly advice |
| recomendar que | to recommend that | e→ie |
| insistir en que | to insist on/that | with the preposition *en* |
| prohibir que | to forbid that | tilde: *prohíbo* |
| permitir que | to allow that | formal |
| dejar que | to let that | everyday |
| hacer que | to make sth happen | causation or result |
| impedir que | to prevent that | e→i |
| es necesario que | it's necessary that | impersonal |
| es preciso que | it's needed that | a touch more formal |
| hace falta que | it's needed that | softer version |
| es importante que | it's important that | |
| es mejor que | it's better that | |
| es urgente que | it's urgent that | |
| ojalá (que) | hopefully / I wish | always subjuntivo |
| que + subj. (standalone) | may… / let… | *Que tengas un buen día.* |

### Exercise 2. Infinitive or subjunctive? One subject or two?

Pick the right form.

1. Quiero (ir / que yo vaya) al cine.
2. Quiero (ir / que vayas) tú al cine conmigo.
3. Esperamos (llegar / que lleguemos) a tiempo.
4. Esperamos (llegar / que llegues) tú a tiempo.
5. Prefiero (quedarme / que me quede) en casa.
6. Prefiero (quedarte / que te quedes) tú en casa.
7. Es necesario (hablar / que hablemos) con el director.
8. Es necesario (que hables / hablar) con él (you — with him).

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *Quiero **ir** al cine.* (same subject — I want, I go)
2. *Quiero **que vayas** tú al cine conmigo.* (two subjects — I want, you go)
3. *Esperamos **llegar** a tiempo.* (same subject — we hope, we arrive)
4. *Esperamos **que llegues** tú a tiempo.* (two subjects — we hope, you arrive)
5. *Prefiero **quedarme** en casa.* (same subject)
6. *Prefiero **que te quedes** tú en casa.* (two subjects)
7. *Es necesario **hablar** con el director.* (no specific subject — general rule)
8. *Es necesario **que hables** con él.* (subject = you)

## Lesson cheat sheet

```
ONE PERSON'S WILL → ANOTHER PERSON'S ACTION → SUBJUNTIVO.

THE TWO-SUBJECT RULE:
  Same subject       → infinitive (no que):   Quiero ir.
  Different subjects → que + subjuntivo:      Quiero que vayas.

ENGLISH HIDES THE QUE — SPANISH NEVER DROPS IT:
  "I want you to eat"  → Quiero que comas.   (not: Quiero comas.)
  "I hope you arrive"  → Espero que llegues. (not: Espero llegues.)

TRIGGERS:
  Wanting:    querer / desear / preferir / esperar         que + subj.
  Requesting: pedir / rogar / exigir                       que + subj.
  Ordering:   mandar / ordenar / hacer                     que + subj.
  Advising:   sugerir / aconsejar / recomendar             que + subj.
  Permit:     permitir / dejar / prohibir / impedir        que + subj.
  Impersonal: es necesario / es preciso / hace falta /
              es importante / es mejor                     que + subj.
  Pure wish:  ojalá (que)                                  + subj. ALWAYS

REFERENCE EXAMPLES:
  Quiero que vengas.            — I want you to come.
  Espero que estés bien.        — I hope you're well.
  Te aconsejo que descanses.    — I advise you to rest.
  Es necesario que estudies.    — You need to study.
  Ojalá llueva.                 — I hope it rains.

TRAPS:
  "Quiero que yo vaya"   — NO. Same subject → Quiero ir.
  "Quiero vengas"        — NO. The que is mandatory.
  "Espero que vienes"    — NO. After esperar que → only subjuntivo.
```
