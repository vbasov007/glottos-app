# Lesson 7: Present tense of regular verbs — -AR, -ER, -IR
**Vocabulary: Everyday-action verbs**

---

## How to work with this lesson

1. **Read** — understand the three endings tables (5 minutes)
2. **Run the scales** — drive every verb through all 6 persons out loud
3. **Matrix** — answer questions, switching person: yo → tú → él → nosotros → vosotros → ellos
4. **Don't learn verbs as a list** — learn them in context, through the exercises

> This is the **engine** of the language. Everything after Lesson 7 gets conjugated. Master the three tables today — the next 40 lessons get twice as easy.

---

## Part 1: English has it easy. Spanish, not so much.

In English: *I speak, you speak, he speak**s**, we speak, you (pl.) speak, they speak.* **Five out of six forms are identical.** English present tense has basically one ending — **-s** in the 3rd singular.

Spanish does not give you that mercy. Each verb has **six different endings** per tense, and there are **three** verb classes (called *conjugations*) — so you're learning **18 endings** instead of one.

**The good news:**
- The patterns are **extremely regular**. About 70% of all Spanish verbs are -AR, and they ALL behave like *<say>hablar</say>*. Learn one, you've got thousands.
- Spanish drops the subject pronoun. The **ending IS the subject**. *<say>Hablo</say>* by itself = "I speak".
- -ER and -IR are nearly identical twins — they differ in only two of six forms.

---

## Part 2: The three families

Every Spanish infinitive ends in **-AR**, **-ER**, or **-IR**. These three endings are three "family names". Each family has its own set of personal endings.

| Group | Example | Share of all verbs |
|--------|---------|--------------------|
| **-AR** | <say>habl**ar**</say> (to speak) | ~70% |
| **-ER** | <say>com**er**</say> (to eat) | ~20% |
| **-IR** | <say>viv**ir**</say> (to live) | ~10% |

> **Key insight:** the **-ER** and **-IR** endings are almost identical. They differ ONLY in *<say>nosotros</say>* and *<say>vosotros</say>*. Treat that pair as twins.

### How to conjugate — 3 steps

1. Take the infinitive: *<say>hablar</say>*
2. Strip -AR / -ER / -IR: stem is **habl-**
3. Stick on the personal ending

Memorize the endings; the stem stays put.

---

## Part 3: -AR — first conjugation (hablar — to speak)

| Person | Pronoun | Ending | Form | English |
|--------|---------|--------|------|---------|
| 1 sg. | <say>yo</say> | **-o** | <say>habl**o**</say> | I speak |
| 2 sg. | <say>tú</say> | **-as** | <say>habl**as**</say> | you speak (informal) |
| 3 sg. | <say>él</say> / <say>ella</say> / <say>usted</say> | **-a** | <say>habl**a**</say> | he/she speaks, you (formal) speak |
| 1 pl. | <say>nosotros</say> / <say>nosotras</say> | **-amos** | <say>habl**amos**</say> | we speak |
| 2 pl. | <say>vosotros</say> / <say>vosotras</say> | **-áis** | <say>habl**áis**</say> | you (pl., Spain) speak |
| 3 pl. | <say>ellos</say> / <say>ellas</say> / <say>ustedes</say> | **-an** | <say>habl**an**</say> | they / you (pl.) speak |

> **The tilde:** *<say>vosotros habláis</say>* — only form here with a tilde. It's an *aguda* ending in -s, so the tilde is required (Lesson 1). Same goes for *<say>coméis</say>* and *<say>vivís</say>* below.

**Run it out loud:** *<say>hablo, hablas, habla, hablamos, habláis, hablan</say>.* Repeat 5 times.

---

## Part 4: -ER (comer — to eat) and -IR (vivir — to live)

| Person | -ER (comer) | English | -IR (vivir) | English |
|--------|-------------|---------|-------------|---------|
| <say>yo</say> | <say>com**o**</say> | I eat | <say>viv**o**</say> | I live |
| <say>tú</say> | <say>com**es**</say> | you eat | <say>viv**es**</say> | you live |
| <say>él</say> / <say>ella</say> / <say>usted</say> | <say>com**e**</say> | he/she eats | <say>viv**e**</say> | he/she lives |
| <say>nosotros</say> | <say>com**emos**</say> | we eat | <say>viv**imos**</say> | we live |
| <say>vosotros</say> | <say>com**éis**</say> | you (pl.) eat | <say>viv**ís**</say> | you (pl.) live |
| <say>ellos</say> / <say>ellas</say> / <say>ustedes</say> | <say>com**en**</say> | they eat | <say>viv**en**</say> | they live |

The -ER and -IR endings are **identical** except in *<say>nosotros</say>* (-emos vs -imos) and *<say>vosotros</say>* (-éis vs -ís).

**Run them out loud:** *<say>como, comes, come, comemos, coméis, comen</say>* / *<say>vivo, vives, vive, vivimos, vivís, viven</say>.*

## Part 5: All three side by side — spot the pattern

| Person | -AR (hablar) | -ER (comer) | -IR (vivir) |
|--------|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| <say>yo</say> | <say>habl**o**</say> | <say>com**o**</say> | <say>viv**o**</say> |
| <say>tú</say> | <say>habl**as**</say> | <say>com**es**</say> | <say>viv**es**</say> |
| <say>él</say> / <say>ella</say> / <say>usted</say> | <say>habl**a**</say> | <say>com**e**</say> | <say>viv**e**</say> |
| **<say>nosotros</say>** | <say>habl**amos**</say> | <say>com**emos**</say> | <say>viv**imos**</say> |
| **<say>vosotros</say>** | <say>habl**áis**</say> | <say>com**éis**</say> | <say>viv**ís**</say> |
| <say>ellos</say> / <say>ellas</say> / <say>ustedes</say> | <say>habl**an**</say> | <say>com**en**</say> | <say>viv**en**</say> |

> **Memory hooks:**
> - **yo** is always **-o** for all three groups. Free gift.
> - **-ER and -IR** are twins. They diverge ONLY in *nosotros* (-emos vs -imos) and *vosotros* (-éis vs -ís).
> - All 3rd plural forms end in **-n** (-an / -en).
> - All 1st plural forms end in **-mos** (-amos / -emos / -imos).
> - "Vowel signature": -AR keeps **a**, -ER keeps **e**, -IR keeps **e** except in nosotros/vosotros where the **i** resurfaces.

### Traps

> **Trap #1:** *<say>habla</say>* = "he/she speaks" **AND** "you (formal, *<say>usted</say>*) speak". Context decides which.
> **Trap #2:** *<say>vosotros</say>* exists only in Spain. In Latin America, plural "you" is *<say>ustedes</say>* — so *<say>vosotros habláis</say>* becomes *<say>ustedes hablan</say>*.
> **Trap #3:** Don't confuse *<say>comemos</say>* (-ER) with *<say>vivimos</say>* (-IR). The nosotros form is the one place -ER and -IR diverge.

---

## Part 6: When to drop the pronoun

In Spanish, **the ending already tells you who**. So the pronoun is usually **dropped**.

| With pronoun | Without pronoun | When you DO use the pronoun |
|--------------|-----------------|------------------------------|
| <say>Yo hablo español.</say> | <say>Hablo español.</say> | for contrast: *<say>Yo hablo, tú escuchas.</say>* |
| <say>¿Tú trabajas aquí?</say> | <say>¿Trabajas aquí?</say> | for emphasis or politeness |
| <say>Nosotros vivimos en Madrid.</say> | <say>Vivimos en Madrid.</say> | to contrast with another group |

> **Rule of thumb:** drop the pronoun **by default**. Include it only for contrast or clarity — especially in 3rd person, where *<say>habla</say>* could be "he", "she", or "you (formal)".

This flips English habits: where dropping "I" sounds like caveman talk, Spanish *<say>yo hablo español</say>* sounds heavy and over-emphatic. Just say *<say>hablo español</say>*.

## Vocabulary — everyday-action verbs

The course goal for this lesson is ten high-frequency regular verbs. Drill these cold.

| Spanish | English | Group |
|---------|---------|-------|
| <say>hablar</say> | to speak, talk | -AR |
| <say>trabajar</say> | to work | -AR |
| <say>estudiar</say> | to study | -AR |
| <say>comprar</say> | to buy | -AR |
| <say>comer</say> | to eat | -ER |
| <say>beber</say> | to drink | -ER |
| <say>leer</say> | to read | -ER |
| <say>aprender</say> | to learn | -ER |
| <say>vivir</say> | to live | -IR |
| <say>escribir</say> | to write | -IR |

**Quick check** — *<say>yo</say>* form for all ten: *<say>hablo, trabajo, estudio, compro, como, bebo, leo, aprendo, vivo, escribo</say>.* All ten end in **-o**. Because yo is always -o.

> **Pronunciation note:** *<say>leer</say>* has two e's. Don't merge them. *<say>Leo</say>* = "LEH-oh" (two syllables). *<say>Leemos</say>* = "leh-EH-mos" (three syllables).

## Language scales

Run each verb through all 6 persons **out loud**, no peeking. Go fast — automaticity matters more than first-try perfection.

### Scale 1: <say>hablar</say> (-AR)

> <say>yo **hablo** — tú **hablas** — él/ella **habla** — nosotros **hablamos** — vosotros **habláis** — ellos **hablan**</say>

### Scale 2: <say>trabajar</say> (-AR) / <say>comprar</say> (-AR)

> <say>trabajo — trabajas — trabaja — trabajamos — trabajáis — trabajan</say>
> <say>compro — compras — compra — compramos — compráis — compran</say>

### Scale 3: <say>comer</say> (-ER) / <say>leer</say> (-ER)

> <say>como — comes — come — comemos — coméis — comen</say>
> <say>leo — lees — lee — leemos — leéis — leen</say>

### Scale 4: <say>vivir</say> (-IR) / <say>escribir</say> (-IR)

> <say>vivo — vives — vive — vivimos — vivís — viven</say>
> <say>escribo — escribes — escribe — escribimos — escribís — escriben</say>

> **Training technique:** take one verb → fire off all 6 forms in 5 seconds → switch verbs. Don't "study conjugation" — **drill** until your mouth produces the form before your brain forms the question.

## Language matrix

Mirror principle: the answer is encoded in the question. Switch only the **person**, keep the rest on autopilot.

### Matrix 1: <say>hablar</say> / <say>trabajar</say> — where?

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| <say>¿Hablas español?</say> | <say>Sí, hablo español.</say> |
| <say>¿Habla María inglés?</say> | <say>Sí, María habla inglés.</say> |
| <say>¿Habláis francés?</say> | <say>Sí, hablamos francés.</say> |
| <say>¿Dónde trabajas?</say> | <say>Trabajo en Madrid.</say> |
| <say>¿Dónde trabaja tu hermano?</say> (answer: in a hospital) | <say>Mi hermano trabaja en un hospital.</say> |
| <say>¿Dónde trabajan tus padres?</say> (answer: at home) | <say>Mis padres trabajan en casa.</say> |

### Matrix 2: <say>comer</say> / <say>beber</say>

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| <say>¿Qué comes?</say> | <say>Como pan y queso.</say> |
| <say>¿Qué bebe Ana?</say> (answer: water) | <say>Ana bebe agua.</say> |
| <say>¿Qué coméis?</say> | <say>Comemos pasta.</say> |
| <say>¿Qué beben los niños?</say> (answer: milk) | <say>Los niños beben leche.</say> |

### Matrix 3: <say>vivir</say> — where?

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| <say>¿Dónde vives?</say> | <say>Vivo en Barcelona.</say> |
| <say>¿Dónde vive Pedro?</say> (answer: in Mexico) | <say>Pedro vive en México.</say> |
| <say>¿Dónde vivís?</say> | <say>Vivimos en un piso pequeño.</say> |
| <say>¿Dónde viven ustedes?</say> | <say>Vivimos en el centro.</say> |

### Matrix 4: mixed (<say>estudiar</say> / <say>leer</say> / <say>escribir</say> / <say>aprender</say> / <say>comprar</say>)

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| <say>¿Qué estudias?</say> | <say>Estudio medicina.</say> |
| <say>¿Qué lee tu madre?</say> (answer: a novel) | <say>Mi madre lee una novela.</say> |
| <say>¿Qué escribís?</say> | <say>Escribimos un correo.</say> |
| <say>¿Qué aprenden ellos?</say> (answer: Spanish) | <say>Aprenden español.</say> |
| <say>¿Qué compras?</say> | <say>Compro pan y leche.</say> |

## Exercises

### Exercise 1. Conjugate the verb in all 6 persons

Without peeking at the tables:

1. *comprar* (to buy)
2. *aprender* (to learn)
3. *escribir* (to write)

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *comprar*: **compro, compras, compra, compramos, compráis, compran**
2. *aprender*: **aprendo, aprendes, aprende, aprendemos, aprendéis, aprenden**
3. *escribir*: **escribo, escribes, escribe, escribimos, escribís, escriben**

Note the tildes: only on *vosotros* — *compráis, aprendéis, escribís*. The rest are untildedoned.

9. ¿Qué ___ (beber) ustedes?

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *hablo*
2. *vives*
3. *estudia*

### Exercise 5. Review — Lessons 4 and 5 (ser and estar with verbs)

## Lesson cheat sheet

```
THREE CONJUGATIONS — THREE FAMILIES OF ENDINGS

         -AR (hablar)    -ER (comer)     -IR (vivir)
yo       -o    hablo     -o    como      -o    vivo
tú       -as   hablas    -es   comes     -es   vives
él/ella  -a    habla     -e    come      -e    vive
nosotros -amos hablamos  -emos comemos   -imos vivimos
vosotros -áis  habláis   -éis  coméis    -ís   vivís
ellos    -an   hablan    -en   comen     -en   viven

KEY ANCHORS:
  yo       → ALWAYS -o (all three groups)
  -ER and -IR → twins, diverge only in nosotros/vosotros
  ellos    → ALWAYS -n
  nosotros → ALWAYS -mos
  tilde    → ONLY in vosotros: habláis, coméis, vivís

ALGORITHM:
  1. Take infinitive:        hablar
  2. Strip -AR / -ER / -IR:  habl-
  3. Add personal ending:    habl + amos → hablamos

DROP THE PRONOUN BY DEFAULT
  Hablo español.   (NOT: Yo hablo español.)
  Use the pronoun only for contrast: Yo hablo, tú escuchas.

CORE TEN VERBS (memorize cold):
  -AR: hablar, trabajar, estudiar, comprar
  -ER: comer, beber, leer, aprender
  -IR: vivir, escribir

TRAPS:
  • habla  = "he/she speaks" AND "you (usted) speak"
  • hablan = "they speak" AND "you (ustedes) speak"
  • vosotros — Spain only; Latin America uses ustedes
  • comemos vs vivimos — only -ER/-IR split in 1st plural
```
