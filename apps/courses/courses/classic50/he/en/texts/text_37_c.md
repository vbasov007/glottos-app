# Text 37c for Lesson 37: Smikhut + definite adjective
**Theme: bet ha-sefer ha-yesodi, tochnit ha-limudim ha-chadasha — where the adjective "hangs" and what it agrees with**

> Text without vowel points. Thirty sentences with smikhut + adjective. The most frequent move in smikhut at B2: the adjective goes **at the very end** of the chain, repeats the article of the second noun, and agrees in gender/number with the word it describes. The gender of the adjective is an arrow: it points to the noun it attaches to.

> **Main rule:** the adjective goes **at the very end** of the chain, **repeats the article** of the second noun of the smikhut, and **agrees in gender/number** with the word it describes by meaning.

> **Notice №1, 4, 5, 11, 15, 22:** the basic model — the adjective is agreed with the **second** noun of the smikhut: *bet ha-sefer ha-yesodi* (yesodi m. — like sefer), *bet ha-mishpat ha-elyon*, *sheirut ha-bituach ha-le'umi*, *te'udat ha-zehut ha-biometrit*, *kupat ha-cholim ha-gdola*, *ma'arekhet ha-sha'ot ha-chadasha*. This is the "default rule".

> **Notice №3, 6, 7, 19:** the adjective may refer to the **first** noun of the smikhut, and then it agrees with it: *tochnit ha-limudim ha-chadasha* (chadasha f. — like **tochnit**, not like *limudim* m. pl.); *misrad ha-bri'ut ha-yisre'eli* (yisre'eli m. — like **misrad**); *ראש העיר החדש* (chadash m. — like **rosh**, the new mayor, not the new city!); *morat bet ha-sefer ha-chadasha* (chadasha f. — like **morat**).

> **Notice №19 vs №20:** the contrastive pair! *morat bet ha-sefer ha-chadasha* — "the new school teacher (f.)" (f. → mora); *morat bet ha-sefer ha-chadash* — "the (female) teacher of the new school" (m. → sefer). The gender of the adjective unambiguously shows what it refers to. **This works only because *mora* and *sefer* have different genders.** If both were the same gender, the phrase would become ambiguous — and then we rewrite via shel.

> **Main lesson of Text C:** the adjective in smikhut is not a "tail-piece at the end" but **a pointer**. By its gender and number the reader figures out **which noun in the chain** it refers to. Learn to "read the arrow".
