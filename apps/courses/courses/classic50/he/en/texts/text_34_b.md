# Text 34b for Lesson 34: Processes — receipt, submission, preparation
**Theme: Pi'el and Hif'il shem pe'ula of process meaning (kabala, mesirut, hakhana, hagasha, haknasa)**

> Text without nikkud. Thirty sentences about everyday and bureaucratic processes: receiving documents, submitting an application, preparing for an exam, paying a bill, registration. All the "process" nouns are CiCuC and haCCaCa templates.

> **Notice:** in this text almost every sentence **begins** with a shem pe'ula. This is a typical "bureaucratic" order: process — subject — circumstance. Compare with the ordinary colloquial "kibalti et ha-darkon" — here "ha-kabala shel ha-darkon".

> **Notice #5–6, 26–27:** the Hif'il pair haknasa / hotsa'a — literally "bringing in / taking out", in the financial context "income / expense". This is the heart of the everyday business vocabulary.

> **Notice #2:** mesira (delivery of documents) and mesirut (devotion, dedication) — two different words from the same root м-с-р. Mesira is a Pa'al shem pe'ula (a process of delivery), mesirut is an abstract quality with the suffix -ut.

> **Notice #15:** hamlatsa — Hif'il (lehamlits, to recommend), well recognized by haCCaCa.
