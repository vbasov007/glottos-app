# Text 34a for Lesson 34: Academic register — verbal nouns in science and education
**Theme: Pa'al and Pi'el shem pe'ula of the formal style (lemida, kti-va, korelut, sipur, chinukh)**

> Text without nikkud. Thirty sentences in the academic register: study, research, teaching, reading, science. Every other sentence contains at least one shem pe'ula. Listen — catch the CCiCa or CiCuC template.

> **Notice:** the academic register of Hebrew is **built** on shem pe'ula. Where English says "to study, to read, to write", academic Hebrew says "learning, reading, writing" — lemida, kri'a, kti-va. Replace a verb with a noun — and the text automatically sounds more formal.

> **Notice #24–27:** paired shem pe'ula are joined by the conjunction ve-: "lemida ve-kti-va" (learning and writing), "kri'a ve-shmi'a" (reading and listening). This is a typical structure of academic enumeration.

> **Notice #28:** korelut (or the borrowed korelatsia) — a verbal-noun template from the Nif'al variant (lehiyot korelativi), but in modern Hebrew it's used as a ready term. Here — for training the ear on shem pe'ula with "-ut".
