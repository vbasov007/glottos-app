# Text 34c for Lesson 34: Transformations — awakening, development, change
**Theme: Hitpa'el and Nif'al shem pe'ula of inner-change processes (hit'orerut, hitpat'kha-vut, hitragshut, hit'anyenut)**

> Text without nikkud. Thirty sentences about transformations, changes of state, and development — what happens "inside" the subject. All the key nouns are built on the hitCaCCut and hiCaCCut templates. Listen — catch "hit-…-ut" and "hi-…-ut" as the signature.

---

## Listening text and repetition

> **Notice:** Hitpa'el shem pe'ula is about **internal** transformations: awakening, excitement, interest, getting used to. The subject changes itself. The **hit-…-ut** signature is instantly recognizable.

> **Notice #20–26:** Nif'al shem pe'ula (hi-…-ut) — close in meaning to Hitpa'el but "more passive": hikansut — "being brought in / entering", hipagshut — "meeting" (mutual). The **hi-…-ut** signature (no t after h).

> **Notice #19 vs #20:** knisa (the everyday Pa'al form, "entrance" both as a place and as an action) vs hikansut (the formal Nif'al form, "entry" as a process). In live speech you say knisa, in a document — hikansut.

> **Notice #27–30:** paired shem pe'ula of the same template give an academic and bureaucratic rhythm: "hit'orerut ve-hitlabshut", "he'adrut ve-hisha'arut". Remember this rhythm — it's the signal of the formal register.
