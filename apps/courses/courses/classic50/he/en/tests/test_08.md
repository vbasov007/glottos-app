# Test for Lesson 8: Pa'al present tense — the participle paradigm

**How do you say it in Hebrew?**
Translate the phrases into Hebrew (with vowel points). Remember: the present tense is a participle, it agrees in gender and number. Read the parenthetical hints carefully — they set the gender and number of the subject and determine the form (m.s. / f.s. / m.pl. / f.pl.).

---

1. I write. (man)
2. I write. (woman)
3. You write. (to a man)
4. You write. (to a woman)
5. He learns Hebrew.
6. She learns Hebrew.
7. We learn Hebrew. (mixed group)
8. We learn Hebrew. (women only)
9. They learn. (men)
10. They learn. (women)
11. I work. (man)
12. I work. (woman)
13. Yossi works at school. (be-veit ha-sefer)
14. Dana works at school.
15. He's going home. (ha-bayta)
16. She's going home.
17. They're going home. (men)
18. The (female) pupils are going home. (ha-talmidot)
19. I read a book. (man; sefer)
20. The (female) teacher reads a book. (ha-mora)
21. I eat. (woman)
22. We eat pizza. (mixed group; pizza)
23. I drink coffee. (woman; kafe)
24. They drink water. (women; mayim)
25. I live in Tel Aviv. (man; be-Tel Aviv)
26. I live in Jerusalem. (woman; bi-Yrushalayim)
27. He sees a book. (sefer)
28. They (women) know.
29. I don't write. (man)
30. The teacher doesn't work today. (ha-mora; ha-yom)

<summary>Key</summary>

1. *אֲנִי כּוֹתֵב.* (ani kotev) — m.s.
2. *אֲנִי כּוֹתֶבֶת.* (ani kotevet) — f.s.
3. *אַתָּה כּוֹתֵב.* (ata kotev) — m.s.
4. *אַתְּ כּוֹתֶבֶת.* (at kotevet) — f.s.
5. *הוּא לוֹמֵד עִבְרִית.* (hu lomed ivrit) — m.s.
6. *הִיא לוֹמֶדֶת עִבְרִית.* (hi lomedet ivrit) — f.s.
7. *אֲנַחְנוּ לוֹמְדִים עִבְרִית.* (anachnu lomdim ivrit) — mixed group → m.pl.
8. *אֲנַחְנוּ לוֹמְדוֹת עִבְרִית.* (anachnu lomdot ivrit) — women only → f.pl.
9. *הֵם לוֹמְדִים.* (hem lomdim) — m.pl.
10. *הֵן לוֹמְדוֹת.* (hen lomdot) — f.pl.
11. *אֲנִי עוֹבֵד.* (ani oved) — m.s.
12. *אֲנִי עוֹבֶדֶת.* (ani ovedet) — f.s.
13. *יוֹסִי עוֹבֵד בְּבֵית הַסֵּפֶר.* (Yossi oved be-veit ha-sefer) — m.s.
14. *דָּנָה עוֹבֶדֶת בְּבֵית הַסֵּפֶר.* (Dana ovedet be-veit ha-sefer) — f.s.
15. *הוּא הוֹלֵךְ הַבַּיְתָה.* (hu holekh ha-bayta) — m.s.
16. *הִיא הוֹלֶכֶת הַבַּיְתָה.* (hi holekhet ha-bayta) — f.s.
17. *הֵם הוֹלְכִים הַבַּיְתָה.* (hem holkhim ha-bayta) — m.pl.
18. *הַתַּלְמִידוֹת הוֹלְכוֹת הַבַּיְתָה.* (ha-talmidot holkhot ha-bayta) — f.pl.
19. *אֲנִי קוֹרֵא סֵפֶר.* (ani kore sefer) — m.s.
20. *הַמּוֹרָה קוֹרֵאת סֵפֶר.* (ha-mora koret sefer) — f.s.
21. *אֲנִי אוֹכֶלֶת.* (ani okhelet) — f.s.
22. *אֲנַחְנוּ אוֹכְלִים פִּיצָה.* (anachnu okhlim pizza) — mixed → m.pl.
23. *אֲנִי שׁוֹתָה קָפֶה.* (ani shota kafe) — f.s.; lamed-hei → no -et
24. *הֵן שׁוֹתוֹת מַיִם.* (hen shotot mayim) — f.pl.
25. *אֲנִי גָּר בְּתֵל אָבִיב.* (ani gar be-Tel Aviv) — m.s.; weak root ג-ו-ר
26. *אֲנִי גָּרָה בִּירוּשָׁלַיִם.* (ani gara bi-Yrushalayim) — f.s.
27. *הוּא רוֹאֶה סֵפֶר.* (hu ro'e sefer) — m.s.; lamed-hei
28. *הֵן יוֹדְעוֹת.* (hen yod'ot) — f.pl.
29. *אֲנִי לֹא כּוֹתֵב.* (ani lo kotev) — negation: lo + participle
30. *הַמּוֹרָה לֹא עוֹבֶדֶת הַיּוֹם.* (ha-mora lo ovedet ha-yom) — f.s.
