# Test for Lesson 19: Comparison of adjectives. Adverbs and intensifiers

**How do you say it in Hebrew?**
Translate the phrases into Hebrew. Use the grammar and vocabulary from this lesson.

---

1. This house is bigger than that car.
2. Anna is taller than David.
3. The coffee is hotter than the tea.
4. This book is more interesting than the film (interesting = me'anyen).
5. The teachers (f.) are better than the students.
6. I am taller than you (to a man: mimkha).
7. She is younger than me (younger = tsa'ir).
8. The car is smaller than the house (smaller = pachot gadol, or use katan).
9. Tel Aviv is as beautiful as Jerusalem (kmo).
10. This is the best day (literary, beyoter).
11. This is the best day (colloquial, hachi).
12. The most beautiful girl in the class (girl = bachura, in the class = ba-kita).
13. These are the best friends (friend = chaver).
14. The most important thing is family (thing = davar, important = chashuv, family = mishpacha).
15. I am very tired (tired = ayef).
16. This song is so beautiful! (song = shir).
17. The soup is too hot, I can't drink.
18. I'm a bit hungry (hungry = ra'ev).
19. The film is quite interesting.
20. He speaks fast.
21. She walks slowly.
22. We study well.
23. They speak quietly (quietly = be-sheket).
24. I always drink coffee in the morning (in the morning = ba-boker).
25. She sometimes comes to us (to us = elenu).
26. We never speak that way (that way = kakha; never = af pa'am lo).
27. He usually works at home (usually = be-derekh klal, at home = ba-bayit).
28. They haven't eaten yet (not yet = od lo; ate = akhlu).
29. You speak too fast.
30. This restaurant is the most expensive in the city (restaurant = mis'ada, expensive = yakar, in the city = ba-ir; use hachi).

<summary>Keys</summary>

1. *הַבַּיִת הַזֶּה יוֹתֵר גָּדוֹל מֵהַמְּכוֹנִית הַהִיא.* (ha-bayit ha-ze yoter gadol me-ha-mekhonit ha-hi)
2. *אַנָּה יוֹתֵר גְּבוֹהָה מִדָּוִד.* (Anna yoter gvoha mi-David) — mi- + consonant without article = mi-
3. *הַקָּפֶה יוֹתֵר חַם מֵהַתֵּה.* (ha-kafe yoter cham me-ha-te)
4. *הַסֵּפֶר הַזֶּה יוֹתֵר מְעַנְיֵן מֵהַסֶּרֶט.* (ha-sefer ha-ze yoter me'anyen me-ha-seret)
5. *הַמּוֹרוֹת יוֹתֵר טוֹבוֹת מֵהַסְּטוּדֶנְטִים.* (ha-morot yoter tovot me-ha-studentim) — adj. f. pl.
6. *אֲנִי יוֹתֵר גָּבוֹהַּ מִמְּךָ.* (ani yoter gavoah mimkha)
7. *הִיא יוֹתֵר צְעִירָה מִמֶּנִּי.* (hi yoter tse'ira mimeni)
8. *הַמְּכוֹנִית יוֹתֵר קְטַנָּה מֵהַבַּיִת.* (ha-mekhonit yoter ktana me-ha-bayit) — or *pachot gdola*
9. *תֵּל אָבִיב יָפָה כְּמוֹ יְרוּשָׁלַיִם.* (Tel Aviv yafa kmo Yerushalayim)
10. *הַיּוֹם הַטּוֹב בְּיוֹתֵר.* (ha-yom ha-tov beyoter) — literary
11. *הַיּוֹם הֲכִי טוֹב.* (ha-yom ha-hachi tov) — colloquial
12. *הַבַּחוּרָה הֲכִי יָפָה בַּכִּתָּה.* (ha-bachura ha-hachi yafa ba-kita)
13. *הַחֲבֵרִים הֲכִי טוֹבִים.* (ha-chaverim ha-hachi tovim) — or *ha-chaverim ha-tovim beyoter*
14. *הַדָּבָר הֲכִי חָשׁוּב הוּא הַמִּשְׁפָּחָה.* (ha-davar ha-hachi chashuv hu ha-mishpacha)
15. *אֲנִי מְאוֹד עָיֵף.* (ani meod ayef) — neutral "very"
16. *הַשִּׁיר כָּל כָּךְ יָפֶה!* (ha-shir kol kakh yafe!) — emotional exclamation
17. *הַמָּרָק יוֹתֵר מִדַּי חַם, אֲנִי לֹא יָכוֹל לִשְׁתּוֹת.* (ha-marak yoter midai cham, ani lo yakhol lishtot) — excess
18. *אֲנִי קְצָת רָעֵב.* (ani ktzat ra'ev) — weakener
19. *הַסֶּרֶט דַּי מְעַנְיֵן.* (ha-seret dai me'anyen) — "quite"
20. *הוּא מְדַבֵּר מַהֵר.* (hu medaber maher) — adverb of manner AFTER the verb
21. *הִיא הוֹלֶכֶת לְאַט.* (hi holekhet le'at)
22. *אֲנַחְנוּ לוֹמְדִים טוֹב.* (anachnu lomdim tov) — tov here is an adverb, coinciding with adj. m. sg.
23. *הֵם מְדַבְּרִים בְּשֶׁקֶט.* (hem medabrim be-sheket) — be + noun = adverb
24. *אֲנִי תָּמִיד שׁוֹתֶה קָפֶה בַּבֹּקֶר.* (ani tamid shote kafe ba-boker) — tamid BEFORE the verb
25. *הִיא לִפְעָמִים בָּאָה אֵלֵינוּ.* (hi lifamim ba'a elenu)
26. *אֲנַחְנוּ אַף פַּעַם לֹא מְדַבְּרִים כָּכָה.* (anachnu af pa'am lo medabrim kakha) — double negation: af pa'am + lo
27. *הוּא בְּדֶרֶךְ כְּלָל עוֹבֵד בַּבַּיִת.* (hu be-derekh klal oved ba-bayit)
28. *הֵם עוֹד לֹא אָכְלוּ.* (hem od lo akhlu)
29. *אַתָּה מְדַבֵּר יוֹתֵר מִדַּי מַהֵר.* (ata medaber yoter midai maher)
30. *הַמִּסְעָדָה הַזֹּאת הֲכִי יְקָרָה בָּעִיר.* (ha-mis'ada ha-zot ha-hachi yekara ba-ir)

**Patterns:**
- **mi- + ha-** → **me-ha-** (with tsere): *me-ha-bayit, me-ha-te, me-ha-mekhonit* (#1, 3, 4, 5, 8).
- **mi- + consonant without article** → **mi-**: *mi-David* (#2). Before pronouns — inflected forms: *mimeni, mimkha* (#6, 7).
- **beyoter** — literary (#10), **hachi** — colloquial (#11–14). Understand both.
- **meod** (neutral "very", #15) ≠ **kol kakh** (emotional "so", #16) ≠ **yoter midai** ("too", excess, #17, 29).
- Adverb of **manner** (maher, le'at, tov, be-sheket) — **after** the verb (#20–23).
- Adverb of **frequency** (tamid, lifamim, af pa'am lo, be-derekh klal, od lo) — **before** the verb (#24–28).
- "Never not" = **af pa'am lo** or **le-olam lo** — obligatorily with **lo** (#26).
