# Test for Lesson 31: Complement clauses with ש- and כי. Reported speech

**How do you say it in Hebrew?**
Translate the phrases into Hebrew. Use the grammar and vocabulary from this lesson.

Use ש- (she-) or כי (ki) for "that"; remember: no comma is placed before them, and there's no backward shift of tenses. In reported questions — a question word or אם (im) for "whether". Verbs of speaking, thinking, knowing — and what kind of subordinate clause each one pulls.

---

1. I think that he is a student.
2. She knows that we are at home.
3. They said that the lesson starts at eight.
4. I understand that this is hard.
5. We hope that the weather will be good.
6. I said that I was tired.
7. He explained that there's no time.
8. She announced that the office is closed.
9. I want you to come (to a man).
10. She wants us to help.
11. They asked me to explain.
12. I thought that he was working (now, at that moment).
13. I thought that he had worked (earlier).
14. I thought that he would work (later).
15. I know that she came.
16. I know that she will come.
17. Dana said that she was tired. (reported speech)
18. Yossi asked where the book is. (reported question)
19. The teacher said that the lesson starts tomorrow.
20. The child asked whether you want to play. (yes/no question → im)
21. I didn't come because I was sick. (ki)
22. He was silent because he didn't know what to say. (ki)
23. I know that this isn't easy. (ki = "that")
24. He explained that he doesn't know. (ki = "that")
25. The lesson was cancelled because the teacher got sick. (biglal she-)
26. She is tired because she worked all day. (mipnei she-)
27. I believe that you are right (to a man).
28. I hope that you will call (to a woman).
29. I forgot that today is a holiday.
30. They thought that we wouldn't come.

<summary>Key</summary>

1. *אני חושב שהוא סטודנט* — **ani choshev she-hu student**
2. *היא יודעת שאנחנו בבית* — **hi yoda'at she-anachnu ba-bayit**
3. *הם אמרו שהשיעור מתחיל בשמונה* — **hem amru she-ha-shi'ur matchil bi-shmone** (subordinate in present although main is in past)
4. *אני מבין שזה קשה* — **ani mevin she-ze kashe**
5. *אנחנו מקווים שמזג האוויר יהיה טוב* — **anachnu mekavim she-mezeg ha-avir yihye tov** (forecast → future in the subordinate)
6. *אמרתי שאני עייף* — **amarti she-ani ayef** (main in the past, subordinate in present: Hebrew doesn't shift back)
7. *הוא הסביר שאין זמן* — **hu hisbir she-ein zman** (or: ki ein zman)
8. *היא הודיעה שהמשרד סגור* — **hi hodi'a she-ha-misrad sagur**
9. *אני רוצה שתבוא* — **ani rotse she-tavo** ("so that / to" → she- + future)
10. *היא רוצה שנעזור* — **hi rotsa she-na'azor**
11. *הם ביקשו שאסביר* — **hem bikshu she-asbir**
12. *חשבתי שהוא עובד* — **chashavti she-hu oved** (simultaneous → present)
13. *חשבתי שהוא עבד* — **chashavti she-hu avad** (earlier → past)
14. *חשבתי שהוא יעבוד* — **chashavti she-hu ya'avod** (later → future)
15. *אני יודע שהיא באה* — **ani yodea she-hi ba'a** (past f.sg. = present f.sg.; context distinguishes)
16. *אני יודע שהיא תבוא* — **ani yodea she-hi tavo**
17. *דנה אמרה שהיא עייפה* — **Dana amra she-hi ayefa** (pronoun "I" → "hi", since we speak about Dana in third person)
18. *יוסי שאל איפה הספר* — **Yossi sha'al eifo ha-sefer** (reported question — **no** she-, with a question word)
19. *המורה אמר שהשיעור מתחיל מחר* — **Ha-more amar she-ha-shi'ur matchil machar**
20. *הילד שאל אם אתה רוצה לשחק* — **Ha-yeled sha'al im ata rotse lesachek** (yes/no question → im "whether")
21. *לא באתי כי הייתי חולה* — **lo bati ki hayiti chole**
22. *הוא שתק כי לא ידע מה לומר* — **hu shatak ki lo yada ma lomar**
23. *אני יודע כי זה לא קל* — **ani yodea ki ze lo kal** (after a verb of knowing ki = "that")
24. *הוא הסביר כי הוא לא יודע* — **hu hisbir ki hu lo yodea**
25. *השיעור בוטל בגלל שהמורה חלה* — **ha-shi'ur butal biglal she-ha-more chala**
26. *היא עייפה מפני שעבדה כל היום* — **hi ayefa mipnei she-avda kol ha-yom**
27. *אני מאמין שאתה צודק* — **ani ma'amin she-ata tsodek**
28. *אני מקווה שתתקשרי* — **ani mekave she-titkashri** (to a woman)
29. *שכחתי שהיום חג* — **shakhachti she-ha-yom chag**
30. *הם חשבו שלא נבוא* — **hem chashvu she-lo navo** (subordinate in the future, because "won't come" is after the moment of their thought)
