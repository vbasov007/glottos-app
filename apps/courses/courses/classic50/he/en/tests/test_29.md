# Test for Lesson 29: The relative prefix she-. Resumptive pronouns

**How do you say it in Hebrew?**
Translate the phrases into Hebrew. Use the grammar and vocabulary from this lesson. Hebrew without vowel points. Every English "who / which / that / whom / whose" becomes a single particle **ש-** (she-) in Hebrew. If the relative stands in an oblique case with a preposition — add a **resumptive pronoun** inside the clause (preposition + suffix by the gender of the Hebrew noun).

---

1. The man who lives here is a teacher.
2. The woman who works in the office is my friend.
3. The children who play in the park are small.
4. The book that lies on the table is new.
5. The students who study well succeed.
6. The teacher who came yesterday is very famous.
7. The young woman who is singing is my sister.
8. The letter I wrote is long.
9. The film we saw was interesting.
10. The song she's listening to is beautiful.
11. The house I live in is big.
12. The city we were born in is small.
13. The country he came to is far away.
14. The apartment they live in is new.
15. The room I sleep in is quiet.
16. The woman I'm talking to is my mother.
17. The friend I work with is an engineer.
18. The teacher I study with is wise.
19. The child I'm helping is small.
20. The girl I gave a gift to is my daughter.
21. The book they talk about in the news is new.
22. The topic you're thinking about is important.
23. The war they wrote about in the newspaper is far away.
24. The country my parents came from is small.
25. The car we rode in is old.
26. The place I live is beautiful.
27. Everything I know, I know from him.
28. Whoever wants to study will come here.
29. Everyone who works gets money.
30. The friends I call every day live in Tel Aviv.

1. *האיש שגר פה הוא מורה.* (ha-ish she-gar po hu more)
2. *האישה שעובדת במשרד היא החברה שלי.* (ha-isha she-ovedet ba-misrad hi ha-chavera sheli)
3. *הילדים שמשחקים בפארק קטנים.* (ha-yeladim she-mesachakim ba-park ktanim)
4. *הספר שנמצא על השולחן חדש.* (ha-sefer she-nimtsa al ha-shulchan chadash)
5. *התלמידים שלומדים טוב מצליחים.* (ha-talmidim she-lomdim tov matzlichim)
6. *המורה שבא אתמול מפורסם מאוד.* (ha-more she-ba etmol mefursam me'od)
7. *הבחורה ששרה היא האחות שלי.* (ha-bachura she-shara hi ha-achot sheli)
8. *המכתב שכתבתי ארוך.* (ha-mikhtav she-katavti arokh) — direct-object resumptive dropped (conversation).
9. *הסרט שראינו היה מעניין.* (ha-seret she-ra'inu haya me'anyen)
10. *השיר שהיא שומעת יפה.* (ha-shir she-hi shoma'at yafe)
11. *הבית שאני גר בו גדול.* (ha-bayit she-ani gar **bo** gadol) — bo, since bayit m.
12. *העיר שנולדנו בה קטנה.* (ha-ir she-noladnu **ba** ktana) — ba, since ir f.
13. *הארץ שהוא הגיע אליה רחוקה.* (ha-eretz she-hu higia **eleha** rechoka) — eleha (to it), eretz f.
14. *הדירה שהם גרים בה חדשה.* (ha-dira she-hem garim **ba** chadasha) — dira f.
15. *החדר שאני ישן בו שקט.* (ha-cheder she-ani yashen **bo** shaket) — cheder m.
16. *האישה שאני מדבר איתה היא אמא שלי.* (ha-isha she-ani medaber **ita** hi ima sheli) — ita (with her).
17. *החבר שאני עובד איתו מהנדס.* (ha-chaver she-ani oved **ito** mehandes) — ito (with him).
18. *המורה שאני לומד אצלו חכם.* (ha-more she-ani lomed **etzlo** chakham) — etzlo (at his place).
19. *הילד שאני עוזר לו קטן.* (ha-yeled she-ani ozer **lo** katan) — lo (to him).
20. *הילדה שנתתי לה מתנה היא הבת שלי.* (ha-yalda she-natati **la** matana hi ha-bat sheli) — la (to her).
21. *הספר שמדברים עליו בחדשות חדש.* (ha-sefer she-medabrim **alav** ba-chadashot chadash) — alav, since sefer m.
22. *הנושא שאתה חושב עליו חשוב.* (ha-nose she-ata choshev **alav** chashuv) — alav, since nose m.
23. *המלחמה שכתבו עליה בעיתון רחוקה.* (ha-milchama she-katvu **aleha** ba-iton rechoka) — aleha, since milchama f.
24. *הארץ שההורים שלי באו ממנה קטנה.* (ha-eretz she-ha-horim sheli ba'u **mimena** ktana) — mimena (from it).
25. *המכונית שנסענו בה ישנה.* (ha-mechonit she-nasanu **ba** yeshana) — ba, since mechonit f.; Hebrew says "in the car".
26. *המקום שאני גר בו יפה.* (ha-makom she-ani gar **bo** yafe) — bo, since makom m.
27. *כל מה שאני יודע אני יודע ממנו.* (kol ma she-ani yodea ani yodea mi-menu)
28. *מי שרוצה ללמוד יבוא הנה.* (mi she-rotse lilmod yavo hena)
29. *כל מי שעובד מקבל כסף.* (kol mi she-oved mekabel kesef)
30. *החברים שאני מתקשר אליהם כל יום גרים בתל אביב.* (ha-chaverim she-ani mitkasher **elehem** kol yom garim b-Tel Aviv) — elehem (to them).

**Main takeaway from the test:** English "who / which / that / whom / whose / who … to / which … in / with whom / about whom" are **one** Hebrew she-. If the case in English is oblique (with a preposition), Hebrew **requires** a resumptive pronoun by the gender of the Hebrew antecedent: **bo, ba, ito, ita, alav, aleha, lo, la, etzlo, mimena, elehem**.
