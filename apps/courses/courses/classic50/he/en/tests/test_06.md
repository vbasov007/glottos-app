# Test for Lesson 6: The root (שׁוֹרֶשׁ) — the atom of Hebrew. Word families

**How do you say it in Hebrew?**
Translate the phrases into Hebrew. Use the grammar and vocabulary from this lesson. Main task: see the root through the prefixes, suffixes and vowels.

---

1. Root (as a term).
2. Name the root of the word כּוֹתֵב.
3. Name the root of the word מִכְתָּב.
4. Name the root of the word כְּתוֹבֶת.
5. What's the meaning of the root כ-ת-ב?
6. Name the root of the word תַּלְמִיד.
7. Name the root of the word לוֹמֵד.
8. What's the meaning of the root ל-מ-ד?
9. Name the root of the word שׁוֹמֵר.
10. Name the root of the word מִשְׁמָר.
11. What's the meaning of the root ש-מ-ר?
12. Name the root of the word מְדַבֵּר.
13. Name the root of the word מִדְבָּר.
14. What's the meaning of the root ד-ב-ר?
15. Name the root of the word אוֹכֵל.
16. What's the meaning of the root א-כ-ל?
17. He wrote a letter.
18. She writes the address.
19. The student studies.
20. The (female) teacher teaches.
21. The watchman watches.
22. He speaks, she speaks.
23. I eat food.
24. This dish is tasty (good).
25. These words share the same root.
26. The word כִּתָּה (classroom) is NOT from the root כ-ת-ב. What's its root?
27. The word מִסְעָדָה (restaurant) is NOT from the root א-כ-ל. What's its root?
28. In the word נִשְׁמָר the root is hidden under a prefix. What is it?
29. Name three words from the כ-ת-ב bush.
30. Name three words from the ש-מ-ר bush.

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *שׁוֹרֶשׁ* (shoresh)
2. *כ-ת-ב* (k-t-v) — writing / he writes.
3. *כ-ת-ב* (k-t-v) — prefix *mi-*, root in place.
4. *כ-ת-ב* (k-t-v) — suffix *-et* (CCoCet pattern), root in place.
5. "Write" (everything to do with writing).
6. *ל-מ-ד* (l-m-d) — prefix *ta-*, root l-m-d.
7. *ל-מ-ד* (l-m-d) — Pa'al participle pattern (o-e).
8. "Learn / teach".
9. *ש-מ-ר* (sh-m-r) — Pa'al participle pattern.
10. *ש-מ-ר* (sh-m-r) — prefix *mi-*, root sh-m-r.
11. "Guard / watch / preserve".
12. *ד-ב-ר* (d-b-r) — prefix *me-* and doubled middle letter (Pi'el).
13. *ד-ב-ר* (d-b-r) — yes, desert shares a root with "speak"!
14. "Speak / word / matter".
15. *א-כ-ל* (')-k-l — Pa'al participle, vowels o-e.
16. "Eat / food".
17. *הוּא כָּתַב מִכְתָּב.* (hu katav mikhtav)
18. *הִיא כּוֹתֶבֶת כְּתוֹבֶת.* (hi kotevet ktovet) — two words from the same bush!
19. *הַתַּלְמִיד לוֹמֵד.* (ha-talmid lomed) — both words from the l-m-d bush.
20. *הַמּוֹרָה מְלַמֶּדֶת.* (ha-mora melamedet) — Pi'el from l-m-d.
21. *הַשּׁוֹמֵר שׁוֹמֵר.* (ha-shomer shomer) — "the watchman watches", classic of the sh-m-r bush.
22. *הוּא מְדַבֵּר, הִיא מְדַבֶּרֶת.* (hu medaber, hi medaberet)
23. *אֲנִי אוֹכֵל אֹכֶל.* (ani okhel okhel) — "I eat food", the same word twice in the א-כ-ל bush.
24. *הַמַּאֲכָל הַזֶּה טוֹב.* (ha-ma'akhal ha-ze tov) — *ma'akhal* — dish, root '-k-l.
25. *לַמִּלִּים הָאֵלֶּה אוֹתוֹ שׁוֹרֶשׁ.* (la-milim ha-ele oto shoresh)
26. *כ-ת-ת* (k-t-t, "to split, divide") — a different root: the third letter is tav, not vet.
27. *ס-ע-ד* (s-'-d, "to support, sustain") — thematically about food, but a completely different root.
28. *ש-מ-ר* (sh-m-r) — prefix *ni-* (Nif'al, passive), same root.
29. Examples: *כָּתַב, כּוֹתֵב, מִכְתָּב, כְּתוֹבֶת, כְּתָב, כָּתוּב, כַּתָּב, כְּתִיבָה* (katav, kotev, mikhtav, ktovet, ktav, katuv, katav, ktiva).
30. Examples: *שָׁמַר, שׁוֹמֵר, שׁוֹמֶרֶת, מִשְׁמָר, שְׁמִירָה, נִשְׁמָר* (shamar, shomer, shomeret, mishmar, shmira, nishmar).
