# Test for Lesson 46: Stylistics and precision in writing

**How do you say it in Hebrew?**
Translate the phrases into Hebrew. Use the grammar and vocabulary from this lesson. Note: the choice of binyan, register, smikhut vs. shel, and synonym shade matters. Many sentences have several grammatically correct variants — pick the one that fits the **specified register**.

---

1. The teacher's book is on the table. (colloquial register)
2. The teacher's book is on the table. (written register, dense)
3. The man whom I saw yesterday was a teacher. (colloquial)
4. The man whom I saw yesterday was a teacher. (literary)
5. The door opened. (neutral, middle voice)
6. They opened the door. (colloquial, impersonal "they")
7. The door was opened by the director. (formal, bureaucratic)
8. The prime minister declared that the country would not sign the agreement. (public declaration)
9. The committee carried out the inspection. (formal action, operation)
10. The poet created a new poem. (creatively)
11. After Dani finished the work, he handed the report to the director. (formal register)
12. We think it's a good idea. (colloquial)
13. We hold that it's a successful idea. (formal)
14. But we didn't have the means. (colloquial)
15. However, we didn't have the means. (written)
16. In view of the fact that the meeting ended, the participants dispersed. (legal)
17. The children's book is in the classroom. (unambiguous, no sefer/safar ambiguity)
18. He wrote the book. (with a pronoun to remove the ambiguity with the imperative "write!")
19. Great wisdom is an important value. (remove the chokhma/chakhama ambiguity)
20. My sister's birthday. (smikhut, fixed expression)
21. Likewise, the participants received gifts. (formal: "in like manner")
22. Yesterday Yossi arrived. (VSO after an adverbial, literary)
23. It's precisely this book that I read. (focus by fronting — "this book")
24. The neighbor's children who live in the synagogue played in the street. (dense: smikhut + participle)
25. Upon completion of writing the book, Dani handed it to his teacher. (verbal noun instead of subordinate)
26. So that he could come, we sent him the butcher. (infinitive instead of "kedei she-")
27. The director's meeting that took place yesterday was long. (smikhut + participle)
28. Since it was raining, we stayed home. (formal: mishum she-)
29. He noted that the results were excellent. (formal: tziyen)
30. In addition to this, we added a note. (formal: be-nosaf)

<summary>Key</summary>

1. *הספר של המורה מונח על השולחן.* (ha-sefer shel ha-mora munach al ha-shulchan) — shel, colloquial.
2. *ספר המורה מונח על השולחן.* (sefer ha-mora munach al ha-shulchan) — smikhut, dense/written.
3. *האיש שראיתי אתמול היה מורה.* (ha-ish she-ra'iti etmol haya more) — she-, colloquial.
4. *האיש אשר ראיתי אתמול היה מורה.* (ha-ish asher ra'iti etmol haya more) — asher, literary.
5. *הדלת נפתחה.* (ha-delet niftecha) — Nif'al, middle voice.
6. *פתחו את הדלת.* (patchu et ha-delet) — 3rd pl. Pa'al, colloquial.
7. *הדלת נפתחה על ידי המנהל.* (ha-delet niftecha al yedei ha-menahel) — formal passive.
8. *ראש הממשלה הצהיר כי המדינה לא תחתום על ההסכם.* (rosh ha-memshala hitzhir ki ha-medina lo tachtom al ha-heskem) — *hitzhir* "declared publicly".
9. *הוועדה ביצעה את הבדיקה.* (ha-va'ada bitze'a et ha-bdika) — *bitze'a* formal "carried out an operation".
10. *המשורר יצר שיר חדש.* (ha-meshorer yatzar shir chadash) — *yatzar* for creative "created".
11. *לאחר סיום העבודה, מסר דני את הדוח למנהלו.* (le-achar siyum ha-avoda, masar Dani et ha-doch le-menahalo) — verbal noun + VSO + suffix.
12. *אנחנו חושבים שזה רעיון טוב.* (anachnu choshvim she-ze ra'ayon tov) — *choshvim* colloquial.
13. *אנו סוברים שזהו רעיון מוצלח.* (anu sovrim she-zehu ra'ayon mutzlach) — *anu/sovrim/zehu/mutzlach* formal.
14. *אבל לא היו לנו אמצעים.* (aval lo hayu lanu emtza'im) — *aval* colloquial.
15. *אך לא היו לנו אמצעים.* (akh lo hayu lanu emtza'im) — *akh* written.
16. *היות והישיבה הסתיימה, המשתתפים התפזרו.* (heyot ve-ha-yeshiva histayma, ha-mishtatfim hitpazru) — *heyot ve-* legal.
17. *הספר לילדים נמצא בכיתה.* (ha-sefer la-yeladim nimtza ba-kita) — *shel + le-* removes the *safar yeladim* reading.
18. *הוא כתב את הספר.* (hu katav et ha-sefer) — adding *hu* marks past, not imperative.
19. *חכמה גדולה היא ערך חשוב.* (chokhma gdola hi erekh chashuv) — *hi erekh* removes the "wise woman" reading.
20. *יום-ההולדת של אחותי.* (yom-ha-huledet shel achoti) — fixed smikhut + shel for the owner.
21. *כמו כן, המשתתפים קיבלו מתנות.* (kemo khen, ha-mishtatfim kiblu matanot) — *kemo khen* formal.
22. *אתמול הגיע יוסי.* (etmol higi'a Yossi) — VSO after an adverbial.
23. *את הספר הזה קראתי.* (et ha-sefer ha-ze karati) — fronting the object = focus.
24. *ילדי השכן הגר בבית הכנסת שיחקו ברחוב.* (yaldei ha-shakhen ha-gar be-veit ha-knesset sichaku ba-rechov) — smikhut *yaldei ha-shakhen* + participle *ha-gar*.
25. *לאחר כתיבת הספר, מסר דני אותו למורתו.* (le-achar ktivat ha-sefer, masar Dani oto le-morato) — *ktivat* verbal noun + smikhut + suffix.
26. *כדי לבוא, הוא שלח לנו את הקצב.* (kedei lavo, hu shalach lanu et ha-katsav) — infinitive *lavo* instead of *kedei she-yavo*.
27. *ישיבת המנהל שהתקיימה אתמול הייתה ארוכה.* (yeshivat ha-menahel she-hitkayma etmol hayta aruka) — smikhut *yeshivat ha-menahel* + participle/past.
28. *משום שירד גשם, נשארנו בבית.* (mishum she-yarad geshem, nish'arnu ba-bayit) — *mishum she-* formal.
29. *הוא ציין שהתוצאות היו מצוינות.* (hu tziyen she-ha-totza'ot hayu metzuyanot) — *tziyen* formal "noted".
30. *בנוסף לכך, הוספנו הערה.* (be-nosaf le-khakh, hosafnu he'ara) — *be-nosaf* formal.

**Core principle:** in every pair (colloquial/formal), **both** forms are grammatically correct. The test checks **register choice**, not "a rule". The error isn't in grammar — it's a stylistic miss.
