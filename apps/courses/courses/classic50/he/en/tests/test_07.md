# Test for Lesson 7: The scaffold of seven binyanim — the map of verb patterns

**Identify the binyan, voice and valence.**
This test isn't about conjugation (you haven't met any yet), but about the **recognition reflex**: see a form → name the binyan → understand the voice (active/passive/reflexive). Verb forms are given in the past, 3rd m.s. ("he …ed"). Write the Hebrew with vowel points (nikkud). Use the vocabulary from lesson 7 (mostly the roots ש-מ-ר, ל-ב-שׁ, כ-ת-ב, ש-ב-ר + the terminology).

---

1. Root (3 consonants).
2. Binyan (verb pattern).
3. Mishkal (noun pattern).
4. Shoresh in Hebrew with vowel points.
5. Binyan in Hebrew with vowel points.
6. Which binyan is the form *shamar* (he guarded) in?
7. Which binyan is the form *nishmar* (he was guarded) in?
8. Which binyan is the form *shimmer* (he carefully preserved) in?
9. Which binyan is the form *hishmir* (he brought about guarding) in?
10. Which binyan is the form *hishtammer* (he survived) in?
11. The old name of binyan Pa'al (with vowel points).
12. Write the form "he guarded" (Pa'al of ש-מ-ר) with vowel points.
13. Write the form "he was guarded" (Nif'al of ש-מ-ר) with vowel points.
14. Write the form "he preserved (long-term)" (Pi'el of ש-מ-ר) with vowel points.
15. Write the form "he survived / preserved himself" (Hitpa'el of ש-מ-ר) with vowel points.
16. Which binyan is the form *katav* (he wrote) in?
17. Which binyan is the form *nikhtav* (he was written) in?
18. Which binyan is the form *hikhtiv* (he dictated) in?
19. Which binyan is the form *hitkattev* (he corresponded) in?
20. Which binyan is the form *hukhtav* (he was dictated) in?
21. Which binyan is the form *lavash* (he put on himself) in?
22. Which binyan is the form *hilbish* (he dressed someone) in?
23. Which binyan is the form *hitlabbesh* (he got dressed) in?
24. Active or passive voice: *shummar*?
25. Active or passive voice: *hishmir*?
26. Which binyan is the passive partner of Pi'el?
27. Which binyan is the passive partner of Hif'il?
28. Which binyan is the passive partner of Pa'al?
29. Which binyan has no passive partner?
30. Write the form "he broke" (Pa'al of ש-ב-ר) with vowel points, then the form "he was broken" (Nif'al of the same root).

<details>
<summary>Key</summary>

1. *שׁוֹרֶשׁ* (shoresh) — root.
2. *בִּנְיָן* (binyan), pl. *binyanim*.
3. *מִשְׁקָל* (mishkal) — noun pattern (for nouns and adjectives).
4. *שׁוֹרֶשׁ*
5. *בִּנְיָן*
6. **Pa'al** (= Qal): bare root, "a-a" vowels.
7. **Nif'al**: prefix *ni-* (נִ-).
8. **Pi'el**: doubled middle letter (dagesh), "i-e" vowels.
9. **Hif'il**: prefix *hi-* (הִ-) + long "i" in the second syllable.
10. **Hitpa'el**: prefix *hit-* (הִתְ-) + doubling; here metathesis: *hit* + *sh* → *hisht*.
11. *קַל* (Qal) — "light".
12. *שָׁמַר* (shamar) — Pa'al.
13. *נִשְׁמַר* (nishmar) — Nif'al.
14. *שִׁמֵּר* (shimmer) — Pi'el (dagesh in מ).
15. *הִשְׁתַּמֵּר* (hishtammer) — Hitpa'el, metathesis *hit + sh → hisht*.
16. **Pa'al** (*katav*, bare root).
17. **Nif'al** (*nikhtav*, prefix *ni-*).
18. **Hif'il** (*hikhtiv*, *hi-* + long "i"). Causative: "brought about writing".
19. **Hitpa'el** (*hitkattev*, *hit-* + doubling). Reciprocal: "wrote to each other".
20. **Huf'al** (*hukhtav*, *hu-* + "a"). Passive to Hif'il.
21. **Pa'al** (*lavash*) — the subject puts on (himself).
22. **Hif'il** (*hilbish*) — causative: "dressed someone else".
23. **Hitpa'el** (*hitlabbesh*) — reflexive: "got dressed himself".
24. **Passive**. *Shummar* — Pu'al, passive to Pi'el (*shimmer*).
25. **Active, causative**. *Hishmir* — Hif'il.
26. **Pu'al** (internal passive: "i-e" → "u-a").
27. **Huf'al** (internal passive: *hi-* → *hu-*).
28. **Nif'al** (external passive via the prefix *ni-*).
29. **Hitpa'el** — the reflexive is self-sufficient, no separate passive partner.
30. *שָׁבַר* (shavar) — Pa'al; *נִשְׁבַּר* (nishbar) — Nif'al, "was broken".
